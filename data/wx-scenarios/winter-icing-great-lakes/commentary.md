**Observation:** KORD's METAR carries the cP post-frontal wind: 320/20 with gusts driven by the post-frontal pressure rise.

**Reason:** Two sources keep the gusts active even after sunset. (1) L-departing-east (1004mb) is deepening; isobars pack tighter behind a deepening trough, so the geostrophic wind speeds up as the cold-sector pressure rise meets the low's pressure fall. The "gusts die at sunset" heuristic applies in stable air; in active cold advection behind the F-trailing-cold front it does not.
