# TSO

A **TSO (Technical Standard Order)** is a minimum performance standard for an article -- a piece of equipment such as a GPS receiver, an anti-collision light, a seatbelt, or an emergency locator transmitter. A TSO defines what that article must do and how well it must do it. When a manufacturer builds equipment to a TSO and the FAA grants **TSO authorization**, the article is marked as TSO-approved.

## Binding or advisory

A TSO is best read as advisory in form but consequential in effect. The TSO document itself is a standard, not a rule aimed at pilots. But TSO authorization is issued under **14 CFR Part 21 Subpart O**, and many operational rules require equipment that meets a specific TSO. So a TSO is not something a pilot complies with directly; it is a building block the regulations and the aircraft's approved equipment list rely on.

## How it is identified

A TSO identifier reads `TSO-C129a`:

- `TSO` -- the prefix.
- `C129` -- the article number (here, airborne supplemental navigation equipment using GPS).
- `a` -- the revision letter.

A given article meets a specific TSO at a specific revision.

## Revisions and currency

TSOs are revised as technology and minimum standards advance, each revision adding a letter. Equipment built to an older TSO revision generally remains approved, but a new installation may be required to meet the current revision. The aircraft's records show which TSO each installed article was approved against.

## Where to find it

`drs.faa.gov` indexes TSOs; `faa.gov/aircraft/air_cert/design_approvals/tso` lists them.

## How it relates to the others

A TSO is an article-level standard. **14 CFR** ties it into operations: a rule that says "the aircraft must be equipped with X" usually points at a TSO that defines X. An **Airworthiness Directive** can be issued against a TSO-approved article when a safety defect is found in it. So the chain runs TSO standard, then approved article, then regulatory requirement, then -- if a problem surfaces -- an AD.

References: **14 CFR Part 21 Subpart O** (TSO authorizations); the TSO index at `drs.faa.gov`.
