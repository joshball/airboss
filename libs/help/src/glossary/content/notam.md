# NOTAM

A **NOTAM (Notice to Air Missions)** is a time-critical notice of a change to the National Airspace System. It is how the system tells you about a condition that the charts and the Chart Supplement cannot, because the condition arose after they were printed: a closed runway, an out-of-service navaid, a temporary flight restriction, an obstruction, a changed procedure.

The term was historically "Notice to Airmen"; it is now "Notice to Air Missions."

## Binding or advisory

Binding. A NOTAM is operative information you are required to account for. **14 CFR 91.103** requires a pilot, before flight, to become familiar with all available information concerning that flight -- and NOTAMs are squarely part of that information. A Temporary Flight Restriction issued by NOTAM is legally enforceable airspace. Flying into a NOTAM'd condition you should have known about is both a safety failure and a regulatory one.

## How it is identified

A NOTAM is identified by an issuing airport or facility identifier and a NOTAM number, for example `!JFK 09/123`. NOTAMs are grouped by type -- aerodrome, obstruction, navaid, airspace, FDC (Flight Data Center, which carries regulatory items such as TFRs and amended instrument procedures) -- and each carries an effective time window.

## Revisions and currency

NOTAMs are inherently time-bounded: each has a start time and either an end time or an estimated duration. They are issued and cancelled continuously. A pre-flight NOTAM check is only valid for the moment it was run; for a long planning horizon, recheck before departure.

## Where to find it

The FAA NOTAM Search at `notams.aim.faa.gov`. NOTAMs are also delivered through a standard weather briefing (Leidos Flight Service) and through every EFB.

## How it relates to the others

A NOTAM is the real-time layer over the static publications. The **Chart Supplement** gives the durable airport and facility data; a NOTAM overrides it when something changes between 56-day cycles. The **AIM** explains the NOTAM system itself. Because a NOTAM can override published information, it is checked last and closest to departure.

References: **14 CFR 91.103** (preflight action); the **AIM** chapter on the NOTAM system; the FAA NOTAM Search at `notams.aim.faa.gov`.
