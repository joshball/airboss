# ForeFlight

**ForeFlight Mobile** is an electronic flight bag (EFB) application -- the most widely used in US general aviation. It aggregates charts, airport data, flight planning, weather, and in-flight moving-map navigation into one iPad/iPhone app.

In a **weather-briefing** context, ForeFlight is a flight-planning aggregator: it pulls METARs, TAFs, AIRMETs, SIGMETs, winds aloft, and graphical products from the same FAA/NWS sources a Flight Service briefing draws on, and overlays them on the route. A briefing assembled in ForeFlight and logged through its Leidos integration counts as an official weather briefing for the purpose of preflight-action requirements.

Note the distinction from **in-flight** weather display. The planning-side ForeFlight briefing uses ground-sourced data on a connected device; the in-flight datalink weather a pilot sees through a connected ADS-B receiver (or SiriusXM) is a separate product with its own latency and coverage characteristics. The [flight-deck weather displays](/reference/knowledge/wx-flight-deck-weather-displays) node covers the in-flight side; this entry is the planning-side tool.

ForeFlight is one of several EFB / planning applications -- see also [Garmin Pilot](/reference/glossary/garmin-pilot) and [SkyVector](/reference/glossary/skyvector). The choice of app does not change the underlying data; all of them resolve to the same FAA/NWS source feeds.

References: **AC 91-78** (Use of Electronic Flight Bags); **AC 120-76** (Authorization for Use of EFBs) for the broader EFB framework.
