# Chart Supplement

The **Chart Supplement** is the FAA's directory of airport and facility data. For every public-use airport in a region it gives the runway dimensions and surfaces, the lighting, the radio frequencies, the services, the traffic pattern notes, the airport diagram, and the surrounding navaids and communications facilities. It is the reference a pilot opens to learn the practical detail of an airport that a sectional chart has no room to print.

It was formerly called the **Airport/Facility Directory (A/FD)**. The content and purpose are the same; only the name changed.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory. The Chart Supplement is a data reference, not a regulation. It carries no legal force of its own. The information in it, though, feeds decisions that *are* regulated: **14 CFR 91.103** requires a pilot to know runway lengths and takeoff and landing distance data for the intended flight, and the Chart Supplement is a primary source for the runway data behind that.

## How it is identified

The Chart Supplement is published as a set of regional volumes covering the United States. An airport entry is found by the airport name or identifier within its regional volume; it is not numbered like an AC or cited by paragraph like the AIM.

## Revisions and currency

The Chart Supplement is reissued on a **56-day cycle** -- the standard FAA aeronautical chart cycle. Each new edition supersedes the prior one entirely. Always use the current edition: airport data goes stale, and a change that lands mid-cycle appears as a NOTAM until the next edition absorbs it.

## Where to find it

The digital Chart Supplement is free at `faa.gov/air_traffic/flight_info/aeronav`. Every EFB integrates it, and printed regional volumes are sold for the 56-day cycle.

## How it relates to the others

The Chart Supplement is the static airport reference; a **NOTAM** is the dynamic override that carries any change occurring between 56-day editions. The two are read together: the Chart Supplement for the durable picture, the NOTAMs for what has changed since. The **AIM** explains the procedures the airport data supports.

References: the **Chart Supplement** at `faa.gov/air_traffic/flight_info/aeronav`; **14 CFR 91.103** for the preflight data it supports.
