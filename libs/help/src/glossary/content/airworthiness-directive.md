# AD

An **AD (Airworthiness Directive)** is a binding, mandatory corrective action. When the FAA finds an unsafe condition in a specific aircraft, engine, propeller, or appliance -- and the condition is likely to exist in other articles of the same type -- it issues an AD that **requires** the fix.

## Binding or advisory

Binding, fully. An AD is regulation: it is issued under **14 CFR Part 39**, and Part 39 makes compliance mandatory. An aircraft with an overdue AD is **not airworthy** and may not legally fly until the AD is complied with. This is the opposite end of the spectrum from an Advisory Circular: an AC shows an optional acceptable means, an AD commands a required action.

## How it is identified

An AD number reads `2023-14-05`:

- `2023` -- the year of issue.
- `14` -- the biweekly issue number within the year.
- `05` -- the sequence number within that issue.

The AD itself specifies the **applicability** (which make, model, and serial numbers it hits), the **required action**, and the **compliance time** (a flight-hour limit, a calendar deadline, or "before further flight"). An **Emergency AD** is issued and effective immediately when the condition will not wait for the normal process.

## Revisions and currency

An AD can be superseded by a later AD that revises the required action; the superseding AD states which AD it replaces. Compliance status is tracked in the aircraft maintenance records, and AD currency is part of any airworthiness check.

## Where to find it

`drs.faa.gov` is the authoritative AD search. `rgl.faa.gov` historically hosted them. ADs are also published in the Federal Register.

## How it relates to the others

An AD is the enforcement end of the chain. A **TSO** sets the standard an article was built to; if a defect later surfaces in that article, an AD mandates the correction. A **SAFO** is the advisory cousin -- a safety alert with no mandatory action -- whereas an AD is the mandatory instrument. The AD's authority is **14 CFR Part 39**.

References: **14 CFR Part 39** (Airworthiness Directives); the AD search at `drs.faa.gov`.
