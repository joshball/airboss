# FIKI / non-FIKI

**FIKI** stands for **Flight Into Known Icing**. It describes an aircraft that is certificated and equipped to be legally and safely operated in known or forecast icing conditions -- a FIKI-approved airplane carries a **known-icing certification** with the deicing or anti-icing system (boots, weeping-wing, heated surfaces) required to earn it.

A **non-FIKI** aircraft is everything else: most piston singles and many light twins. A non-FIKI airplane may carry partial ice protection -- a heated pitot tube, an alternate static source, perhaps a single heated windshield panel -- but that equipment does not amount to a known-icing certification. The airplane is **not approved for flight into known icing**, full stop.

The operational consequence is the decision rule it forces. For a non-FIKI aircraft, a forecast or report of icing along the route is not a "manage it" item -- it is a no-go or a reroute. The pilot's job is to stay out of the icing entirely: pick an altitude below the freezing level or above the cloud tops, route around it, or not go. A FIKI aircraft has more options, but even then the certification buys time to exit icing, not a license to loiter in it.

Whether an airplane is FIKI is found in its Aircraft Flight Manual and type certificate data. It is one of the first facts to establish when an icing forecast touches the route.

References: **AC 91-74B** (Pilot Guide: Flight in Icing Conditions); **14 CFR 91.527** (operating in icing conditions); the **Aircraft Flight Manual** limitations section for the specific aircraft.
