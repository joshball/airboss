# FAA Handbook

**FAA handbooks** are the training-depth references. Where 14 CFR is terse and an Advisory Circular is procedural, a handbook teaches: it explains the underlying aerodynamics, weather, systems, and technique at the depth a student needs to actually understand the subject. The core titles are:

- **PHAK** -- Pilot's Handbook of Aeronautical Knowledge (the broad ground-school reference).
- **AFH** -- Airplane Flying Handbook (flight technique and maneuvers).
- **IFH** -- Instrument Flying Handbook (instrument procedures and technique).
- **Aviation Weather Handbook** -- the consolidated weather reference (it absorbed several older weather ACs).
- Plus the Helicopter Flying Handbook, Weight and Balance Handbook, and others.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory. Handbooks are educational references, not regulation. They carry no legal force, and a checkride is graded against the ACS rather than against a handbook. Their authority is the depth and reliability of the explanation, not enforceability.

## How it is identified

Handbooks carry an **FAA-H-NNNN** document number (for example `FAA-H-8083-25` for the PHAK) plus a revision letter. In practice pilots cite them by the familiar abbreviation -- PHAK, AFH, IFH -- and by chapter.

## Revisions and currency

Handbooks are revised periodically and reissued with a new revision letter. Editions can lag current rules and equipment, so for a currency-sensitive detail confirm against 14 CFR or the AIM rather than relying on a handbook alone.

## Where to find it

`faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation` hosts the current handbooks as free PDFs.

## How it relates to the others

A handbook is the deepest training layer. **14 CFR** binds; an **Advisory Circular** gives an acceptable means of compliance; the **AIM** gives operational procedure; the handbook gives the full background a learner needs to understand all three. The **ACS** task tables cite handbook chapters directly, so a handbook is usually the right place to study a checkride task in depth.
