# 1800wxbrief / Flight Service

**1800wxbrief.com** is the web portal of **Leidos Flight Service**, the contractor that operates the FAA's pilot weather briefing and flight-plan filing service. The name comes from the legacy telephone number 1-800-WX-BRIEF, which still reaches a Flight Service briefer by voice.

This is the **official-briefing** source. A briefing obtained through 1800wxbrief (web, app, or phone) is logged against the pilot's account, which establishes a record that a preflight weather briefing was obtained -- relevant to the preflight-action requirement of **14 CFR 91.103**. EFBs like [ForeFlight](/reference/glossary/foreflight) and [Garmin Pilot](/reference/glossary/garmin-pilot) integrate with Leidos so a briefing requested in the app is logged the same way.

A briefing can be requested as **standard** (full picture for a flight more than six hours out, or the first briefing for a flight), **abbreviated** (an update to supplement a briefing already obtained), or **outlook** (a planning-horizon look six or more hours before departure). The portal also handles VFR and IFR flight-plan filing, activation, and closing.

The distinction from a planning aggregator like [SkyVector](/reference/glossary/skyvector): SkyVector shows you the weather, but 1800wxbrief is where the official, logged briefing is obtained and the flight plan is filed.

References: **AIM 7-1-2** (FAA Weather Services) and **AIM 5-1** (Preflight) for the briefing types and their use; **14 CFR 91.103** for the preflight-action requirement.
