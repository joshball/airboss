# Surface analysis chart

A **hand-drawn synoptic map** of surface pressure systems (highs and lows), frontal boundaries (cold, warm, occluded, stationary), isobars, and station observations across the analysis region. Issued every 3 hours (00, 03, 06, 09, 12, 15, 18, 21Z) by the **Weather Prediction Center** (WPC) and consumed as the synoptic frame for every preflight briefing.

The chart is a meteorologist's interpretation of the observation network, not an algorithmic plot -- pressure centers, fronts, and isobar shape reflect human analysis. Pilots read it for the big-picture story: where the energy is, which way fronts are moving, where pressure gradient is steep enough to drive strong surface winds.

The pilot-pitched skill is a three-step ladder: **decode** the symbol set (H/L markers, frontal glyphs, station models, isobar spacing), **understand** the synoptic story (what airmass is replacing what, where the gradient lives), and **triage** which features on the chart matter for the planned route at the planned ETA. Pair the surface analysis with the prog chart for "where is this going next?" and with the CVA (Ceiling and Visibility Analysis) for the operational VFR/MVFR/IFR overlay.

References: **AC 00-45H** (Aviation Weather Services, Surface Analysis Chart section) for the symbol format and update cadence; **AIM 7-1** (National Weather Service Aviation Products) for operational use; **FAA-H-8083-28B** Chapter 25 (Analysis) for the modern consolidated treatment.
