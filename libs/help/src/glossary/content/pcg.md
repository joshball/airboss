# P/CG

The **P/CG (Pilot/Controller Glossary)** is the shared dictionary of aviation terms. It defines, precisely, the words that pilots and controllers use so that both sides of a radio exchange mean the same thing by the same word. When a controller says "expedite" or "immediately" or "resume own navigation," the P/CG is where those terms are defined to a fixed meaning.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory. The P/CG is a reference, not a regulation. Its value is not legal force but precision: it removes ambiguity from the language that the AIM and ATC procedures are written in. The binding obligations sit in 14 CFR; the P/CG just nails down what the words mean.

## How it is identified

The P/CG is not numbered like an AC or cited by chapter like the AIM. It is an alphabetical glossary, so a term is referenced simply by the word itself, for example the P/CG entry for "CEILING" or "TRAFFIC IN SIGHT." It is published as a component of the AIM.

## Revisions and currency

The P/CG is updated on the same publication cycle as the AIM and reissued with it. As ATC phraseology and procedures evolve, terms are added, revised, or removed; use the current edition.

## Where to find it

The P/CG is published as part of the **AIM** at `faa.gov/air_traffic/publications`, and is included in the combined **FAR/AIM** book.

## How it relates to the others

The P/CG backs the **AIM**: the AIM's procedures and the controller phraseology in **FAA Order JO 7110.65** are written in the vocabulary the P/CG defines. Reading the P/CG alongside the AIM is how a pilot learns to interpret a clearance exactly as the controller intends it.
