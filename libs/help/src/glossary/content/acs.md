# ACS

The **ACS (Airman Certification Standards)** is the document that defines what a checkride tests. For each certificate or rating it lists the **areas of operation**, the **tasks** within each area, and for every task the specific **knowledge**, **risk management**, and **skill** elements an applicant must demonstrate. It is the examiner's blueprint and the applicant's study map.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory in the regulatory sense -- the ACS is not 14 CFR -- but it is the operative standard for a practical test. A designated examiner conducts the checkride against the ACS, so while it does not carry the force of regulation, it effectively governs what you will be asked and what counts as a pass. The certificate requirements themselves come from 14 CFR Part 61; the ACS defines how those requirements are evaluated.

## How it is identified

ACS documents carry an **FAA-S-ACS-NN** number (for example `FAA-S-ACS-6` for Private Pilot - Airplane) plus a revision letter. The number identifies the certificate; the revision letter tracks the edition.

## Revisions and currency

The ACS **replaced the older PTS (Practical Test Standards)**. The PTS listed tasks and tolerances; the ACS adds the explicit knowledge and risk-management elements and ties each to the relevant references. ACS documents are revised periodically; an applicant must prepare against the edition in effect on the date of the test, so always confirm the current revision before a checkride.

## Where to find it

`faa.gov/training_testing/testing/acs` lists the current ACS for every certificate and rating.

## How it relates to the others

The ACS is the destination the rest of the system points at. **14 CFR Part 61** sets the certificate requirements; **FAA handbooks** (PHAK, AFH, IFH) supply the training-depth knowledge; the **AIM** supplies operational procedure. The ACS task tables cite those documents directly, so the ACS is the index that tells a learner which handbook chapter and which regulation a given checkride task draws on.

References: the **Airman Certification Standards** at `faa.gov/training_testing/testing/acs`; **14 CFR Part 61** for the underlying certificate requirements.
