# SAFO

A **SAFO (Safety Alert for Operators)** is a time-sensitive FAA safety message. It exists to get an important safety concern in front of operators and pilots quickly -- a hazard pattern, a recurring mistake, a lesson from an accident or incident -- without waiting for a rulemaking or a handbook revision.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory, but high-priority. A SAFO carries no mandatory action and no legal force: it cannot ground an aircraft or require a procedure the way an Airworthiness Directive can. What it carries is urgency and credibility. The FAA issues a SAFO when it judges the safety information important enough that operators should act on it now. Ignoring a SAFO is not a regulatory violation, but it is poor airmanship.

## How it is identified

A SAFO number reads `SAFO 23005`:

- `SAFO` -- the prefix.
- `23` -- the year of issue.
- `005` -- the sequence number within the year.

The document is short, usually one or two pages: a clear statement of the safety concern, who it affects, and the recommended action.

## Revisions and currency

SAFOs are dated and are not revised the way an AC is; a newer SAFO can supersede the message of an older one. Because they are time-sensitive, the most recent SAFOs are the ones to scan.

## Where to find it

`faa.gov/other_visit/aviation_industry/airline_operators/airline_safety/safo` lists SAFOs by year.

## How it relates to the others

A SAFO is the advisory partner of the **InFO (Information for Operators)**: the SAFO carries safety-critical, time-sensitive content, while the InFO carries non-urgent operational information. Both are advisory. Where a hazard requires a mandatory fix to specific equipment, the FAA reaches instead for an **Airworthiness Directive**, which is binding.

References: the **SAFO** index at `faa.gov`; compare the companion **InFO** series.
