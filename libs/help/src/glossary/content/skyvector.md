# SkyVector

**SkyVector** is a free, browser-based flight-planning website. It displays sectional, terminal-area, low/high IFR enroute charts, lets a pilot draw a route, and overlays weather -- METARs, TAFs, AIRMETs, SIGMETs, winds aloft -- on the chart.

Unlike [ForeFlight](/reference/glossary/foreflight) and [Garmin Pilot](/reference/glossary/garmin-pilot), SkyVector is not a mobile EFB and offers no in-flight moving-map navigation. It is purely a **pre-flight planning aggregator**: a desktop tool for laying out a route and scanning the weather along it before a flight. It is popular precisely because it is free and requires no install.

For **weather briefing**, SkyVector pulls the same FAA/NWS source feeds the EFBs use. It does not file or log an official briefing through Flight Service -- a pilot who plans on SkyVector still obtains the official briefing through [1800wxbrief.com](/reference/glossary/flight-service-1800wxbrief) or an EFB with Leidos integration.

References: **AIM 7-1** (National Weather Service Aviation Products) for the products SkyVector aggregates.
