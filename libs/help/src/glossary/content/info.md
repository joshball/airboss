# InFO

An **InFO (Information for Operators)** is an FAA notice that shares operational information of interest to operators and pilots. It is the FAA's channel for telling the operating community about something useful or noteworthy that does not rise to the urgency of a safety alert: a clarification, a process change, an advisory reminder, a heads-up about an upcoming requirement.

## Binding or advisory

Advisory. An InFO carries no mandatory action and no legal force. It is informational: it tells you something worth knowing, but it does not require you to do anything. The binding obligations still live in 14 CFR.

## How it is identified

An InFO number reads `InFO 23002`:

- `InFO` -- the prefix.
- `23` -- the year of issue.
- `002` -- the sequence number within the year.

Like a SAFO, an InFO is short -- a page or two stating the information and who it concerns.

## Revisions and currency

InFOs are dated and are not formally revised; later notices can supersede earlier ones on the same topic. They are issued as needed rather than on a fixed cycle.

## Where to find it

`faa.gov/other_visit/aviation_industry/airline_operators/airline_safety/info` lists InFOs by year.

## How it relates to the others

The InFO is the companion of the **SAFO (Safety Alert for Operators)**. They share a format and a numbering style, and the FAA issues them through the same channel, but they carry different weight: the SAFO is for time-sensitive safety content that operators should act on, the InFO is for non-urgent operational information. Both are advisory. If you remember one distinction, remember that: SAFO means "safety, act now," InFO means "for your information."
