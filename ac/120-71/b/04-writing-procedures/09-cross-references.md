4.9     Cross-References. Cross-references to other procedures should be avoided whenever
        possible. In most cases, a “Go-in, Stay-in” strategy should be used whereby all necessary
        steps for completing a procedure are included in that procedure. In some cases, this may
        prove impractical.

4.9.1   Explicit Reference. When used, cross-references should be explicit and use consistent
        wording.

4.9.2   Version Control. When references to other procedures are required, a system should be in
        place to monitor version control. Changes made to either the referring or referred
        procedures could affect the execution of the included steps.
