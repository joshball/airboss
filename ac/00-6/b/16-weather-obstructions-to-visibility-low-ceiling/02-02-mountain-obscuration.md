16.2.2 Mountain Obscuration. A mountain obscuration is a condition in which mountains
       or mountain ridges are obscured due to clouds, precipitation, smoke, or other
       obscurations.

       Flight can be especially hazardous over mountain routes when the mountains are
       obscured. The large elevation variations around mountains can cause surface weather
       observations to mislead. For example, a weather station located in a valley could report
       a visual flight rules (VFR) cloud ceiling, while a hiker in the mountains sees fog.
