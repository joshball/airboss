9.6     Valley Breeze. A valley breeze (see Figure 9-8) is a wind that ascends a mountain valley
        during the day. Air in contact with the sloping terrain becomes warmer (less dense) than
        air above the valley. This is because the air in contact with the sloping terrain heats
        up faster than air above the valley.

      Pressure gradients develop (along a horizontal reference) with lower pressure over the
      warmer sloping terrain and higher pressure over the cooler valley winds develop in the
      direction of the PGF. Thus, the wind blows from the valley up the mountain slopes.
      Air rises over sloping terrain and sinks over the valley. Clouds and precipitation may
      develop over mountain slopes.
