5.2.3    Station Pressure. The pressure measured at an airport is called station pressure, or the
         actual pressure at field elevation. Pressure is lower at higher altitudes. Therefore, airports
         with higher field elevations usually have lower pressure than airports with lower field
         elevations. For instance, station pressure at Denver is less than at New Orleans
         (see Figure 5-3).
