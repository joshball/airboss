20.1.4 Wavelengths. The wavelength is the distance between two crests or two troughs within
       the radio wave emitted from the radar (see Figure 20-3). The WSR-88D has a wavelength
       of 10 centimeters. Most aircraft radars have a wavelength of 3 centimeters. Although
       shorter wavelengths are better at detecting smaller targets, they are significantly more
       attenuated than longer wavelengths.
