21.2.1 Remnants of Polar Fronts and Shear Lines. Remnants of a polar front can become lines
       of convection and occasionally generate a tropical cyclone. By the time a cold air mass
       originating in high latitudes reaches the Tropics, temperature and moisture are virtually
       the same on both sides of the front. A shear line, or wind shift, is all that remains
       (see Figure 21-3). These mainly influence storms in the Atlantic Ocean, Gulf of Mexico,
       or Caribbean Sea early or late in the hurricane season.
