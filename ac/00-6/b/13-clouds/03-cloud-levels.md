13.3   Cloud Levels. By convention, the part of the atmosphere in which clouds are usually
       present has been divided into three levels: high, middle, and low (see Table 13-2). Each
       level is defined by the range of heights at which the cloud of a certain type occurs most
       frequently. The levels overlap and their limits vary with latitude. The approximate
       heights of the limits are as follows:

                                       6,500-23,000 ft (2-7 km)       6,500-25,000 ft (2-8 km)
