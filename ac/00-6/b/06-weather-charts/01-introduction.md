6.1     Introduction. A weather chart is a map on which data and analyses are presented that
        describe the state of the atmosphere over a large area at a given moment in time.

        The possible variety of such charts is enormous, but in meteorological history there has
        been a more or less standard set of charts, including surface charts and the constant
        pressure charts of the upper atmosphere. Because weather systems are three-dimensional
        (3-D), both surface and upper air charts are needed. Surface weather charts depict
        weather on a constant-altitude (usually sea level) surface, while upper air charts depict
        weather on constant-pressure surfaces.

        The National Weather Service (NWS) produces many weather charts that support the
        aviation community. Refer to the current edition of AC 00-45, Aviation Weather
        Services, for details.
