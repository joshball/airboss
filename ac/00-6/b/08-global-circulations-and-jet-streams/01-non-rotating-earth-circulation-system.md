8.1   Non-Rotating Earth Circulation System. Global circulations explain how air and storm
      systems travel over the Earth’s surface. The global circulation would be simple (and the
      weather boring) if the Earth did not rotate, the rotation was not tilted relative to the sun,
      and had no water.

      Without those factors, the ground and atmosphere directly beneath the sun would
      be subject to more of the sun’s heat than anywhere else on the planet. The result would
      be the Equator becoming very hot, with the hot air rising into the upper atmosphere.

      That hot air would then move toward the poles, where it would become very cold and
      sink, returning to the Equator (see Figure 8-1). One large area of high pressure would
      be at each of the poles, with a large belt of low pressure around the Equator.
