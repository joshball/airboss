9.1 Restricting Airborne Cellular Telephone Use. There are additional considerations for
     PEDs with cellular or mobile telephony capabilities. These PEDs include mobile
     telephones and computers with cellular wireless network capabilities. The FCC prohibits
     cellular telephone operation while airborne, as noted in Title 47 of the Code of Federal
     Regulations (47 CFR) part 22, § 22.925. This restriction is valid for all aircraft without
     technical provisions that enable control of onboard mobile devices to eliminate
     interference between ground-based cellular stations and airborne cellular devices. If
     technical provisions are installed, compliant with, or exempt from 47 CFR § 22.925, then
     usage of cellular-enabled PEDs is acceptable.
