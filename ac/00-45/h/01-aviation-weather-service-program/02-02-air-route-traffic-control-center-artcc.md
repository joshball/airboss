1.2.2   Air Route Traffic Control Center (ARTCC).
        An ARTCC is a facility established to provide air traffic control (ATC) service to aircraft

        operating on instrument flight rules (IFR) flight plans within controlled airspace,
        principally during the en route phase of flight. When equipment capabilities and
        controller workload permit, certain advisory/assistance services may be provided to
        visual flight rules (VFR) aircraft.

        En route controllers become familiar with pertinent weather information and stay aware
        of current weather information needed to perform ATC duties. En route controllers
        advise pilots of hazardous weather that may impact operations within 150 nautical miles
        (NM) of the controller’s assigned sector(s), and may solicit PIREPs from pilots.
