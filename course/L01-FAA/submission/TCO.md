# Training Course Outline (TCO)

## Flight Instructor Refresher Course (FIRC)

### Interactive Scenario-Based Instruction System

---

## 1. Course Overview

### 1.1 Course Title

Flight Instructor Refresher Course (FIRC) -- Scenario-Based Interactive Delivery

### 1.2 Purpose

The purpose of this course is to enhance the instructional knowledge, risk management ability, and decision-making skills of certificated flight instructors in order to improve instructional effectiveness, build confident, safety-oriented pilots, and reduce general aviation training accidents.

This course emphasizes:

- Scenario-based instruction (SBT)
- Risk-based decision making
- Instructional judgment
- Real-time intervention skills
- Regulatory and administrative compliance

### 1.3 Target Audience

- FAA Certificated Flight Instructors (CFI, CFII, MEI)
- Instructors seeking renewal under 14 CFR SS 61.197
- Instructors seeking reinstatement under 14 CFR SS 61.199(a)(1)

Note: Instructors who have gone beyond their recent experience requirement or any other interested parties are encouraged to participate; however, those attending for reasons other than recent experience will receive a FIRC Certificate of Completion but will not be able to establish recent experience.

### 1.4 Course Delivery Method

This course is delivered via an interactive, web-based training platform utilizing:

- Scenario-based instruction (SBT)
- Guided simulation exercises
- Decision-based interaction
- Adaptive reinforcement techniques
- Integrated knowledge and application assessments

Learners actively participate in instructional scenarios requiring real-time decision-making, evaluation, and intervention.

### 1.5 Course Duration

- Minimum required instructional time: **16.0 hours**
- Time is tracked through active interaction
- Periods of inactivity pause course progression

### 1.6 Completion Requirements

To receive a graduation certificate:

- Complete all required instructional modules
- Achieve required performance thresholds in assessments (minimum competency equivalent to >= 70%)
- Demonstrate competency across all required subject areas
- Meet or exceed the minimum time requirement (16 hours)

## 2. Course Structure

The course is organized into six instructional modules, each integrating multiple FAA-required subject areas.

### Module 1 -- Instructor Foundations & Modern Cockpit

**Time: 2.75 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- Navigating in the 21st Century (GPS, TAA, automation)
- Regulatory / policy / publication updates
- Airman Certification Standards (ACS)

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Demonstrate understanding of automation capabilities and limitations
- Identify and correct automation dependency in students
- Integrate ACS elements (knowledge, skill, risk) into instruction
- Apply current regulatory and policy guidance
- Distinguish current from outdated regulatory sources

**Instructional Methods:**

- Scenario-based automation exercises
- System interpretation challenges
- Guided debrief and ACS mapping
- Source-verification exercises

**Assessment:**

- Scenario performance scoring
- Knowledge reinforcement prompts
- ACS-based evaluation tasks

---

### Module 2 -- Instructional Effectiveness & Safety Culture

**Time: 3.0 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- Teaching and building a culture of safety
- Ethics and professionalism
- Current safety trends in general aviation

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Apply effective instructional techniques in dynamic scenarios
- Demonstrate appropriate intervention strategies
- Model professional and ethical behavior
- Resist social and commercial pressure to compromise safety
- Identify accident trends and apply mitigation strategies
- Diagnose student misunderstanding and calibrate intervention level

**Instructional Methods:**

- Real-time instructional decision scenarios
- Communication and coaching simulations
- Guided debrief analysis
- Risk identification exercises

**Assessment:**

- Intervention timing and appropriateness scoring
- Instructional quality evaluation
- Scenario outcome analysis
- Decision branch evaluation

---

### Module 3 -- Loss of Control & Flight Risk Intervention

**Time: 3.25 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- Loss of Control (LOC)
- Pilot deviations
- Positive exchange of controls
- Traffic pattern and approach safety

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Detect early indicators of unstable flight conditions
- Prevent stall/spin scenarios through appropriate intervention
- Execute proper transfer of controls procedures (three-step verbal handoff)
- Mitigate risks in traffic pattern operations
- Teach taxi discipline and clearance readback discipline

**Instructional Methods:**

- Time-based ("tick") scenario simulations
- Real-time intervention decision-making
- Replay and alternate outcome analysis
- Surface operation exercises

**Assessment:**

- Cue detection accuracy
- Intervention timing and selection
- Safety outcome evaluation
- Exchange-of-controls procedure verification

---

### Module 4 -- Airspace, Security, and Compliance

**Time: 2.0 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- Security-related special use airspace
- TSA / FTSP requirements
- Accepting student and remote pilot applications

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Identify and avoid restricted or special-use airspace
- Demonstrate proper procedures for TSA compliance and FTSP verification
- Determine eligibility for flight training candidates
- Correctly process certificate applications (IACRA and paper)
- Know when training must not begin and why

**Instructional Methods:**

- Scenario-based decision exercises
- Administrative workflow simulations
- Regulatory application exercises
- Distributed instruction with spaced reinforcement

**Assessment:**

- Correct decision pathways in compliance scenarios
- Knowledge reinforcement checks
- Scenario-based validation
- Improvement tracking on later variants

---

### Module 5 -- Evaluation, Flight Reviews, and IPCs

**Time: 2.5 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- Conducting effective flight reviews
- Conducting effective instrument proficiency checks (IPCs)
- FAASTeam and WINGS program

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Design and conduct meaningful flight reviews
- Tailor IPCs to pilot proficiency
- Apply FAASTeam and WINGS resources
- Identify and address pilot deficiencies
- Avoid rote minimum-time reviews

**Instructional Methods:**

- Evaluation planning scenarios
- Adaptive review simulations
- Debrief and performance analysis
- Proficiency plan building

**Assessment:**

- Evaluation plan quality
- Diagnostic accuracy
- Instructional adaptability

---

### Module 6 -- Integrated Scenario Assessment (Capstone)

**Time: 2.5 hours**

**Topics Covered:**

- All core topics integrated
- Cross-domain assessment

**Learning Objectives -- The instructor will:**

- Demonstrate integrated instructional competency across all domains
- Apply risk management across multiple simultaneous factors
- Make effective real-time instructional decisions
- Analyze and improve performance through replay

**Instructional Methods:**

- Multi-phase scenario simulations
- Replay and refinement cycles
- Guided reflective debrief

**Assessment:**

- Comprehensive performance scoring
- Improvement tracking (initial vs final performance)
- Competency validation across all domains

---

## 3. Assessment Strategy

### 3.1 Scenario-Based Evaluation

- Decision-making performance
- Intervention timing
- Instructional effectiveness

### 3.2 Continuous Performance Tracking

- Targeted recall prompts
- Contextual questioning
- Reinforcement checks

### 3.3 Improvement Measurement

- Initial vs final performance comparison
- Competency progression tracking
- Replay delta analysis

### 3.4 Completion Threshold

- Minimum performance equivalent to >= 70%
- Demonstrated competency across all required domains

---

## 4. Time & Engagement Model

- Minimum course time: 16 hours
- Time is accumulated only during active interaction
- Periods of inactivity pause progress
- Learners must continuously engage with course material
- Active decisions required throughout all modules

Time is accumulated across multiple instructional interactions within and across modules. A single scenario may contribute to multiple FAA core topic areas.

---

## 5. Records and Reporting

The system maintains:

- Course completion records
- Time tracking logs (per topic and total)
- Topic coverage tracking
- Assessment results
- Scenario performance logs

Certificates are issued upon successful completion.

Records are retained in accordance with FAA requirements.

---

## 6. System Description (Summary)

The course is delivered via a web-based platform that:

- Presents interactive instructional scenarios
- Records user interactions and decisions
- Tracks instructional time and engagement
- Evaluates performance across defined competencies
- Provides feedback and reinforcement
- Prevents idle completion through activity requirements

The system ensures that all learners receive sufficient instructional exposure in each FAA-required core topic area through structured scenario sequencing and reinforcement.
