### Q-A7-01 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-01)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-3, PS-2

A student completing a flight review asks about the WINGS program. They want to know if completing a WINGS phase can substitute for a flight review. What is the correct answer?

A) No, WINGS and flight reviews are completely separate programs with no overlap
B) Yes, completing a phase of the WINGS program satisfies the requirements of a flight review per 14 CFR 61.56(e)
C) Only the Master level of WINGS substitutes for a flight review
D) WINGS can substitute for the ground portion but not the flight portion of a review

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 61.56(e), completing a phase of the FAA's WINGS Pilot Proficiency Program satisfies the flight review requirement. This is one of the key benefits of WINGS that CFIs should actively promote. Each phase requires both knowledge and flight activities, which together meet the intent of a flight review. Option A is incorrect per 61.56(e). Option C incorrectly limits the benefit to Master level. Option D misunderstands the program structure.

### Q-A7-02 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-01)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2

How should a CFI best integrate FAASTeam resources into their instruction?

A) Direct students to the FAASTeam website after they earn their certificate, as it's mainly for licensed pilots
B) Use FAASTeam safety seminars, online courses, and resources as supplemental training throughout a student's progression, and credit applicable activities toward WINGS
C) FAASTeam resources are intended for flight schools, not individual instructors
D) Recommend FAASTeam events only to students who are struggling

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** The FAASTeam provides free resources -- safety seminars, online courses, publications, and local events -- that supplement CFI instruction at all stages. Integrating these resources throughout training builds the habit of continuous learning and introduces students to WINGS early. Option A delays introduction of a valuable safety resource. Option C is incorrect -- FAASTeam resources are for all aviation participants. Option D inappropriately limits a resource that benefits all students.

**Competencies:** PS-2, ES-1

A certificated private pilot comes to you and says they want to improve their skills but doesn't need a flight review for another year. How would you use the WINGS program in this situation?

B) Suggest they enroll in a WINGS phase, which provides structured proficiency activities that can also satisfy their next flight review requirement
C) WINGS is only for pilots who need a flight review

**Correct:** B

Recommending a WINGS phase gives this pilot structured goals, access to FAASTeam training topics, and the bonus of satisfying their eventual flight review requirement. This is exactly the kind of proactive safety engagement AC 61-83K wants CFIs to promote. Option A turns away a motivated pilot. Option C misunderstands WINGS. Option D provides no structure for improvement.

### Q-A7-06 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-03)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** PS-2

According to AC 61-83K, which of the following are benefits available to flight instructors through the FAASTeam and WINGS program? (Select all that apply)

A) Flight instructors can earn WINGS credit for completing a FIRC
B) Instructors who teach in the WINGS program can establish flight instructor recent experience based on WINGS instructional flight activities
C) The FAASTeam website (faasafety.gov) provides automatic email announcements of local and national safety seminars to registered users
D) WINGS participation automatically upgrades a CFI's certificate to Gold Seal
E) Flight instructors can increase their client pool through delivery of WINGS post-certification proficiency training

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.5, CFIs earn WINGS credit for completing a FIRC (A), can establish recent experience through WINGS instructional activities (B), and can grow their client base via WINGS proficiency training (E). Per A.7.2, the FAASTeam website provides automatic email announcements to registered users (C). There is no automatic Gold Seal upgrade through WINGS (D) -- Gold Seal requirements are defined in 14 CFR 61.195(h) and involve pass rates and recommendation counts.

**Competencies:** PS-2, RC-1

You are a newly certificated CFI building your instruction practice. A fellow instructor mentions that the FAASTeam is "a totally free resource" but you're skeptical. Which statement best describes what the FAASTeam offers at no cost?

A) Free aircraft rental for CFIs conducting WINGS flights
B) A network of FAASTeam Program Managers, safety seminars, online courses, publications, and the faasafety.gov website -- all at no charge to flight instructors
C) Free liability insurance for CFIs who participate in WINGS
D) Free DPE services for CFI applicants who are active WINGS instructors

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7, the FAASTeam is described as "a vast and totally free resource of personnel and tools," coordinated through the SPPO and consisting of FPMs, safety seminars, online courses, and the faasafety.gov website. None of these require payment. Options A, C, and D describe benefits that do not exist within the FAASTeam program.

### Q-A7-08 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, AV-2

A CFI at a small airport has no local flight school and few networking opportunities. They want to connect with the FAA's safety infrastructure. What is the primary coordination mechanism for the FAASTeam in their area?

A) The local FSDO handles all FAASTeam coordination directly
B) The Safety Promotion Program Office (SPPO) coordinates the FAASTeam through a network of FAASTeam Program Managers (FPMs)
C) FAASTeam coordination is handled exclusively through airline safety departments
D) The NTSB manages the FAASTeam network at the regional level

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7, the FAASTeam is "coordinated through the FAA's Safety Promotion Program Office (SPPO), consisting of a network of FAASTeam Program Managers (FPM)." FPMs are the local point of contact for CFIs wanting to engage with FAASTeam resources. The FSDO has a related but distinct role. The NTSB and airline safety departments are not part of the FAASTeam coordination structure.
