# A.7.1 -- FAASTeam Collaborative Partners

Questions covering FAASTeam Members, FAASTeam Representatives (REPs), and FAASTeam Industry Members (FIMs).

---

### Q-A7-17 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-09)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-2, PS-1

A pilot at your airport tells you they recently became a "FAASTeam Member" and want to know what that means in practice. According to AC 61-83K, which description is most accurate?

A) FAASTeam Members are FAA employees assigned to monitor local flight operations
B) FAASTeam Members are individuals who promote aviation safety within the aviation community
C) FAASTeam Members are retired airline pilots who volunteer as check airmen
D) FAASTeam Members are insurance company representatives who assess pilot risk

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, FAASTeam Members are described as "individuals promoting aviation safety." They are volunteers from the aviation community -- not FAA employees (A), retired airline pilots specifically (C), or insurance representatives (D). Any pilot, instructor, or aviation enthusiast can become a FAASTeam Member.

### Q-A7-18 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-09)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, RC-1

You are mentoring a newer CFI who wants to get more involved in aviation safety beyond their daily instruction. They ask about the different levels of FAASTeam participation. What distinguishes a FAASTeam Representative (REP) from a general FAASTeam Member?

A) REPs receive FAA compensation for their safety work; Members do not
B) REPs are active safety volunteers who work directly with FAASTeam Program Managers; Members are individuals who promote safety in a general capacity
C) REPs are limited to organizing fly-ins; Members handle all other events
D) There is no functional difference -- the terms are interchangeable

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, FAASTeam Representatives are "active safety volunteers working with FPMs," while FAASTeam Members are "individuals promoting aviation safety." REPs have a more active, coordinated role working alongside FPMs. Neither role is compensated (A), REPs are not limited to fly-ins (C), and the terms describe distinct roles (D).

### Q-A7-19 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-10)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** AV-1, PS-2

According to AC 61-83K, which of the following correctly describe the three categories of FAASTeam collaborative partners? (Select all that apply)

A) FAASTeam Members are individuals who promote aviation safety
B) FAASTeam Representatives (REPs) are active safety volunteers who work with FAASTeam Program Managers
C) FAASTeam Industry Members (FIMs) are companies with a vested interest in aviation safety
D) FAASTeam Inspectors are FSDO personnel who conduct surveillance of flight schools
E) FAASTeam Examiners are designated pilot examiners appointed through the program

**Correct:** A, B, C
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, the three collaborative partner categories are: (1) FAASTeam Members -- individuals promoting aviation safety (A); (2) FAASTeam Representatives (REP) -- active safety volunteers working with FPMs (B); (3) FAASTeam Industry Members (FIM) -- companies with a vested interest in aviation safety (C). "FAASTeam Inspectors" (D) and "FAASTeam Examiners" (E) are not categories defined in the AC.

### Q-A7-20 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-10)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, PS-2

A local avionics shop owner asks you how their company can formally participate in FAA safety promotion. Based on AC 61-83K A.7.1, which FAASTeam partner category would apply to a business?

A) FAASTeam Member, since the owner is an individual
B) FAASTeam Representative, since the company works with local pilots
C) FAASTeam Industry Member (FIM), which is the category for companies with a vested interest in aviation safety
D) Companies cannot participate in the FAASTeam -- it is limited to individual pilots and instructors

**Correct:** C
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, FAASTeam Industry Members (FIMs) are "companies with vested interest in aviation safety." An avionics shop fits this category. FAASTeam Members (A) are individuals, not companies. REPs (B) are individual volunteers. Option D is incorrect -- the FAASTeam explicitly includes a business participation category.

### Q-A7-21 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-11)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-1, RC-2

A CFI wants to organize a safety seminar at their local airport but doesn't know who to coordinate with. According to AC 61-83K, which FAASTeam partner would be the most appropriate local contact for planning a collaborative safety event?

A) A FAASTeam Industry Member, since companies sponsor events
B) A FAASTeam Representative (REP), who is an active safety volunteer working with the local FAASTeam Program Manager
C) The National Transportation Safety Board regional office
D) A FAASTeam Member, who is assigned to manage local events

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, FAASTeam Representatives are "active safety volunteers working with FPMs." REPs serve as the bridge between the CFI and the FAASTeam Program Manager, making them the ideal local contact for coordinating safety events. FIMs (A) are companies, not event coordinators. The NTSB (C) is not part of the FAASTeam structure. FAASTeam Members (D) promote safety generally but are not assigned to manage events.

B) FIM stands for FAASTeam Industry Member -- a company with a vested interest in aviation safety, and their products and services may benefit pilots at all levels
C) FIM stands for Federal Inspection Manager -- an FAA official who inspects flight schools
D) FIM stands for Flight Information Module -- an online training course on faasafety.gov

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** CJ-1, PS-2

You are explaining the FAASTeam structure to a group of local pilots. Which of the following statements about FAASTeam collaborative partners are accurate? (Select all that apply)

A) Any individual can become a FAASTeam Member by promoting aviation safety
B) FAASTeam Representatives work more closely with FAASTeam Program Managers than general Members do
C) FAASTeam Industry Members must be Part 121 air carriers
D) The three partner categories create a network that includes individuals, active volunteers, and companies
E) FAASTeam Representatives are paid a stipend for their volunteer work

**Correct:** A, B, D

REPs are "active safety volunteers working with FPMs," indicating closer coordination than general Members (B). The three categories -- Members, REPs, and FIMs -- encompass individuals, volunteers, and companies (D). FIMs are described as "companies with vested interest in aviation safety" -- not limited to Part 121 carriers (C). REPs are volunteers, not paid (E).

### Q-A7-24 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-12)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-1, PS-2

A CFI operating at a small rural airport has limited access to other aviation professionals. They want to engage with the FAASTeam but assume they need to be near a major airport. Based on AC 61-83K A.7.1, what should they know about FAASTeam participation?

A) FAASTeam participation requires proximity to a Class B or Class C airport
B) The FAASTeam collaborative partner structure -- Members, REPs, and FIMs -- is designed as a network that connects individuals and organizations regardless of location, coordinated through FPMs
C) Only pilots at Part 141 flight schools can become FAASTeam participants
D) FAASTeam events are exclusively held at FAA facilities in metropolitan areas

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7 and A.7.1, the FAASTeam is organized as a network coordinated through FPMs, with partner categories that include individuals (Members), active volunteers (REPs), and companies (FIMs). Nothing in the AC limits participation by airport size (A), school type (C), or geographic location (D). The network structure is designed to extend safety promotion throughout the aviation community.

### Q-A7-25 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-13)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-3, OD-1

A flight school owner wants to formally align their business with FAA safety promotion efforts. They already have individual instructors who are FAASTeam Members. What additional step would leverage the FAASTeam Industry Member (FIM) category?

A) Have the school register as a FAASTeam Industry Member, establishing the business itself as a collaborative safety partner alongside its individual instructor Members
B) Nothing further is needed -- individual memberships automatically cover the business
C) The school must first become a Part 141 certificated institution before FIM eligibility
D) FIM status is only available to aircraft manufacturers and insurance companies

**Correct:** A
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, FAASTeam Industry Members are "companies with vested interest in aviation safety" -- a distinct category from individual FAASTeam Members. A flight school qualifies as a company with a vested interest in aviation safety. Individual instructor memberships (B) don't cover the business entity. There is no Part 141 requirement for FIM status (C), and FIMs are not limited to manufacturers or insurers (D).

### Q-A7-26 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-13)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** PS-2, CJ-2

A CFI is considering how the FAASTeam collaborative partner structure could benefit their professional practice. Which of the following are valid strategies? (Select all that apply)

A) Become a FAASTeam Member to connect with the safety promotion network
B) Pursue FAASTeam Representative status to actively volunteer alongside their local FPM
C) Recommend that their flight school register as a FAASTeam Industry Member
D) Use FAASTeam Member status to bypass CFI renewal requirements
E) Network with FAASTeam Industry Members to learn about new safety-related products and services

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.1, becoming a Member (A) and pursuing REP status (B) are both valid ways to engage. Recommending FIM registration for a flight school (C) leverages the industry partner category. Networking with FIMs (E) exposes CFIs to safety products and services. Option D is incorrect -- FAASTeam membership does not waive or replace CFI renewal requirements, which are governed by 14 CFR 61.197.
