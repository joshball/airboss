# A.7.2 -- FAASTeam Website (faasafety.gov)

Questions covering faasafety.gov registration, email announcements, and topic customization.

---

### Q-A7-27 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-14)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-2, PS-1

A certificated pilot asks you where to find information about upcoming FAA safety seminars in their area. According to AC 61-83K, what is the primary online resource?

A) The NTSB accident database at ntsb.gov
B) The FAASTeam website at faasafety.gov, which is the prime education and communication tool for the FAASTeam
C) The FAA's main regulatory website at faa.gov/regulations
D) Local airport authority websites, which host all FAA safety event listings

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, "The FAASTeam website (faasafety.gov) is the prime education and communication tool." It serves as the central hub for safety seminars, webinars, online courses, and other FAASTeam resources. The NTSB site (A) covers accident investigations, faa.gov (C) covers regulations, and airport websites (D) are not the FAA's designated safety communication platform.

### Q-A7-28 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-14)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, ES-1

You want to encourage your students to stay engaged with aviation safety topics after they earn their certificates. What is the primary benefit of registering on faasafety.gov?

A) Registration grants access to discounted flight training rates
B) Registration is required before a pilot can take a practical test
C) Free registration provides automatic email announcements of local and national safety seminars and webinars
D) Registration automatically enrolls pilots in the WINGS program at the Basic level

**Correct:** C
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, "Free registration provides automatic email announcements of local and national safety seminars and webinars." This keeps pilots informed about safety events without requiring them to actively search. There are no discounted training rates (A), no practical test prerequisite (B), and registration alone does not enroll pilots in a WINGS phase (D).

### Q-A7-29 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-15)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** PS-2, AV-1

According to AC 61-83K A.7.2, which of the following are features of the FAASTeam website (faasafety.gov)? (Select all that apply)

A) It is described as the prime education and communication tool for the FAASTeam
B) Registration is free
C) Registered users receive automatic email announcements of safety seminars and webinars
D) The website charges a monthly subscription fee for premium safety content
E) Email announcements cover both local and national events

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, faasafety.gov is the "prime education and communication tool" (A), registration is "free" (B), it provides "automatic email announcements" (C) of "local and national safety seminars and webinars" (E). There is no subscription fee or premium tier (D) -- the entire resource is free.

### Q-A7-30 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-15)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-1, RC-1

A CFI has been instructing for five years but has never registered on faasafety.gov. They claim they stay current through industry magazines and word of mouth. Why should they register?

A) Registration is mandatory for all active CFIs under 14 CFR Part 61
B) Without registration, they miss automatic email notifications of local and national safety seminars and webinars -- a free service that keeps them informed about FAASTeam education events relevant to their area
C) Unregistered CFIs are flagged by the FAA for additional surveillance
D) Registration is only useful for student pilots, not experienced CFIs

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, faasafety.gov registration provides "automatic email announcements of local and national safety seminars and webinars." This passive notification system ensures the CFI stays aware of relevant events without effort. Registration is not mandatory (A), unregistered CFIs are not flagged (C), and the resource benefits all aviation participants, not just students (D).

**Competencies:** PS-2, ES-3

A pilot you instruct mentions they get "too many emails" from faasafety.gov about topics that don't interest them. Based on the capabilities of the FAASTeam website, what advice would you give?

A) Unsubscribe entirely -- the emails aren't useful for certificated pilots
B) The website allows users to customize their topic preferences so they receive announcements relevant to their interests and flying activities
C) There is no way to filter the emails -- users must accept all announcements or none
D) Forward all emails to their CFI and let the instructor decide which are relevant

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, the FAASTeam website supports topic customization, allowing users to tailor the email announcements they receive. Option C is incorrect -- customization is available. Option D places an unnecessary burden on the CFI.

D) The website is only accessible to pilots with instrument ratings or higher
E) Notifications cover both local events near the user and national-level programs

### Q-A7-33 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-17)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, PS-1

A CFI at a remote airport with no local FAASTeam events argues that faasafety.gov is useless to them. What is the best counter to this perspective?

A) They're correct -- faasafety.gov only lists in-person events at major airports
B) The website provides email announcements of national webinars and online courses in addition to local events, making it valuable regardless of location
C) Remote airport CFIs are exempt from FAASTeam participation
D) The FAA will establish local events at any airport that requests them within 30 days

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, faasafety.gov provides announcements of both "local and national safety seminars and webinars." National webinars and online courses are accessible from anywhere with internet access, making the website valuable regardless of geographic location. Option A ignores the national and online components. There is no exemption for remote locations (C), and the FAA does not guarantee on-demand event creation (D).

### Q-A7-34 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-17)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-1, RC-2

During a FIRC session, a participant asks why AC 61-83K specifically calls out faasafety.gov. What distinction does the AC give to this website?

A) It is one of many FAA websites with no special significance
B) It is the FAASTeam's "prime education and communication tool" -- the central platform for safety promotion
C) It is the only FAA website that contains regulatory information
D) It is a paid service that replaces the need for flight instructor renewal courses

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, faasafety.gov is explicitly described as "the prime education and communication tool" for the FAASTeam. This language elevates it above other FAA web resources as the central platform for safety education and communication. It is not one of many equivalent sites (A), it focuses on safety promotion rather than regulations (C), and it is free, not a replacement for FIRCs (D).

You are developing a post-certification syllabus for private pilot graduates. You want to include ongoing FAASTeam engagement. What is the simplest first step you should recommend to every graduate?

B) Register for free on faasafety.gov to begin receiving automatic email announcements about local and national safety seminars and webinars
C) Apply to become a FAASTeam Representative immediately after earning their certificate
D) Purchase a subscription to the FAA's safety newsletter service

**Correct:** B

### Q-A7-36 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-18)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** AV-1, CJ-1

A CFI wants to ensure all their active students and recent graduates are connected to FAASTeam resources. Which actions are supported by the faasafety.gov platform as described in AC 61-83K? (Select all that apply)

A) Students can register for free to receive safety event notifications
B) The website sends automatic email announcements without requiring users to manually check for events
C) Users can customize topics to receive announcements relevant to their flying activities
D) The website will automatically notify a student's CFI about their activity and progress
E) Announcements include both local events near the user and national programs

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.2, faasafety.gov offers free registration (A), automatic email announcements (B), topic customization (C), and coverage of local and national events (E). Option D is incorrect -- the platform does not send activity reports to a student's CFI. Privacy and individual account management are standard; CFIs cannot monitor student accounts through the platform.
