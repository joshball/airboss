### Q-A8-01 (Module 1, Pool A8-M1-01)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-4

You discover that a lesson plan you've used for 3 years references an Advisory Circular that has been superseded. The new AC contains updated guidance on a procedure you've been teaching. What is your obligation?

A) Continue using the current lesson plan since the old AC was valid when you created it
B) Update your lesson plan immediately to reflect the current AC and review whether any previous instruction needs correction
C) Wait until the next FIRC to learn about the update, then revise
D) The FAA only requires Part 141 schools to update materials, not Part 61 instructors

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K is explicit: the FAA expects instructors to keep materials current and to update them immediately when relevant changes occur. Flying and teaching from outdated guidance creates real risk, even if the original material was correct at the time. The CFI has a professional obligation to stay current and to review whether previously taught material needs correction. Option A normalizes stale information. Option C waits too long. Option D is incorrect -- all instructors share this responsibility.

### Q-A8-03 (Module 1, Pool A8-M1-02)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RC-4

Which of the following are reliable sources for staying current with FAA regulatory changes? (Select all that apply)

A) The Federal Register for final rules and proposed rulemaking
B) FAA.gov for updated Advisory Circulars, Orders, and policy documents
C) Aviation forums and social media groups
D) FAASTeam safety seminars and webinars
E) The Aeronautical Information Manual (AIM) for procedural updates

**Correct:** A, B, D, E
**Explanation:** Reliable regulatory currency comes from authoritative sources. The Federal Register (A) publishes all rulemaking. FAA.gov (B) maintains current ACs and policy. FAASTeam (D) specifically addresses current topics. The AIM (E) reflects current procedures. Option C -- aviation forums and social media -- may provide early awareness but are not authoritative sources and frequently contain misinformation, outdated interpretations, or anecdotal guidance.

### Q-A8-04 (Module 1, Pool A8-M1-02)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-4

A Part 141 school you instruct at still uses Training Course Outline (TCO) materials referencing PTS standards that have been replaced by ACS. The chief instructor says "We'll update at the next curriculum review." What is your responsibility?

A) Follow the school's timeline since the chief instructor has authority over curriculum
B) Teach from the current ACS standards regardless of what the school's materials say, and flag the discrepancy to the chief instructor as requiring immediate attention
C) The difference between PTS and ACS is minor and doesn't affect instruction
D) Wait for the FAA to notify the school of the discrepancy

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** ACS replaced PTS as the airman certification standard. Teaching from outdated standards means students are being prepared for the wrong evaluation criteria. While the Part 141 chief instructor has curriculum authority, the individual CFI has a responsibility to teach accurate, current material. Flagging the discrepancy with urgency is appropriate because the FAA expects immediate updates when standards change. Option A accepts teaching incorrect standards. Option C is wrong -- ACS fundamentally changed the evaluation framework. Option D assumes FAA oversight that may not occur in time.

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-4

The FAA publishes a Safety Alert for Operators (SAFO) that changes recommended practices for a maneuver you teach regularly. A colleague argues that SAFOs are advisory, not mandatory, so updating lesson plans is optional. What is the correct perspective?

B) While SAFOs are technically advisory, a professional CFI should review and incorporate relevant safety guidance into their instruction, because the standard of care expected of an instructor includes staying current with best practices -- not just minimum regulatory compliance
C) SAFOs only apply to Part 121 operators, not Part 61 instructors
D) The FAA will issue a violation if you don't comply with every SAFO within 30 days

Teaching outdated techniques when better guidance exists falls below the professional standard, even if the old technique isn't technically illegal. Option A takes a legalistic view that ignores professional responsibility. Option C is incorrect. Option D overstates the enforcement mechanism.

### Q-A8-06 (Module 1, Pool A8-M1-03)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RC-4

AC 61-83K describes why staying current with regulatory changes is challenging for flight instructors. Which of the following accurately reflect the AC's reasoning about this challenge? (Select all that apply)

A) Part-time weekend instructors with other jobs may struggle to track regulatory changes
B) Very busy full-time instructors at high-activity flight schools face the same challenge
C) Effective recurrent flight instructor training directly relates to the quality of flight instruction and the furtherance of flight safety
D) The FAA considers regulatory currency optional for instructors who only teach private pilot students
E) The FIRC offers an ideal opportunity for instructors to learn about recent regulatory and policy changes

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.8, the AC explicitly acknowledges that staying current is difficult for both part-time weekend instructors (A) and busy full-time instructors (B). It states that "effective recurrent flight instructor training directly relates to the quality of flight instruction provided to pilots and the furtherance of flight safety" (C), and that "the FIRC offers an ideal opportunity" to learn about changes (E). Option D is incorrect -- the AC makes no distinction based on what certificate a CFI's students are pursuing. All instructors share this obligation.

### Q-A8-07 (Module 1, Pool A8-M1-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-4, CJ-2

You are a FIRC provider and the FAA publishes a revised Advisory Circular that changes guidance on spin training for CFI applicants. Your current FIRC materials reference the old AC. What does AC 61-83K require you to do?

A) Wait for the FAA to notify you that an update is needed, then revise your materials
B) Update your presentation materials immediately to reflect the change -- no prior FAA approval is needed
C) Submit the proposed changes to the FAA for review before incorporating them into your course
D) Note the change in a supplemental handout and update the main materials at the next scheduled revision cycle

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.8 states that the FAA expects FIRC providers to "immediately update their presentation materials to reflect relevant changes." It further states that providers "may make such changes without prior approval at any time, provided changes accurately reflect the current regulatory or policy state." Option A waits for external prompting that may never come. Option C adds an approval step the AC explicitly waives. Option D delays the update unnecessarily.

Option A treats the FIRC as a one-time compliance event. Option C shifts responsibility to the provider. Option D defers a professional obligation for nearly two years.

AC 61-83K A.8 describes the relationship between regulatory currency and flight safety. Which of the following statements are supported by the AC? (Select all that apply)

D) The quality of flight instruction is directly tied to the furtherance of flight safety
