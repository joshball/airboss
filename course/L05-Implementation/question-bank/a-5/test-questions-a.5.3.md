### Q-A5-37 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, OD-1

What international organization does the FAA partner with to promote helicopter safety and reduce civil helicopter accidents worldwide?

A) The International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO)
B) The International Helicopter Safety Team (IHST)
C) The European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA)
D) The Helicopter Association International (HAI)

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K identifies the International Helicopter Safety Team (IHST) as the organization the FAA partners with to promote safety and reduce civil helicopter accidents and fatalities worldwide. While ICAO (A), EASA (C), and HAI (D) all play roles in aviation safety, the IHST is the specific international partnership referenced in this context. CFIs who instruct in rotorcraft should be aware of this partnership and the safety resources it produces.

### Q-A5-38 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-1, RM-1

What is the domestic counterpart to the IHST that focuses specifically on U.S. helicopter safety?

A) The FAA Rotorcraft Directorate
B) The U.S. Helicopter Safety Team (USHST)
C) The NTSB Rotorcraft Division
D) The National EMS Pilots Association (NEMSPA)

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K identifies the U.S. Helicopter Safety Team (USHST) as the domestic partner organization working alongside the IHST to reduce helicopter accidents and fatalities. The USHST focuses on U.S.-specific rotorcraft safety data and interventions. The FAA Rotorcraft Directorate (A) handles certification, the NTSB (C) investigates accidents, and NEMSPA (D) represents a specific segment of rotorcraft operations. CFIs should understand that both the IHST and USHST inform best practices for rotorcraft training.

### Q-A5-39 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-05)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, RM-2

You are a CFI-H (helicopter) preparing a student for a commercial rotorcraft checkride. The student consistently performs autorotations to the same field at the same altitude. When you suggest varying the entry altitude and location, the student says "I have the procedure down perfectly for this spot." How does this training approach relate to rotorcraft safety goals?

A) Consistent practice at the same location builds strong muscle memory and is preferred
B) Training autorotations only in a predictable environment fails to prepare the student for the unpredictable conditions of a real engine failure, which is a key factor in rotorcraft accidents that the IHST and USHST are working to reduce
C) Varying practice locations is only necessary for instrument-rated helicopter pilots
D) The student's proficiency at one location demonstrates adequate skill

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** The IHST and USHST work to reduce helicopter accidents and fatalities, and a significant portion of rotorcraft accidents involve loss of control during emergency procedures. A student who can only perform autorotations in a single, practiced scenario lacks the adaptability needed when an engine failure occurs at an unpredicted moment, altitude, or location. The CFI's role is to build robust skill that transfers to novel situations, not rehearsed performance in familiar conditions. This aligns with the broader IHST/USHST goal of reducing accidents through improved training quality.

### Q-A5-40 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-05)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** PS-2, RM-1

According to AC 61-83K, what are the stated objectives of the FAA's partnership with the IHST and USHST? (Select all that apply)

A) Promote helicopter safety
B) Reduce civil helicopter accidents
C) Eliminate all rotorcraft training requirements
D) Reduce helicopter fatalities worldwide
E) Establish mandatory helicopter safety equipment standards

**Correct:** A, B, D
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K states that the FAA partners with the IHST and USHST to promote safety, reduce civil helicopter accidents, and reduce fatalities worldwide. The partnership does not seek to eliminate training requirements (C) or establish equipment standards (E) -- those are regulatory functions. The three objectives -- promote safety, reduce accidents, reduce fatalities -- form a layered approach: awareness leads to prevention, prevention reduces accidents, and better training and equipment reduce fatalities when accidents do occur.

### Q-A5-41 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-06)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-1, RM-3

A helicopter CFI notices that several students struggle with hovering in crosswind conditions, often over-controlling the cyclic and creating a dynamic rollover risk. The USHST has published data showing dynamic rollover as a persistent accident category. How should the CFI apply this information?

A) Avoid crosswind hovering until students have 50+ hours of rotorcraft time
B) Develop a progressive crosswind hovering training sequence that starts with light crosswinds and gradually increases, with specific emphasis on recognizing the early signs of dynamic rollover and the correct response -- reducing collective, not fighting it with cyclic
C) Assign the students to read about dynamic rollover in the rotorcraft flying handbook
D) Practice dynamic rollover recovery at altitude first

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** USHST data identifying dynamic rollover as a persistent accident category means CFIs must train for it proactively and progressively. Avoidance (A) creates the skill gap the data warns about. Reading (C) provides knowledge but not the psychomotor skill or judgment needed in the moment. Dynamic rollover cannot be safely practiced as a recovery at altitude (D) -- the training must focus on recognition and prevention in the ground-effect environment where it occurs. A progressive sequence builds competence while managing risk, directly supporting the USHST's accident reduction objectives.

### Q-A5-42 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-06)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RM-2, OD-2

Why does AC 61-83K specifically address helicopter safety as a separate sub-section within GA safety trends, rather than including rotorcraft accidents in the general GA statistics?

A) Helicopter accidents are investigated by a different agency than fixed-wing accidents
B) Rotorcraft operations involve unique aerodynamic, operational, and training considerations that produce distinct accident patterns requiring specialized safety strategies -- as evidenced by the existence of dedicated organizations like the IHST and USHST
C) Helicopter accident rates are lower than fixed-wing rates and do not need the same emphasis
D) The FAA regulates helicopters under different regulations than fixed-wing aircraft

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Helicopters face unique challenges -- autorotation versus glide, dynamic rollover, settling with power, confined area operations, external load work, and low-altitude missions by design. These produce accident patterns distinct from fixed-wing operations that require specialized analysis and intervention. The existence of dedicated organizations (IHST, USHST) reflects this need for specialized attention. Both fixed-wing and rotorcraft accidents are investigated by the NTSB (A). Helicopter accident rates are not lower (C). While some regulatory differences exist, both operate primarily under Part 91 for GA operations (D).

### Q-A5-43 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-07)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, PS-1

A helicopter student is preparing for a flight to a remote landing zone for a mountain training exercise. The USHST has published safety guidance emphasizing the risk of wire strikes during low-altitude operations. The student's preflight planning includes a sectional chart review but no reconnaissance of the specific landing area. What should the CFI address?

A) The sectional chart is sufficient for identifying all obstacles
B) Wire strikes are a documented cause of helicopter accidents, and wires are frequently unmarked and invisible on charts -- the student should be taught to assume wires are present near any road, building, or utility corridor and to plan approach and departure paths that minimize exposure to wire-strike zones
C) Wire strike awareness is only important for EMS and utility helicopter operations
D) The student should call the property owner to ask about wires

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Wire strikes are a persistent category in rotorcraft accident data, and the USHST has specifically addressed this risk. Wires are frequently unmarked, difficult to see, and not depicted on charts. Teaching the student to assume wires exist near any human development and to plan approaches accordingly addresses the root cause of these accidents -- the assumption that if wires aren't visible or charted, they aren't there. This proactive hazard identification is the CFI's direct contribution to the USHST's accident reduction goals. Charts alone (A) are insufficient, and wire risk applies to all low-altitude rotorcraft operations (C), not just specialized missions.

### Q-A5-44 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-07)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RM-1, RM-3

The IHST operates at the international level while the USHST focuses on domestic helicopter safety. Why is this two-tiered structure relevant to CFIs who instruct in rotorcraft? (Select all that apply)

A) International data from the IHST reveals accident patterns that may not yet be apparent in U.S. data alone, providing early warning of emerging risks
B) USHST domestic data reflects the specific regulatory, environmental, and operational conditions U.S. helicopter pilots encounter
C) CFIs are required to report directly to both the IHST and USHST
D) Safety strategies developed from a larger international dataset may be more robust than those based on domestic data alone
E) CFIs must be certified by the USHST to instruct in helicopters

**Correct:** A, B, D
**Explanation:** The two-tiered structure provides complementary perspectives. International data (A) may reveal patterns earlier due to larger sample sizes. Domestic data (B) reflects U.S.-specific conditions. Combined, they produce more robust safety strategies (D). CFIs do not report to these organizations (C) and are not certified by them (E). The practical value for CFIs is that safety recommendations from these organizations are informed by comprehensive data analysis across multiple operational environments, making them more reliable than anecdotal experience.

### Q-A5-45 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-08)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-1, RM-2

A CFI-H is reviewing a helicopter student's log of recent practice. The student has been renting a helicopter and practicing solo -- primarily hover work and pattern flying at the home airport. The student has not practiced any emergency procedures, off-airport landings, or cross-country operations since the last dual lesson three weeks ago. How does this relate to rotorcraft safety trends?

A) Solo practice at the home airport is the safest way for students to build hours
B) The student's practice pattern is developing proficiency in routine operations while creating a growing gap in the high-risk skill areas that rotorcraft accident data identifies as most dangerous -- confined areas, emergency procedures, and operations in unfamiliar environments
C) Students should not be allowed to solo practice in helicopters
D) The student is making good progress by focusing on the fundamentals

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Rotorcraft accident data from the IHST and USHST shows that accidents cluster in non-routine operations: confined areas, off-airport landings, emergency procedures, and operations in unfamiliar conditions. A student who only practices routine pattern work is building a false sense of proficiency that does not extend to the situations where accidents actually occur. The CFI's role is to structure training so that high-risk skill areas receive proportional attention, ensuring the student's capability matches the actual risk profile of helicopter operations.

### Q-A5-46 (Module 3, Pool A5-M3-08)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, OD-1

What distinguishes the IHST/USHST approach to rotorcraft safety from general aviation safety efforts like the GAJSC?

A) There is no meaningful difference -- they use identical methods
B) The IHST/USHST specifically addresses the unique operational and aerodynamic characteristics of rotorcraft that produce distinct accident patterns, requiring specialized data analysis and tailored safety interventions rather than adapting fixed-wing safety strategies
C) The IHST has more authority than the GAJSC
D) The USHST focuses only on commercial helicopter operations

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** While both the GAJSC and IHST/USHST use data-driven approaches, the rotorcraft organizations specifically address the aerodynamic, operational, and mission characteristics unique to helicopters. Autorotation, dynamic rollover, settling with power, and low-altitude mission profiles produce accident patterns that require specialized analysis. Safety interventions designed for fixed-wing operations may not translate effectively to rotorcraft. The IHST and USHST ensure that helicopter-specific risks receive focused attention rather than being diluted in broader GA statistics. Neither organization has regulatory authority (C), and the USHST addresses all civil helicopter operations (D).
