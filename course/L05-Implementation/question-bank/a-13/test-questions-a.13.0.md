### Q-A13-01 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-01)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3

A person comes to you wanting to apply for a student pilot certificate. They present a paper application (FAA Form 8710-1). You notice the medical certificate section is blank. What should you do?

A) Accept the application -- the medical certificate is obtained separately and isn't required at the time of application
B) Reject the application until the individual obtains a medical certificate
C) Accept the application and remind the applicant that they must hold at least a third-class medical (or BasicMed) before solo flight, but the medical is not required at the time of application
D) Fill in the medical certificate information yourself based on what the applicant tells you

**Correct:** C
**Explanation:** A student pilot certificate can be applied for before obtaining a medical certificate. However, the student cannot exercise solo flight privileges until they hold the appropriate medical (or BasicMed) authorization. The CFI should accept the application and educate the student about the medical requirement and its timing. Option A omits important guidance. Option B incorrectly delays the application process. Option D falsifies an official document.

### Q-A13-02 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-01)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3

A student pilot certificate applicant brings their application through IACRA. As the recommending instructor, what must you verify?

A) Only that the applicant has a valid photo ID
B) The applicant's identity, that the application information is accurate, and that you have verified the applicant's citizenship or TSA eligibility
C) Only that the applicant can read and speak English
D) Nothing -- IACRA verifies everything electronically

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** The recommending instructor on an IACRA application has specific verification responsibilities: confirming the applicant's identity (typically through government-issued photo ID), verifying the accuracy of application information, and confirming citizenship status or TSA eligibility for flight training. IACRA is a processing system, not a verification system -- the instructor provides the in-person verification that the electronic system cannot. Option A is incomplete. Option C is only one element. Option D misunderstands the CFI's role.

### Q-A13-03 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-02)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3

A person wants to apply for a remote pilot certificate (Part 107). They have never held any FAA certificate. What is the application path?

A) They must first obtain a student pilot certificate, then apply for the remote pilot certificate
B) They must pass the initial Part 107 knowledge test (UAG) at an FAA-approved testing center, then apply for the certificate through IACRA
C) A flight instructor can issue a remote pilot certificate directly
D) Remote pilot certificates are issued by the TSA, not the FAA

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** For initial Part 107 remote pilot certification, the applicant must pass the UAG (Unmanned Aircraft General) knowledge test at an FAA-approved testing center, then apply through IACRA. No prior FAA certificates are required, and no flight instructor endorsement is needed for the initial certificate. Option A confuses manned and unmanned certificate paths. Option C overstates CFI authority. Option D is incorrect -- the FAA issues Part 107 certificates.

### Q-A13-04 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-02)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3

A current Part 61 private pilot wants to add a remote pilot certificate. They ask if they need to take the full Part 107 knowledge test. What do you tell them?

A) Yes, all remote pilot applicants must take the full UAG knowledge test
B) No -- holders of a current Part 61 certificate (other than student pilot) may complete an initial online training course instead of the full knowledge test, provided they have a current flight review
C) No -- Part 61 certificate holders are automatically qualified for Part 107 operations
D) Yes, but they can take a shortened version of the test

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 107.63, a person who holds a Part 61 certificate (other than student pilot) and has completed a flight review within the preceding 24 months may complete an initial online training course (Part 107 small UAS recurrent training) instead of taking the full UAG knowledge test. This recognizes that manned aircraft pilots already have relevant aeronautical knowledge. Option A doesn't account for the Part 61 pilot pathway. Option C overstates the exemption. Option D is not the correct mechanism.

### Q-A13-05 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-03)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RC-3

Which of the following are responsibilities of a flight instructor when accepting a student pilot certificate application? (Select all that apply)

A) Verifying the applicant's identity through government-issued photo identification
B) Conducting a background check on the applicant
C) Ensuring the application is complete and accurate before submitting
D) Verifying the applicant meets the age requirement (16 for airplane, 14 for glider/balloon)
E) Providing initial flight training before the application can be submitted

**Correct:** A, C, D
**Explanation:** The CFI's responsibilities when accepting a student pilot application include identity verification (A), ensuring completeness and accuracy (C), and verifying age requirements per 14 CFR 61.83 (D). Background checks (B) are not a CFI responsibility -- TSA handles security vetting for applicable individuals. Initial flight training (E) is not required before applying for a student pilot certificate.

### Q-A13-06 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3, AV-2

A CFI colleague who has been out of instruction since 2014 asks you what changed about student pilot certificates. Which statement best describes the April 1, 2016 change?

A) Student pilot certificates are now issued only by FSDOs and can no longer be endorsed by flight instructors
B) Student pilot certificates changed from a paper certificate issued by CFIs to a plastic certificate applied for through a FSDO, DPE, ACR, or flight instructor, processed via IACRA
C) Student pilot certificates are now combined with the third-class medical certificate into a single document
D) Student pilot certificates are no longer required -- students can begin training with only a medical certificate

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Effective April 1, 2016, the FAA changed student pilot certificates from paper certificates to durable plastic cards. Applications are submitted through IACRA and accepted by a FSDO, DPE, Airman Certification Representative (ACR), or certificated flight instructor. The certificate is then issued by the FAA as a standalone plastic card. This replaced the old system where a CFI could issue a combined student pilot certificate/medical on the spot.

### Q-A13-07 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-04)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3, CJ-2

A prospective student walks into your flight school and wants to start training immediately. Under the post-April 2016 rules, which of the following is true about when they can begin flight training?

A) They cannot receive any flight training until the plastic student pilot certificate arrives in the mail
B) They can begin dual instruction immediately but cannot solo until they hold a valid student pilot certificate and appropriate medical
C) They must wait for both the student pilot certificate and medical certificate before any flight training can occur
D) They can solo immediately as long as the IACRA application has been submitted

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** A student pilot certificate is not required to receive dual flight instruction -- it is required to exercise solo flight privileges. A student can begin training with an instructor before the plastic certificate is issued. However, before solo flight, the student must hold a valid student pilot certificate, an appropriate medical certificate (or BasicMed), and the required CFI endorsements. Per 14 CFR 61.87, solo flight requires all three elements.

### Q-A13-08 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-05)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, RC-3

You are preparing to endorse a student for their first solo flight. They have their plastic student pilot certificate and a current third-class medical. Per 14 CFR 61.87, which additional prerequisites must the student have met before you can endorse them for solo?

A) Pass a pre-solo written exam and receive training in all required maneuvers and procedures listed in 61.87
B) Log at least 20 hours of dual instruction
C) Pass the pre-solo written exam only -- flight proficiency is at the instructor's discretion
D) Hold a valid student pilot certificate for at least 90 days

**Correct:** A
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 61.87, before a student pilot can solo, the CFI must have given the student training in the applicable maneuvers and procedures listed in that section, and the student must have passed a pre-solo knowledge test (written exam) administered by the instructor covering the applicable areas. There is no minimum dual time requirement before first solo (B is incorrect), and proficiency in the specific regulatory maneuvers is required -- not just instructor discretion (C is incorrect). There is no waiting period for the certificate (D is incorrect).

### Q-A13-09 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-05)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RC-3, CJ-1

A student is ready for their first solo. Which of the following must be completed before you can endorse them? (Select all that apply)

A) The student must hold a valid student pilot certificate
B) The student must hold an appropriate medical certificate or BasicMed
C) You must administer and grade a pre-solo knowledge test covering the applicable regulations and procedures
D) The student must have logged at least 10 hours of flight training
E) You must have given flight training in the maneuvers and procedures required by 14 CFR 61.87
F) The student must have passed an FAA-administered practical test

**Correct:** A, B, C, E
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 61.87, solo flight requires: a valid student pilot certificate (A), appropriate medical authorization (B), a pre-solo knowledge test administered by the instructor (C), and flight training in the maneuvers and procedures listed in 61.87 (E). There is no minimum hour requirement before first solo (D is incorrect). The FAA practical test (F) is for certificate issuance, not solo endorsement.

### Q-A13-10 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-06)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3, AV-1

A 15-year-old comes to your flight school wanting to learn to fly airplanes and eventually solo. What do you tell them about the age requirement for a student pilot certificate?

A) They must be 17 years old to apply for a student pilot certificate for airplane
B) They must be 16 years old to apply for a student pilot certificate for airplane, but they can begin dual instruction now
C) They can apply for a student pilot certificate at any age
D) They must be 14 years old to apply for a student pilot certificate for airplane

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 61.83, the minimum age to apply for a student pilot certificate is 16 years for airplane, helicopter, powered-lift, or airship, and 14 years for glider or balloon. However, there is no minimum age to receive dual flight instruction. The 15-year-old can start training now but must wait until age 16 to obtain the student pilot certificate needed for solo flight. Option A confuses the student pilot age (16) with the private pilot age (17). Option D applies to glider/balloon only.

### Q-A13-11 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-06)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3, AV-2

A student pilot applicant can read English but speaks with a heavy accent that makes some aviation terminology difficult to understand on the radio. As the recommending CFI on their IACRA application, how should you handle the English proficiency requirement?

A) Deny the application -- applicants must speak English without any accent
B) Approve the application if the applicant can read, speak, write, and understand the English language; an accent alone does not constitute a failure to meet the requirement
C) Approve the application but add a limitation restricting the student to non-towered airports only
D) Refer the applicant to the FSDO for an English proficiency evaluation before processing

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 61.83(c), a student pilot applicant must be able to read, speak, write, and understand the English language. An accent does not disqualify an applicant -- the standard is functional ability to communicate, not accent-free speech. If the applicant can understand and be understood in aviation contexts, the requirement is met. The CFI should assess whether the applicant can effectively communicate with ATC and other pilots. Option A applies an incorrect standard. Option C invents a limitation not provided for in the regulations. Option D is unnecessary if the CFI can make the determination.

### Q-A13-12 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-07)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-3, AV-2

A Part 107 remote pilot certificate holder asks you, a CFI, to provide their recurrent training so they can maintain their certificate privileges. What is the correct guidance?

A) As a CFI, you can administer the Part 107 recurrent knowledge test at your flight school
B) Part 107 recurrent training is completed online through the FAA Safety Team (FAASTeam) website -- no CFI involvement is required
C) Remote pilot certificates do not expire and require no recurrent training
D) You must provide at least 3 hours of ground instruction covering Part 107 topics before they can renew

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 107.65, remote pilot certificate holders must complete recurrent training every 24 calendar months to maintain their small UAS privileges. This training is completed online through the FAA Safety Team (FAASTeam) website as a free course. No CFI is involved in the process -- it is entirely self-service. Option A confuses CFI authority with FAA testing center authority. Option C is incorrect -- recurrent training is required. Option D invents a ground instruction requirement that does not exist for Part 107.

### Q-A13-13 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-07)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, RC-3

A Part 61 private pilot with a current flight review completed the online Part 107 initial training and received their remote pilot certificate 26 months ago. They have not completed any Part 107 recurrent training. What is their current status?

A) Their remote pilot certificate is revoked and they must retake the full UAG knowledge test
B) Their remote pilot certificate remains valid but they cannot exercise small UAS privileges until they complete Part 107 recurrent training
C) They are still current because the Part 61 flight review covers Part 107 recurrency
D) Their remote pilot certificate expired and they must reapply through IACRA

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 107.65, a remote pilot must complete recurrent training or a recurrent knowledge test every 24 calendar months. After 26 months without recurrent training, the certificate itself remains valid (it does not expire or get revoked), but the pilot cannot exercise small UAS privileges until the recurrent requirement is satisfied. A Part 61 flight review does not satisfy Part 107 recurrent requirements (C is incorrect). The certificate does not expire (D) or get revoked (A) -- only the privilege to operate is suspended until recurrency is completed.

### Q-A13-14 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-08)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** RC-3, AV-2

Under 14 CFR Part 107, which of the following are true about remote pilot certificate requirements? (Select all that apply)

A) Applicants must be at least 16 years old
B) Applicants must pass a TSA security background check
C) Applicants must hold at least a third-class medical certificate
D) Applicants must be able to read, speak, write, and understand the English language
E) Applicants must demonstrate in-flight proficiency with a small UAS during a practical test
F) The initial knowledge test covers airspace classification, weather, loading, and emergency procedures

**Correct:** A, B, D, F
**Explanation:** Per 14 CFR 107.61, remote pilot applicants must be at least 16 years old (A), must pass a TSA background check (B), and must be able to read, speak, write, and understand English (D). The initial knowledge test (UAG) covers airspace, weather, loading/performance, emergency procedures, and other aeronautical knowledge areas (F). A medical certificate is not required for Part 107 operations (C) -- Part 107 has a self-certification provision requiring only that the pilot be in a condition safe for flight. There is no practical flight test for remote pilot certification (E).

### Q-A13-15 (Module 4, Pool A13-M4-08)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-1, RC-3

A person who holds both a Part 61 private pilot certificate and a Part 107 remote pilot certificate asks whether their next Part 61 flight review will also satisfy the Part 107 recurrent training requirement. What is the correct answer?

A) Yes -- a Part 61 flight review satisfies all FAA recurrency requirements including Part 107
B) No -- Part 107 recurrent training and Part 61 flight review are separate requirements that must each be completed independently
C) Yes, but only if the flight review includes at least 1 hour of ground instruction covering Part 107 topics
D) It depends on whether the reviewing instructor also holds a Part 107 remote pilot certificate

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Part 107 recurrent training (14 CFR 107.65) and the Part 61 flight review (14 CFR 61.56) are entirely separate regulatory requirements. Each must be satisfied independently on its own terms and timeline. The flight review does not include Part 107 content and does not satisfy Part 107 recurrency, regardless of what the instructor covers or holds. The pilot must complete the online Part 107 recurrent training through the FAASTeam website separately from their flight review.
