# Part 141 as a CFI -- the chief structure, stage checks, enrollment

Lesson 01 was the structure. This lesson is the daily life. If you teach at a 141 school, these are the rules you'll cross every shift. If you teach at a 61 school and a 141 school down the field offers you a job, this is what changes.

## What you'll be able to do

- Name the chief instructor / assistant chief / check instructor structure and which sections of 141 govern each
- Locate the enrollment rule ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/55?at=2026)) and the enrollment-certificate rule ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026))
- State who can give a stage check at your school and who cannot
- Predict how recordkeeping at a 141 school differs from a 61 school

## Why this matters

A 61 instructor's regulatory life is *Part 61 plus Part 91*. A 141 instructor's regulatory life is *Part 61 plus Part 91 plus the school's certificate plus the TCO plus the chief structure plus 141.93 enrollment paperwork*. The volume of regulation a 141 instructor lives inside is considerably larger. Most of it is hidden by the school's standard operating procedures (the TCO references the regulation; the syllabus references the TCO; the lesson plan references the syllabus), but when something goes wrong the chain back to the regulation is what saves the day.

## The discovery question

You've been hired as a CFI at a 141 school. Day one. Your student finishes a lesson and hands you their training folder for an entry. You sign it as you would at any 61 school: `Dual instruction received -- 1.2 hours, traffic pattern operations, S/E. /s/ J. Ball CFI 1234567`.

Your chief instructor walks over and tells you the entry is not enough. What did you miss?

Pause.

At a 141 school, every dual entry must reference the *syllabus lesson* it covered, must indicate whether the lesson is satisfactorily completed, and must align with the stage the student is in. The school's TCO is the contract; your logbook entry is the audit trail showing the contract was performed. A 61-style entry doesn't satisfy [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/95?at=2026), the recordkeeping rule.

This is the kind of friction a new 141 instructor hits constantly. The regulations don't change much from a 61 school -- the *recordkeeping discipline* changes a lot.

## The chief instructor structure

Every 141 school has a *chief instructor* for every course it teaches. Some courses share a chief; some have their own. The chief is the person whose name is on the school's certificate as the authority for that course's curriculum.

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/35?at=2026) is the chief instructor section. It has three big requirements:

1. **Eligibility.** The chief must hold the certificate level the course teaches (a private chief must be at least a CFI-A; an instrument chief must be a CFII; a commercial chief must be a CFI-A with commercial privileges; etc.). Plus minimum experience: typically 2,000 hours total time, of which a substantial portion as PIC, plus instructor experience.
2. **Continuous duty.** The chief is responsible for the day-to-day curriculum, the TCO compliance, the certification of graduates, and the supervision of the instructional staff. This is a full duty, not an absentee role.
3. **Stage-check authority.** The chief either gives stage checks personally or designates check instructors who do.

For larger schools, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/36?at=2026) defines the *assistant chief instructor* role -- a deputy who can act in the chief's absence -- and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/37?at=2026) defines the *check instructor* role, the staff member designated by the chief to give stage checks.

```text
Chief instructor             (141.35)
  |
  +-- Assistant chief        (141.36)  -- larger schools, can act for the chief
  |
  +-- Check instructor       (141.37)  -- gives stage checks; not the student's primary CFI
  |
  +-- Line CFIs              (no 141 section -- they teach the syllabus, no special role)
```

The structural insight: a *line CFI* at a 141 school is an ordinary CFI who happens to be teaching off a TCO. The school's special structure lives above them in the chief / assistant / check-instructor layer.

## Stage checks

Stage checks are the heart of what makes Part 141 different from Part 61. A stage check is an *internal-to-the-school* practical test, given at the end of each stage (typically 3 stages for a private course), by an instructor who is *not* the student's primary instructor.

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/63?at=2026) governs the structure: the stage check tests the maneuvers and ground knowledge required for that stage of the TCO. A student who fails must remediate and re-check before progressing. A student who passes is signed off by the check instructor and can move into the next stage.

The pedagogical purpose: a *second pair of eyes* on the student's progress, calibrated against the school's standard rather than the primary instructor's habits. This catches "my CFI says I'm fine but actually I'm dangerous" cases that otherwise wouldn't surface until the FAA practical test.

Three rules to remember about stage checks:

1. **Not your students.** A line CFI cannot give a stage check to their own student. The whole point is the second pair of eyes.
2. **Check instructor authority.** Only check instructors (designated by the chief under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/37?at=2026)) or the chief themselves can give stage checks. A line CFI cannot decide to give a stage check just because they're available.
3. **End-of-course test.** The end-of-course test ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/65?at=2026)) is the school's internal final. A student who passes the end-of-course test receives a graduation certificate, which is what [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/71?at=2026) accepts in lieu of full Part 61 hour minimums when they sit the FAA practical test with a DPE.

## Enrollment -- 141.55 and 141.93

Two sections cover the paperwork that *creates* a 141 student.

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/55?at=2026) is the enrollment rule. It says that to enroll a student in a 141 course, the school must:

- Confirm the student meets the prerequisites for that course (medical, age, knowledge-test prereq if any)
- Issue an enrollment certificate per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026)
- Place the student in a specific TCO and stage

A 141 student is not just "a student at the school." They are *enrolled in a specific course at a specific stage*. If they want to switch from Appendix B (private) to Appendix M (combined private + instrument), the school re-enrolls them with a new enrollment certificate.

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026) is the enrollment certificate rule. The certificate must contain:

- Student's name and identifying info
- The course they're enrolled in (by appendix and approved-course number)
- The date of enrollment
- A reference to the TCO
- The chief instructor's signature

The student keeps a copy. The school keeps a copy. If the FAA shows up, both copies should match.

## Recordkeeping -- 141.95

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/95?at=2026) is the recordkeeping rule. The school must maintain, for at least one year after a student finishes:

- The enrollment certificate
- The student's training records, including each lesson, the syllabus lesson it satisfied, and whether the lesson was satisfactorily completed
- Stage-check records
- The end-of-course test record
- The graduation certificate

The student's logbook is *not* the official record at a 141 school. The school's training file is. A logbook entry is still required (under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/51?at=2026), Part 61), but the *audit trail* the FAA looks at lives in the school's file. This is why every dual entry at a 141 school cross-references the syllabus lesson.

## Who can sign what

The most-asked question for a new 141 instructor: who can sign which endorsement?

| Endorsement                                                | Who signs                                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Solo endorsement ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026)) | Any CFI -- this is a Part 61 endorsement, not a 141 endorsement                  |
| Solo cross-country endorsement ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/93?at=2026)) | Any CFI -- still Part 61                                            |
| Stage check sign-off                                       | Check instructor or chief, never the student's primary CFI ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/63?at=2026)) |
| End-of-course test sign-off                                | Check instructor or chief ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/65?at=2026))              |
| Graduation certificate                                     | Chief instructor only ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/95?at=2026))                  |
| Practical-test endorsement ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/39?at=2026)) | Any CFI -- still Part 61, but at a 141 school the chief usually writes it |

The pattern: Part 61 endorsements are signed by line CFIs as usual. Part 141 sign-offs (stage check, end-of-course, graduation certificate) escalate up the chief structure.

## Where Part 141 differs operationally from Part 61

A side-by-side for a CFI considering a move:

| Area                | Part 61 school                              | Part 141 school                                                                |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Curriculum          | CFI's choice; AC 61-65 endorsement language | Approved TCO; line CFI follows the syllabus                                    |
| Lesson order        | Flexible; instructor's call                 | Stage-bound; the syllabus dictates                                             |
| Stage checks        | None                                        | Required at end of each stage (typically 3); given by check instructor or chief |
| Hour minimum        | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/109?at=2026) etc. (40 private) | Appendix B etc. (35 private)                            |
| Logbook entry style | Free-form                                   | Must reference syllabus lesson and stage                                       |
| Records             | Mostly student's logbook                    | School's training file, retained per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/95?at=2026) |
| Graduation          | None (student presents endorsements + hours)| Graduation certificate from chief                                              |
| FAA visibility      | Low (school not certificated)               | Higher (school is certificated, FSDO oversight)                                |

## A line CFI's typical week at a 141 school

Concrete: what a Tuesday morning looks like at a 141 school for a line CFI with three students.

```text
0700  Open the school's training file system. Pull each student's
      training folder. Confirm each student is enrolled in the right
      course (Appendix B for two of them, Appendix M for the third)
      and note their current stage and last completed lesson.

0730  First student. Pre-flight brief references the syllabus lesson
      number (e.g. "B-1.7 Stalls and Slow Flight"). The brief covers
      the maneuvers in the syllabus, the standards, and the
      completion criteria. Fly the lesson. Debrief.

0900  Logbook + training file entry. Logbook entry references the
      syllabus lesson. Training file entry repeats it with the
      satisfactory / unsatisfactory marking. Stage progress updated.

0930  Second student. Same shape. This student is one lesson away
      from a stage check. Brief that the stage check will be given
      by check instructor X next week.

1130  Third student. Same shape. They're in Appendix M (combined
      private + instrument) so the syllabus is different and the
      progression is different.

1300  Lunch. Read the school memo from the chief about an FSDO
      audit next month -- training files will be reviewed for TCO
      compliance, enrollment certificate currency, recordkeeping
      under 141.95.

1400  Ground school -- two students, one hour each. Same syllabus-
      reference discipline applies to ground.

1700  Close the day. Each student's training folder updated. Any
      stage check or enrollment-certificate questions go to the
      chief.
```

The friction points a 61-school CFI feels in their first week at a 141 school:

- The syllabus reference. Every lesson, every entry. No more "today we worked on landings."
- The stage discipline. You can't skip ahead, even if the student is ready, because the TCO requires the lesson order.
- The training file. Not just the logbook -- the school's file is the audit record.
- The chief structure. Questions you used to answer for yourself ("can my student do X?") may need to escalate.

## Common misreadings

- **"As a line CFI at a 141 school, I can give my own students stage checks."** Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/63?at=2026) requires that stage checks be given by an instructor *other* than the student's primary instructor, designated as a check instructor by the chief.
- **"The chief instructor has to give every stage check personally."** Wrong. The chief designates check instructors under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/37?at=2026); they give the checks. The chief signs the *graduation* certificate personally.
- **"A 141 enrollment certificate is the same as a student pilot certificate."** Wrong. A 141 enrollment certificate ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026)) is the school's record that this student is enrolled in this course. It has nothing to do with the airman certificate. The student still needs a Part 61 student pilot certificate ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/83?at=2026)) to solo.
- **"A 141 student can switch courses by just changing what they're learning."** Wrong. Switching from one approved course to another (e.g. from Appendix B to Appendix M) requires re-enrollment under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/55?at=2026), with a new enrollment certificate and TCO assignment. The school can't quietly migrate a student; it's a paperwork event.
- **"Part 141 doesn't apply to my CFI training."** It might. Many 141 schools hold an Appendix F (CFI) course rating. A CFI candidate trained under Appendix F gets the same hour-floor benefit (25 hours under 141 versus the Part 61 floor) -- but the CFI candidate must still meet [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/183?at=2026) eligibility on the airman side.
