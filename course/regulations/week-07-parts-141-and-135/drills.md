# Week 7 drills

Week 7 lessons walked Parts 141, 135, 119, 121, and 145. The drills exercise three skills:

1. **Classify** -- given an operational scenario, name the part(s) that govern
2. **Locate** -- given a question about an operations part, name the section in seconds
3. **Diagnose** -- given a transitioning pilot, identify what changes for them

These drills feed `apps/study/`. Format 1 -> classification cards. Format 2 -> navigation cards. Format 3 -> scenario cards.

## Format 1 -- Classify (which part?)

Cover the right column. For each scenario, name the *primary* commercial / training part that governs (Part 91 always applies on top; the question is what *adds* to 91).

| 10. Bonanza owner pays a friend "just gas money" to fly them to a meeting                       | If pro-rata cost-sharing under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026)(c) -> 91; if not -> illegal without 119 + 135 |

| 15. EMS helicopter on a hospital flight                                                         | 91 + 119 + 135 (HEMS subpart of 135)                                                                                         |
| 16. Drone delivery operation by Amazon                                                          | 91 + 135 (Part 135 cargo, with Part 107 not applicable to scheduled commercial)                                              |
| 17. CFI candidate doing 10 hr of solo CFI prep at a 141 school                                  | 91 + 61 + 141 Appendix F                                                                                                     |
| 18. Same candidate flying a discovery flight as PIC for a paying student-prospect               | 91 + 61 (this is instruction, not commercial transportation -- but compensation has rules)                                   |
| 19. Single-pilot Cessna 208 charter to take a businessman to a meeting, $1500 fee               | 91 + 119 + 135                                                                                                               |
| 20. Skydiving operation -- jump pilot flying jumpers in a 182                                   | 91 + 105 (parachute operations) -- not 135 if jumpers are in voluntary descent contracts; 135 in some configs                |

## Format 4 -- Trap detector

For each statement, identify the misreading and correct it.

| Statement                                                                         | Trap and correction                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Part 141 lowers the practical-test standard."                                    | Wrong. Same DPE, same standard. 141 lowers the *hour minimums*, not the test.                                                                                                                                                 |
| "If I have my commercial cert I can fly Part 135 tomorrow."                       | Wrong. Need [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/135/243?at=2026) hours, the operator's training, a competency check, and to be on the operator's ops specs.                                                                       |
| "I don't need to hold out to the public; private carriage is exempt."             | Wrong. Private carriage still requires a 119 cert per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/119/1?at=2026). Common-carriage holding-out is a separate test.                                                                         |
| "A line CFI at a 141 school can give my own student's stage check."               | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/63?at=2026) requires a different instructor (designated check instructor or chief).                                                                                                |
| "The chief instructor signs every solo endorsement."                              | Wrong. Solo endorsements ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026)) are Part 61, signed by any CFI. The chief signs the *graduation* certificate.                                                                       |
| "Part 145 governs all aircraft maintenance."                                      | Wrong. Part 145 governs *certificated repair stations*. Individual A&P/IA work falls under Part 43 + Part 65.                                                                                                                 |
| "All scheduled airline ops are Part 121."                                         | Mostly right. Some smaller scheduled commuters fall to 135 under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/119/21?at=2026) thresholds.                                                                                                  |
| "FAR 117 applies to my 135 charter operation."                                    | Wrong. FAR 117 is Part 117, applicable only to 121 air carriers. Part 135 rest rules live in 135 subpart F.                                                                                                                   |
| "A 141 enrollment certificate replaces the student pilot certificate."            | Wrong. The enrollment certificate ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026)) is the school's record. The student pilot certificate ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/83?at=2026)) is the airman cert. Both required. |
| "Cost-sharing under §61.113 covers paying the pilot a fee."                       | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026)(c) is *pro-rata operating expenses* (fuel, oil, airport fees, rental), with a common purpose. Pilot fees fall outside.                                                 |
| "NetJets is a Part 121 operation because they fly jets and look like an airline." | Wrong. Fractional operations are 91K plus often 135. Not 121.                                                                                                                                                                 |

## Hardcover drill -- 60 seconds, no notes

Integration. Read the prompt, give the answer in 60 seconds without referring to anything.

1. **Name the parts that govern "the operation" and what each does.** -> 119 (certifies operators); 121 (scheduled airline ops); 135 (on-demand / charter / smaller scheduled); 141 (pilot schools); 145 (repair stations).
2. **A 200 hr commercial pilot is offered $300 to fly an acquaintance to a wedding. What's the right answer?** -> Decline. The flight requires Part 135 conduct under a 119 certificate. Neither party has one. Cost-sharing under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) doesn't cover pilot fees. The right answer is "charter from a 135 operator."
3. **Why does a 141 school graduate a private pilot at 35 hours when a 61 school requires 40?** -> The TCO + stage checks substitute for hours. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/71?at=2026) is the bridge -- 141 graduates present a graduation certificate that the FAA accepts in lieu of full Part 61 hour minimums.
4. **Your student is leaving you for a 135 operator. Name three things that change for them tomorrow.** -> Second-class medical required. Hour minimums under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/135/243?at=2026) (1,200 IFR PIC). Competency check every 12 months under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/135/293?at=2026), plus the operator's training program and FAR 117-style duty/rest under 135 subpart F.
5. **What's in a Part 141 enrollment certificate, and why does it matter?** -> Per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/141/93?at=2026): student name, course (appendix + approved-course number), date, TCO reference, chief instructor signature. Matters because it's the record that this student is enrolled in this approved course; loss of the certificate or mismatch with school records is a 141 violation.

## Where this drill set fits

These drills are the connective tissue from Week 7 lessons to `apps/study/`. Format 1 (Classify) -> the high-yield "what part?" cards. Format 2 (Locate) -> navigation cards for the operations parts.
