# Week 3 -- Part 61 subpart H, the CFI

The CFI side of Part 61. You're becoming this. The regulation defines what you are authorized to do, what you are limited from doing, what training records you must keep, how you renew, and which endorsements your signature actually creates.

This week is deep. Subpart H is short by section count (about 20 sections) but every section in it is operationally load-bearing for a working CFI. Skim it and you walk into your initial CFI checkride with the wrong mental model of your own privileges; teach from the wrong understanding and your students inherit the same gap.

## What you'll be able to do by end of week

- Cite CFI eligibility (61.183), aeronautical experience (61.187), logbook records (61.189) without a section index
- Distinguish privileges (61.193) from limitations (61.195) and apply each one to a candidate-CFI scenario
- Walk through every CFI renewal pathway under 61.197 (FIRC, practical test, 80%-recommend record, designated examiner, military) and pick the right one for a real CFI's situation
- Find any endorsement in AC 61-65 by category (solo / cross-country / endorsement-for-checkride / additional-rating / FIRC-equivalent / 61.31) and apply the correct AC 61-65 paragraph language
- Diagnose where 61.193 ends and 61.113 begins (when you're being paid as a CFI vs when you're being paid as a pilot)

## How this week relates to the others

Subpart H sits *next to* the cert-level subparts (E, F, G) walked in Week 2. The structure:

- Week 2 taught the parallel anatomy of E (private), F (commercial), G (ATP)
- Week 3 (this week) zooms into H, which has its own anatomy: eligibility, aero experience, logbook records, privileges, limitations, renewal
- Subparts I (ground instructors), J (sport pilots), K (sport pilot CFIs) are named-and-located in this week's lesson 1, not deep-dived

Forward references: Week 4-5 (Part 91) is where your students operate. Week 9 (enforcement) is where 61.13 (refusal to submit to test for alcohol/drugs), 61.15 (offenses), 61.16 (refusal to submit to test for alcohol or drugs), and the CFI's certificate-action exposure live.

## Companion documents this week leans on

- AC 61-65 (current revision) -- the endorsement reference. Not regulation; recommended language. Examiners expect the AC 61-65 phrasing.
- AC 61-98 -- flight review and IPC. Explains the FAA's expected scope for the 1+1 hour 61.56 review.
- FAA Order 8900.1 Volume 5 Chapter 1 -- airman certification surveillance, the order that DPEs and FSDOs work from. Names how a CFI's records are inspected.
- FAA Form 8710-1 -- airman cert application. CFI candidates fill it out for their initial; CFI renewals via practical test or designated examiner do too.
