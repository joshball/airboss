# Week 3 drills

Week 3 lessons walked subpart H (the CFI subpart), the eligibility / aero-experience / records sections (61.183, 61.187, 61.189), the daily-life sections (61.193, 61.195), the renewal cycle (61.197), and the endorsement set (AC 61-65, 61.31, 61.39, 61.56, 61.87, 61.93, 61.183(i)).

The drills exercise four skills:

1. **Locate** -- given a CFI question, name the section or AC paragraph in seconds
2. **Match-the-endorsement** -- given a student / situation, pick the right endorsement and cite the source
3. **Apply the limitation** -- given a CFI in a specific situation, identify which 61.195 limitation binds
4. **Trap detector** -- catch the misreading and correct it

These drills feed `apps/study/` flash cards. Format 1 -> navigation cards. Format 2 -> scenario-and-endorsement cards. Format 3 -> diagnostic cards. Format 4 -> misconception-correction cards.

## Format 4 -- Trap detector

For each statement, identify the misreading and correct it.

| Statement                                                                                       | Trap and correction |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| "I need 200 hours of dual given before I can apply for the CFI."                                | Wrong. No such requirement in [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/183?at=2026). The hour bind is the underlying commercial cert (250 hr per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/129?at=2026)). |
| "Signing the student's logbook covers all my CFI record obligations."                           | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (a) covers the student-side. (b) requires a separate ledger of solos / knowledge tests / practical tests, retained 3 years per (c). |
| "AC 61-65 is regulation."                                                                       | Wrong. AC 61-65 is recommended language. The regulatory authority lives in [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/39?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026), etc. |
| "FIRCs can be done up to 24 months before expiration."                                          | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) (a)(4) requires the FIRC be in the 3 calendar months preceding the expiration month. |
| "I can FIRC my way out of an expired CFI cert."                                                 | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/199?at=2026) (expired CFI) excludes the FIRC pathway. Practical test or DPE check are the only options. |
| "I had 4 students recommended, all passed first time. I qualify for the 80% renewal."           | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) (a)(2) requires *at least 5* students recommended. 4 doesn't reach the floor. |
| "If a student fails the practical and re-takes and passes, that counts as a first-attempt pass." | Wrong. The 80% bar is *first-attempt* pass rate. Second-attempt passes don't count toward the 80%. |
| "The 61.87(n) solo endorsement is good for the rest of training."                               | Wrong. 90-day re-review required per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026) (n). |
| "The complex endorsement is aircraft-specific."                                                 | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) (e) endorsement travels with the pilot. Same endorsement is good in any complex airplane. |
| "I can teach instrument training as long as I have the IR on my pilot cert."                    | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (c) requires the CFII on the CFI cert. The pilot-cert IR is necessary but not sufficient. |
| "My CFI rating lets me accept gas money for any flight."                                        | Wrong. The CFI rating only kicks in for instructional flight. Non-instructional flights are governed by [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) (private) or [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/133?at=2026) (commercial). |
| "Spin endorsement is required only for CFI multi-engine."                                       | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/183?at=2026) (i) applies to both CFI airplane single-engine and multiengine initial candidates. |
| "I can sign 61.31 endorsements based on the student's reading the POH alone."                   | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) requires *training* (ground and flight). Reading material is not training. |
| "61.193 lists everything I can do as a CFI."                                                    | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) lists categories; [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (b) constrains them to the cat/class/type rating you hold on the CFI cert. |
| "I'll renew my FIRC the day before expiration."                                                 | Risk. The FIRC graduation date is not the renewal date until the FAA processes the paperwork. Norm: complete the FIRC at least 30 days before expiration. |
| "AC 61-65 paragraph A.1 covers all endorsements."                                               | Wrong. A.1 is the flight-review endorsement specifically. Different endorsements have different paragraphs (A.4 IPC, A.6 pre-solo knowledge, A.43 spin, etc.). |
| "If my CFI cert expires, my 61.189 record obligation ends."                                     | Wrong. 3-year retention under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (c) survives certificate lapse. |

## Hardcover drill -- 60 seconds, no notes

Integration. Read the prompt, give the answer in 60 seconds without referring to anything.

1. **Name the eight (or nine) categorical privileges of 61.193(a) in order.** -> Student pilot cert, pilot cert, flight instructor cert, ground instructor cert, aircraft rating, instrument rating, flight review / operating privilege / recency, practical test, knowledge test.
2. **A primary student is going for first solo tomorrow. Name the endorsements you owe before they walk to the airplane.** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026) (b) pre-solo knowledge test, (c) pre-solo flight maneuvers, (n) solo flight authorization. AC 61-65 paragraphs A.6, A.7, A.8.
3. **A friend asks if you'll fly them to dinner in their airplane and they'll pay for the gas. Walk me through the regulatory question.** -> 61.113 governs (private privileges) because no instruction is contemplated. The CFI rating is irrelevant. 61.113(c) cost-sharing requires pro-rata + common purpose; the friend's destination is the common purpose; the gas payment must be split pro-rata. If you "give some pointers" mid-flight, the analysis shifts toward 61.193 (instructional content) per Walker (2011); avoid the trap by not teaching during the recreational flight.
4. **A CFI's cert expires April 30, 2026. They complete an FIRC on February 15, 2026. When does the new cert expire, and why?** -> April 30, 2028. The February FIRC is in the 3-calendar-month preceding window, so [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) (b)(2) gives the back-end gift: new clock from April 2026 = April 2028.
5. **A CFI ASE without a CFII is asked to give 10 hours of instrument training to a commercial student. Walk me through whether it's legal.** -> No. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (c) requires the CFII on the CFI certificate for any instrument instruction toward a rating, including the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/129?at=2026) (a)(3)(i) commercial 10-hour requirement. The CFI must add the CFII (which requires the IR on the underlying pilot cert) before instructing.
6. **Distinguish a renewal from a reinstatement.** -> Renewal under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) is for a non-expired cert; pathways include FIRC, practical test, 80%-record, designated examiner, military. Reinstatement under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/199?at=2026) is for an expired cert; only practical test or DPE check applies.
7. **What is the spin endorsement, who needs it, who can sign it, and when?** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/183?at=2026) (i). Required for initial CFI airplane and powered-lift candidates; one-time-forever. Signed by an authorized instructor in a spin-certified airplane. Recommended language in AC 61-65 paragraph A.43.
8. **Two record types for CFIs. Distinguish them.** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (a): student-side logbook, signed for every flight or ground training session. (b): instructor-side ledger of solo / knowledge-test / practical-test endorsements, retained 3 years per (c).
9. **Mangiamele in two sentences.** -> Compensation in 61.113 is "any benefit received in exchange for the flight," not just money. Cost-sharing under 61.113(c) requires pro-rata payment AND common purpose for the destination. Affirmed widely in subsequent letters; binds CFIs flying recreationally as well as private pilots.
