# Week 3 oral -- "A friend asks for a flight review sign-off"

## The question

> "A friend asks you to sign them off for their flight review. Walk me through what you check, what you require, and what you sign. From the moment they call you, to the moment your name is in their logbook."

This oral integrates Week 3 (CFI subpart H, endorsements, and the daily-life CFI authority) with the Week-2 flight-review work. It tests whether the structural map of subpart H is in your head, whether you can apply 61.193 / 61.195 to a specific operation, whether you can drive 61.56 cleanly to its endorsement, and whether you handle the compensation lane (61.113 vs 61.193) correctly when the flight is happening with a friend.

## What this is testing

- Whether you can route a CFI authority question through 61.193 (privileges) and 61.195 (limitations) cleanly
- Whether you know the 61.56 flight-review requirements (1+1, "authorized instructor," AC 61-98 recommended content) cold
- Whether you keep 61.193 (CFI lane) separate from 61.113 (private-pilot compensation lane)
- Whether you check your own CFI currency before signing -- the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) self-audit
- Whether you write the 61.56(c)(2) endorsement from the AC 61-65 A.1 recommended language
- Whether you also check the pilot's underlying readiness (recency, BFR scope, possibly endorsements they're missing)
- Whether you keep the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) record correctly afterward (the student-side logbook AND the instructor-side ledger, even though a flight review is not a 61.189(b) ledger entry by default)

## Model answer (full walkthrough)

A defensible answer takes about 15-18 minutes spoken.

### Stage 1: Self-check first (the CFI lane)

> "Before I say yes, I check myself against [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026). My CFI cert has to be valid -- if it's expired, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/199?at=2026) governs and I can't sign anything until I take a practical test or DPE check. If it's valid but expiring within 3 calendar months, my next renewal is the FIRC pathway, fine, but I still hold valid privileges today.
>
> I check my rating: my friend is asking for a flight review in an aircraft for which I hold the matching CFI rating per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (b). If they're flying their Cessna 172 and I'm a CFI airplane single-engine, that matches. If they're asking for a multiengine BFR and I'm only CFI single-engine, I refer them to a CFI multiengine -- the privilege exists in [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) (a)(7), but the scope on my certificate doesn't reach.
>
> I check make-and-model under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (f): the 5-hour PIC threshold applies for *rating-issuance* training. A flight review isn't rating issuance, so the 5-hour rule technically isn't a 61.195(f) bind. But if I haven't flown the make-and-model in years, I should hesitate before signing -- not because the regulation requires more, but because AC 61-98 expects me to evaluate the maneuvers in conditions I can teach in. A defensible CFI declines a review in an unfamiliar make/model and refers out."

This opening earns trust. It demonstrates you treat your own currency as the first gate, not the last.

### Stage 2: Lane check (CFI vs. compensation)

> "Next, I figure out which lane I'm in. The friend is asking me to sign a flight review. That's instruction -- 61.56 requires 1+1 of training. So this is a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) (a)(7) flight, not a recreational flight. My CFI rating activates. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) is not the binding compensation lane here; [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) is.
>
> I can charge the friend for the review, or I can do it for free. The CFI lane permits compensation; the friend can also gift me a meal or pay for fuel without that triggering [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) cost-sharing rules. Mangiamele (2009) and the related Chief Counsel framing apply to 61.113 flights, not to 61.193 instructional flights. The CFI lane is cleaner for this purpose."

Demonstrates the compensation-boundary literacy that catches CFIs flying as friends.

### Stage 3: The pilot (61.56 scope check)

> "I check the friend's status. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) is the flight-review section. Subsections to walk:
>
> - 61.56(a) -- the 1+1 minimum: 1 hour of flight, 1 hour of ground. I plan a session that satisfies (a)(1) review of Part 91 operating rules and (a)(2) maneuvers to the friend's certificate scope.
> - 61.56(c) -- the 24-calendar-month rule: I confirm when the friend's last review was. If they're 25 months past, they're not legally PIC right now and the BFR has to happen before any solo flying.
> - 61.56(d) -- a practical test in the same category and class within 24 months substitutes. If the friend got their instrument rating last year, that practical test counted. They might not actually need a flight review yet.
> - 61.56(f) -- a WINGS phase substitutes. If the friend has WINGS phase credits, we check whether they've completed a full phase within 24 months.
>
> The (d) and (f) substitutes are why I ask 'when was your last training event?' rather than 'when was your last flight review?' Different events count.
>
> If the friend is genuinely past 24 months without an equivalent, the BFR is the correct remedy."

Demonstrates the 61.56 deep-read from Week 2.

### Stage 4: AC 61-98 expectations (what to actually cover)

> "I plan the review against AC 61-98. The reg requires 1+1 minimum and the (a)(1)/(a)(2) elements; the AC recommends much more. AC 61-98D recommends:
>
> - Pre-review pilot self-assessment: where does the friend feel rusty?
> - Ground portion (1+ hour): Part 91 review, weather, performance, airspace updates, recent accident-trend conversation
> - Flight portion (1+ hour): preflight risk-management briefing, slow flight, stalls, steep turns, ground reference, emergency descent, engine-out, lost-comm
> - Post-flight: debrief, areas for continued study, the endorsement
>
> For a friend who has been flying actively, the AC content collapses to about 2 hours total. For a friend who has been off the controls for a year or more, the review may take 3 hours and may need to be spread across multiple sessions until I'm satisfied per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) (c)(2)'s 'satisfactorily completed' bar. The Hicks (2010) framing: my signature certifies *my* judgment that the review was satisfactory.
>
> Important: the AC is guidance, not regulation. The regulatory floor is 1+1. The professional standard is the AC."

Demonstrates the regulation/guidance distinction without dismissing AC 61-98.

### Stage 5: Other endorsements the friend might need

> "While I'm at it, I check what else the friend might need. The flight review only addresses [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026). Other items they might be missing:
>
> - [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (a) passenger-carrying recency: 3 takeoffs and landings in the preceding 90 days. The flight review can incorporate three full-stop landings if I plan it that way.
> - [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (b) night recency: 3 night full-stop landings if the friend wants night passenger ops. I don't have to address this in the BFR, but the conversation should mention it.
> - [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (c) IFR currency: separate timer. The BFR doesn't refresh IFR recency. If the friend is instrument-rated and lapsed, they need an IPC under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (d) -- I can combine the IPC with the BFR if I hold the CFII; otherwise I refer.
> - [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsements: if the friend is now flying a complex airplane they didn't have before (HP, complex, tailwheel, high-altitude), they may need the endorsement before they can act as PIC. I check before the review.
>
> The flight review is the *headline* event but not the only event. A working CFI sweeps the friend's whole pilot status and addresses what's missing."

Demonstrates the integration thinking the cumulative oral expects.

### Stage 6: The endorsement itself

> "After the review is satisfactorily completed, I sign the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) (c)(2) endorsement in the friend's logbook. The recommended language is in AC 61-65 paragraph A.1:
>
> 'I certify that [name] has satisfactorily completed a flight review as required by 61.56 on [date].'
>
> I add my signature, my CFI certificate number, and the date. I stamp or print my full name where the friend can read it.
>
> The wording is short. The implication is large: the friend now has a 24-calendar-month window of currency for [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) purposes. The clock is the back-end-month math: a review on March 15, 2026 is good through March 31, 2028."

### Stage 7: The records side

> "After signing, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) governs my records:
>
> - 61.189(a): I sign the friend's logbook entry showing the dual time and the BFR endorsement. I document my entry against the 61.56 review.
> - 61.189(b): the *instructor-side* ledger only requires entries for solo / knowledge-test / practical-test endorsements. A flight review endorsement isn't a (b) entry by the regulation. But many CFIs include flight reviews in their personal record because it tracks their teaching history; that's beyond the regulatory minimum but professionally valuable.
> - 61.189(c): three-year retention applies to (b) entries. I keep the records.
>
> I close my own logbook with the dual given. The flight review can also feed my [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) renewal -- if I'm tracking flight time toward an [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) (a)(2) renewal pathway in the future, the recommendation entries from this period factor in."

### Stage 8: The "satisfactorily completed" judgment

> "If the review is *not* satisfactory, I don't sign. The Hicks (2010) framing: my signature certifies my judgment in real-time. I cannot retroactively withdraw the endorsement, and I cannot pre-issue an endorsement contingent on future improvement. If the friend isn't ready today, we plan additional sessions until they are. The friend's flight review is open until I sign; they remain not-current until then.
>
> The professional question: what if the friend gets pushy? 'Just sign it; we're friends.' My answer is no. The friend's BFR is my professional act, and I owe my professional standard to the FAA and to the next 24 months of flying the friend will do under that endorsement. If we're friends, the friend respects that. If they don't respect it, the friendship is more strained than the BFR. Either way, my signature is professional, not personal."

Closes with the professional ethic. This is the part DPEs reward when an oral candidate gets it right.

## Compressed answer (under 90 seconds)

> "I check myself first ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026)): my CFI cert is current and rated for the friend's airplane. The flight is in the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) lane (instruction), not the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) lane (recreational). I check the friend's status against [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026): 24 months past last review or equivalent? Does (d), (f) apply? I plan the 1+1 review per (a) and pad it to AC 61-98 standards. I sweep [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) recency and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsements while I'm at it. I conduct the review; if satisfactorily completed, I sign the AC 61-65 paragraph A.1 endorsement: 'I certify that [name] has satisfactorily completed a flight review as required by 61.56 on [date].' I sign the friend's logbook ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (a)). My instructor-side ledger doesn't require this entry under (b), but I track it anyway. The friend now has a 24-calendar-month window."

## What goes wrong (failure modes)

| Failure mode                                                                          | Diagnosis |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------- |
| Doesn't check own CFI currency first                                                  | Misses [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) self-audit; expired CFIs sign invalid endorsements. |
| Skips the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (b) rating-match check                | A CFI signing a flight review in an aircraft they don't hold the appropriate CFI rating for has signed an invalid endorsement. |
| Treats the review as a recreational flight with the friend                            | Misses the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/113?at=2026) / [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) lane separation; might also miscount the time as PIC vs dual given. |
| Cites only [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) (a), forgets the (d), (f) substitutes | The friend may not actually need a BFR if a recent practical test or WINGS phase counts. Asking the wrong question wastes everyone's time. |
| Conflates the BFR with [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) recency                   | Two different timers. A current BFR doesn't refresh passenger or IFR recency. |
| Skips the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsement sweep                       | A friend who bought a complex airplane since their last BFR may need [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) (e) endorsement before legally exercising PIC. The BFR is a good moment to address it. |
| Quotes AC 61-65 wording loosely                                                       | A.1 is short and exact. Loose paraphrase suggests the candidate hasn't read the AC. |
| Treats AC 61-98 as regulation                                                         | AC 61-98 is guidance. Saying "the regulation requires 2 hours" is wrong. The regulation requires 1+1 minimum. |
| Says "I'd just sign it; the friend asked"                                             | Hicks (2010) framing matters: the signature is the CFI's professional judgment in real-time. Pressure-signing is the kind of behavior that creates 61.59 falsification exposure if the review wasn't actually conducted. |
| Forgets the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (a) student-side logbook entry        | The endorsement *is* the entry. Missing it is a record violation as well as a regulatory one. |
| Tries to combine BFR with IPC without holding CFII                                    | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (c) requires CFII for IPC. CFI airplane single-engine alone can do the BFR but not the IPC. |
| Quotes the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) (b) ledger as required for BFRs          | Subtly wrong. (b) is for solo / knowledge-test / practical-test endorsements. A BFR endorsement isn't a (b) entry by the regulation. (Many CFIs track BFRs anyway; that's professional practice, not regulatory requirement.) |
| Forgets the back-end calendar-month math                                              | A March 15, 2026 BFR is good through March 31, 2028, not March 15, 2028. The back-end month is free per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) (c). |

## Variant prompts

| Variant                                                                                                  | What changes |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| "A friend asks for an IPC sign-off"                                                                      | Add [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (d) and AC 61-65 A.4. CFII required per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (c). The 6-month / 6-month grace / IPC structure of [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) (c) and (d) becomes central. |
| "A primary student asks for first solo sign-off"                                                         | The pre-solo trio: [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/87?at=2026) (b) knowledge, (c) maneuvers, (n) authorization. AC 61-65 A.6, A.7, A.8. The 90-day re-review under (n) is high-yield. |
| "A pilot asks for a high-performance endorsement"                                                        | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) (f) and AC 61-65 A.15. One-time-forever, travels with the pilot, requires ground and flight training. |
| "A CFI candidate asks for the spin endorsement"                                                          | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/183?at=2026) (i) and AC 61-65 A.43. Requires spin-certified airplane. The CFI signing the spin endorsement must be airplane-CFI-rated and willing to demonstrate the maneuvers. |
| "A pilot asks for a flight review and an IPC at the same session"                                        | The CFII is required for the IPC. The two endorsements are separate (A.1 and A.4). The same session can satisfy both, but the endorsements are individual and reflect the specific operations conducted. |
| "Your friend who is asking has expired flight review by 30 months and an expired medical"                | The medical is a pilot question (Part 67/68); the BFR can be conducted but the friend cannot exercise PIC privileges until the medical is also addressed. Sweep both. |
