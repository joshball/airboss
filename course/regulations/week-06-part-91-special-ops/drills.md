# Week 6 drills

Week 6 covered the special-operations subpart, the locate-only subparts (F, G, H, J, K, L), and the integration walk. The drills exercise the navigation reflex across the entire part. By the end of these drills a CFI candidate should be able to route any Part 91 question to a subpart in seconds.

These drills feed `apps/study/` flash cards. Format 1 -> navigation cards (locate the section). Format 2 -> integration cards (which subparts apply). Format 3 -> trap cards (Common Misreadings transformed into prompt/correction). Format 4 -> integration scenarios (cumulative, drawing from any of Weeks 1-6).

## Format 3 -- Trap detector

For each statement, identify the misreading and correct it.

| Statement                                                                      | Trap and correction                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Aerobatics under 1,500 AGL is fine over open country."                        | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/303?at=2026)(e) prohibits aerobatics below 1,500 AGL anywhere; "open country" is necessary but not sufficient.                                                                                                                  |
| "My CFI tow endorsement covers banner towing."                                 | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/309?at=2026) covers gliders/unpowered ultralight only. Banner towing is [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/311?at=2026), separate LOA.                                                                                          |
| "Two airplanes flying to lunch together are in formation."                     | Conditional. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/111?at=2026)(b) requires *prior arrangement*. Without it, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/111?at=2026)(a) (close-as-collision-hazard) governs.                                                                         |
| "Solo aerobatics requires a parachute."                                        | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)(c) requires parachutes only when more than one person is aboard.                                                                                                                                                   |
| "Restricted-category aircraft can fly any commercial mission."                 | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/313?at=2026)(c) prohibits carriage for hire; [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/313?at=2026)(a) limits ops to the certificated special purpose.                                                                                 |
| "Experimental aircraft can fly IFR if equipped."                               | Conditional. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(d)(2) defaults to VFR day only. IFR requires specific authorization in the operating-limitations document.                                                                                                   |
| "Subpart F applies to any turbine airplane."                                   | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/501?at=2026) limits applicability to large airplanes (> 12,500 lb), turbojet multi-engine, or fractional. A single-engine turboprop is not subpart F.                                                                           |
| "RVSM is automatic above FL290."                                               | Wrong. RVSM requires authorization under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/706?at=2026) -- aircraft equipment, pilot training, operator authorization.                                                                                                                   |
| "SFARs are old rules I don't need to worry about."                             | Wrong. SFAR 73 (R22/R44) and SFAR 108 (MU-2B) are routine and binding for those communities.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| "Subpart H is just RVSM."                                                      | Wrong. Subpart H covers foreign aircraft, U.S. ops abroad, MNPS, RVSM, and special bilateral arrangements. RVSM is one section.                                                                                                                                           |
| "Fractional pilots are governed by Part 135."                                  | Wrong. Fractional operates under Part 91 subpart K. The operational-control concept ports in from 135 / 121 but the regulatory home is 91.                                                                                                                                |
| "The 4-nm-from-airway aerobatic buffer is from the airway edge."               | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/303?at=2026)(d) measures from airway centerline (effectively beyond the 4-nm half-width of the airway itself).                                                                                                                  |
| "An experimental can carry passengers without telling them it's experimental." | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(d)(1) requires every passenger be advised of the experimental nature.                                                                                                                                              |
| "Steep turn instruction triggers the parachute requirement."                   | Conditional. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)(c)(1) triggers at > 60-deg bank or > 30-deg pitch; [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)(d) carves out CFI instruction required for a cert/rating. Standard 45-deg-bank steep turns don't trigger. |
| "Carriage for hire in formation is OK if briefed."                             | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/111?at=2026)(c) bans formation flight when *any* aircraft carries passengers for hire. No briefing exception.                                                                                                                   |

## Format 4 -- Integration scenarios (cumulative, Weeks 1-6)

These prompts pull from Part 61 and Part 91 together. Each tests routing first, then specific section recall.

> A 1,200-hour CFI is asked to give a flight review in a friend's RV-7 (experimental amateur-built). The friend earned the experimental certificate two months ago and just finished the 40-hour Phase I. The CFI plans 1.5 hours of dual including some steep turns and a simulated emergency descent. What regulatory layers apply?

Walkthrough:

1. **Pilot side ([Week 2-3]):** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review. The CFI must hold a current CFI certificate ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026)) and the right rating for the airframe. The friend (private pilot) needs the review to act as PIC; [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(a) requires 1+1 minimum.
2. **Flight side ([Weeks 4-5]):** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/103?at=2026) preflight, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/3?at=2026) PIC authority. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/107?at=2026) seatbelts. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(d)(1) experimental briefing must be given to the friend (they own the airplane and know it's experimental, but the regulation requires the brief be given anyway).
3. **Operating limitations:** The RV-7 is post-Phase I -- passenger-carrying authorized. The CFI is a "passenger" in the sense that they're aboard for instruction, not testing.
4. **CFI compensation:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(a)(2) prohibits carriage for compensation. Per the 2021 LODA framework, paid CFI instruction in an experimental amateur-built requires a Letter of Deviation Authority. Without the LODA, the CFI's compensation creates a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(a)(2) issue.
5. **Steep turns:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)(c)(1) parachute trigger applies if steep turns exceed 60-deg bank. Standard 45-deg-bank steep turns don't trigger; if the CFI plans 60+ deg bank, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)(d) carves out the requirement when the maneuver is part of CFI instruction for a cert/rating, but the flight review isn't strictly a cert/rating. Conservative answer: stay at or below 45-deg bank, or both pilots wear parachutes.

> A pilot wants to skydive over their own home with a friend acting as jump pilot in a Cessna 182. The home is in a residential subdivision in Class G airspace. Walk the regulatory considerations.

Walkthrough:

1. **The pilot side:** Jump pilot needs a current pilot certificate, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026) recency for any passengers carried (no jumpers on the climb to altitude wouldn't count as passengers in the traditional sense, but currency still matters). The skydiver needs USPA certification or equivalent.
2. **The flight side:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/3?at=2026) PIC, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/103?at=2026) preflight, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/119?at=2026) minimum altitudes (over a residential subdivision = congested area, 1,000 ft AGL min, 500 ft horizontal buffer to people / vessels / structures). [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026) parachute pack cycle and rigger seal verification.
3. **Subpart D / Part 105:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026) covers the airplane side; 14 CFR Part 105 covers the jumper side -- including [Part 105.13](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/105/13?at=2026) on jumps over congested areas (prohibited absent specific permission). A jump over a residential neighborhood is almost certainly a Part 105 violation.
4. **Subpart B catch-all:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/13?at=2026) -- if the operation endangers persons or property, careless or reckless attaches.

> A pilot earns their commercial certificate today. They want to take their first commercial flight tomorrow night, IFR, in a Beechcraft Bonanza they own. They have a current third-class medical (issued last month). What's their full regulatory checklist?

Walkthrough:

1. **The pilot side:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/23?at=2026) -- second-class medical required for commercial privileges (third-class is insufficient). The flight tomorrow can't legally be exercised as a commercial flight under the third-class. The pilot can either delay until they get a second-class or fly the trip *not for compensation* under their private privileges (the third-class is fine for that).
2. **Currency / recency:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(a) passenger recency, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b) night passenger recency (3 full-stop night landings in 90 days), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(c) IFR currency.
3. **The flight side:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/103?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/167?at=2026) IFR fuel, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/169?at=2026) IFR alternate (1-2-3 rule against the destination forecast at ETA).
4. **Equipment / inspections:** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026) IFR equipment, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026) altimeter/static current, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026) transponder current, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/207?at=2026) ELT, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/225?at=2026) ADS-B Out as airspace requires.

> A pilot flying a Cessna Citation Mustang under Part 91 in fractional service files IFR FL370 over the North Atlantic. Walk the regulatory layers.

Walkthrough:

1. **Universal:** Subparts A, B, C, D as applicable, E.
2. **Population overlay:** Subpart F (Citation Mustang is turbojet multi-engine). Subpart G if transport-category certification basis. Subpart H ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/703?at=2026) U.S. ops abroad, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/705?at=2026) MNPS, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/706?at=2026) RVSM at FL370). Subpart K (fractional ownership program rules including operational control [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/1011?at=2026)).
3. **No SFAR for the Citation Mustang. ICAO Annex 2 governs over high seas. Foreign-airspace rules govern in foreign airspace (subject to [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/703?at=2026) keeping U.S. rules in force where they don't conflict).**

## Hardcover drill -- 60 seconds, no notes

Read the prompt; give the answer in 60 seconds without referring to anything.

1. **Name the subparts of Part 91 in order.** -> A general; B flight rules; C equipment; D special ops; E maintenance; F large/turbine/fractional; G additional transport; H foreign / U.S. abroad / RVSM; I noise; J waivers; K fractional; L continued airworthiness.
2. **An experimental amateur-built Phase I airplane wants to land at the major nearby airport. The airport has a Class C surface area. Legal?** -> Probably not without specific authorization. Phase I operating limitations typically prohibit operations within densely populated areas / congested airways and require flight within a specific test area. Class C surface area is usually outside the test area, and the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)(d)(3) tower notification requirement is the additional Phase II step.
3. **Solo aerobatic flight at 1,800 AGL, 6 nm from the centerline of V25, 3 SM viz, no congested area, in Class G. Legal?** -> Yes. All six [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/303?at=2026) conditions met. No parachute required (solo, no other person aboard).
4. **Distinguish [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/309?at=2026) from [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/311?at=2026).** -> 309 covers towing gliders and unpowered ultralight vehicles; pilot needs an endorsement and recurrent towing currency. 311 covers towing anything else (banners, target sleeves, advertising); requires a Letter of Authorization from the FAA.
5. **A pilot at NetJets has a question about duty/rest cycles. Where in 14 CFR is the answer?** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/1057?at=2026) -- subpart K. (Distinct from Part 117 -- which covers Part 121 duty/rest -- and Part 135.267 / 135.273 which cover Part 135 duty/rest.)
6. **A pilot is asked: "Does the FAA require an SIC for our King Air 350?" What sections govern?** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/531?at=2026) (subpart F SIC requirement) plus the type-certificate data sheet for the King Air 350 (which dictates whether two pilots are required by certification basis). If the type cert says one pilot, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/531?at=2026) doesn't add an SIC requirement. If two pilots are required by type cert or operation, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/531?at=2026) governs.
7. **Where does the SFAR list live?** -> Front of 14 CFR, in the prefatory "Special Federal Aviation Regulations" section that precedes Part 21. Many airframes also list applicable SFARs in their type certificate data sheet.

## Where this drill set fits

These drills bring the structural map of Part 91 into reflex. Format 1 (Locate) -> navigation cards. Format 2 (Subparts that apply) -> integration cards exercising the cumulative-stack model. Format 3 (Trap detector) -> misconception correction. Format 4 (Integration scenarios) -> cumulative scenario cards bridging Weeks 1-6.

The Week 6 drills are the densest in the course so far because the integration is the load-bearing skill of the second half of the course. After Week 6 the course shifts to operations (141 / 135) where the same integration skill is exercised against a new layer.
