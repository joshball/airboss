# Week 6 -- Part 91 special ops + integration

Wraps Part 91. The first four lessons cover the rules pilots actually invoke (or see invoked above them) plus the structural map for the parts of 91 a CFI rarely touches but should be able to locate. The fifth lesson is the integration walk: all of Part 91 in 90 minutes, end to end. By the end of the week the learner can answer "what's where in 91?" without flipping a table of contents.

## What you'll be able to do by end of week

- Cite and apply the special-operations rules: aerobatic ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/303?at=2026)), parachute ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/307?at=2026)), towing ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/309?at=2026)), formation ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/111?at=2026))
- Locate the restricted, limited, and experimental category operating rules ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/313?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/315?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/319?at=2026)) and state who is and isn't allowed aboard
- Name the contents of subparts F, G, H, I, J, K, L without memorizing their interiors
- Recognize when an SFAR overlay modifies Part 91 for a specific airframe, region, or operation
- Walk the entirety of Part 91 from subpart A through the SFARs in 90 minutes, with running commentary on what each subpart owns and when it applies
