# Week 5 -- Part 91, equipment and maintenance

The airplane side of "the pilot, the flight, the operation." Week 4 walked subpart B (the flight rules). Week 5 zooms in on **subpart C** (equipment) and **subpart E** (maintenance). These two subparts together answer one question: *is this airplane legal to fly today, for this operation?*

Students get destroyed on checkrides here. The inspections are spread across five sections (91.207, 91.409, 91.411, 91.413, 91.417). The equipment rules require navigating 91.205 and 91.213 *together*. The ADS-B rules sit in their own corner (91.225, 91.227) and almost nobody reads them carefully.

This week is **deep**. Front-to-back reading of subparts C and E. Subpart D (special operations) is mostly Week 6 territory; we name and locate it where it overlaps. Subparts F (large and turbine multi-engine) and G (RVSM) we skim and locate -- they apply to airplanes most GA pilots will never fly, but a CFI candidate is expected to know they exist.

## What you'll be able to do by end of week

- State which documents must be aboard the aircraft, by memory, and locate them in the regulation
- Apply 91.205 to a specific operation, building the cumulative VFR-day, VFR-night, and IFR equipment lists from the same source rule
- Walk the no-MEL pathway in 91.213(d) for an inoperative instrument or piece of equipment, including the placard step
- Recall every inspection currency that applies to a typical Part 91 GA airplane and which section it lives in
- Distinguish "stays in the airplane" records from "stays in the maintenance logs" records and cite 91.417 / 91.419
- Apply 91.225 and 91.227 to determine whether ADS-B Out is required for a planned flight in specific airspace

## How this week fits

Week 4 built the flight-rules half of Part 91 (subparts A and B). Week 5 builds the equipment-and-maintenance half (subparts C and E). Together they answer the two halves of the airplane question: *can the airplane do this flight?* (Week 5) and *can the flight be conducted this way?* (Week 4). Subpart D (special operations -- altitude rules, oxygen, large-aircraft towing, etc.) is split: the universal pieces are in Week 4, the niche operational pieces sit in Week 6.

Subparts F and G are named-and-located only:

- **Subpart F** (sections 91.501 through 91.535) -- additional rules for large and turbine multi-engine airplanes operated under Part 91. Includes the famous 91.501 "fractional ownership and management" rules. Most GA pilots never touch this subpart. Know it exists.
- **Subpart G** (sections 91.701 through 91.715) -- additional rules for foreign civil aircraft, plus the **RVSM** (Reduced Vertical Separation Minimum) rules in 91.706 / 91.180 (referencing the new RVSM authorization scheme post-2019). RVSM is universal above FL290 in the U.S., but the operating authorization is now data-driven through ADS-B Out instead of the old letter-of-authorization process. We mention it; we don't lesson on it.
