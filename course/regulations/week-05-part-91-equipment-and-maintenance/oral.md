# Week 5 oral -- "Walk me through every inspection currency check on this airplane for an IFR flight tomorrow"

## The question

> "Tomorrow morning you and a passenger are flying IFR from KSLE to KSBA in this Cirrus SR22. The flight is mostly in IMC, with an ILS into KSBA. Walk me through every inspection currency check on this airplane that affects whether you can legally fly that mission. While you're at it, tell me what currencies *you* need on the pilot side, what documents and equipment must be aboard, and how you'd handle an inop fuel quantity gauge if you discovered one on preflight."

This oral integrates Week 5 (equipment and maintenance) with the Week 2 pilot currencies. It tests whether the candidate can walk the *full* legality stack -- airplane and pilot together -- for a specific mission, and whether they can apply [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d) on the spot.

## What this is testing

- Whether you can list every inspection currency on a Part 91 IFR airplane cold, including the four 24-calendar-month checks that are easy to forget
- Whether you can walk the no-MEL deferral pathway under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d) and apply it correctly to a specific item
- Whether you keep airplane currency, pilot currency, and operational requirements separate while still routing them all through the dispatch decision
- Whether you understand the cumulative structure of [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026) (b -> c -> d) and apply it to an IFR-night-vs-IFR-day case
- Whether you know the airspace-based ADS-B Out rule and the 91.225(g) deviation pathway
- Whether you cite the AROW documents under both [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/203?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/9?at=2026) without prompting

## Model answer (full walkthrough)

A defensible answer takes about 18-20 minutes spoken. The structure walks airplane currency first, then pilot currency, then equipment, then the inop-item handling.

### Stage 1: The frame (one sentence)

> "I'm answering 'is this airplane legal for this operation?' separately from 'is this pilot legal for this operation?' The airplane question routes through Part 91 subparts C (equipment) and E (maintenance). The pilot question routes through Part 61 subpart B. Both must be yes for the flight to launch. Then I'm answering 'is the operation legal?' which is Part 91 subpart B and the airspace-equipment rules in subpart C. Same flight, three independent legality questions."

This opening earns trust by separating the three checks the student of Week 4 + Week 5 should never confuse.

### Stage 2: Aircraft inspection currencies

> "For the airplane, four current inspections matter for an IFR flight in 2026:
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(a) annual** -- 12 calendar months from the month of the most recent annual signoff by an Inspection Authorization holder. I check the airframe logbook for the most recent annual entry; if signed in October 2025, valid through October 31, 2026. If we're past that, the airplane is grounded.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(b) 100-hour** -- not applicable here. This is a personal Cirrus, not a for-hire or instruction-for-hire airplane. If it were a flight school SR22 used for instruction with the school providing the airplane, the 100-hour would apply -- 100 hours of time-in-service from the most recent 100-hour or annual signoff, with the 10-hour ferry overrun limited to ferry use only.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026) altimeter / static / encoder** -- 24 calendar months, IFR in controlled airspace. This one trips students up because it's calendar-based and easy to forget. I check the airframe logbook (or the avionics shop record) for the most recent 411 inspection. The 411 covers three components: the static system, the altimeter, and the automatic pressure altitude reporting equipment (the encoder feeding the transponder). If 411 is expired, the airplane cannot fly IFR in controlled airspace -- VFR is still available.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026) transponder** -- 24 calendar months, all operations with an installed transponder. Independent of 411. Often signed at the same shop visit as 411, but they're separate regulatory checks. If 413 is expired and the airplane has a transponder, the transponder cannot be operated under 91.413(a). That conflicts with [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/215?at=2026)(c), which requires operating an installed transponder. The resolution is to treat the expired-413 transponder as not approved for use, and either avoid 91.215(b) airspace or get a 91.215(d) ATC deviation.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/207?at=2026)(d) ELT 12-month inspection** -- the ELT itself must have been inspected within the preceding 12 calendar months. Independent of the battery replacement rule in 91.207(c).
>
> **AD compliance** -- ongoing under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/403?at=2026)(a). Not a calendar event, but the AD list against the airframe / engine / prop must show compliance for each applicable AD."

This stage is the spine of the oral. The candidate names the four-or-five currencies cleanly and distinguishes IFR-only from all-ops.

### Stage 3: Pilot currencies (cumulative from Week 2)

> "On the pilot side, separate from the airplane:
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review** -- 24 calendar months. Or an equivalent under 61.56(d)/(e)/(f)/(g) -- a practical test in same category and class, a sport-pilot proficiency check, a WINGS phase, or a military competency check.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(a) passenger currency** -- three takeoffs and landings within 90 days, in same category, class, type if applicable. Required for carrying passengers; the flight tomorrow has a passenger.
>
> **[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(c) IFR currency** -- six instrument approaches, holding procedures, intercepting and tracking courses within the preceding 6 calendar months. Required to act as PIC of an IFR flight in IMC. The 6-month grace and the IPC pathway apply if currency lapsed.
>
> **Medical or BasicMed** -- a current third-class (or higher) medical, or a BasicMed cycle (physician exam within 4 calendar years, online course within 24 calendar months). [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/23?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/53?at=2026) on always-on fitness.
>
> Plus type-specific endorsements under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) if the airplane requires one (the SR22 doesn't typically need a complex / HP endorsement -- the gear is fixed and the engine is below 200 HP)."

The pilot stage names the 61.56 / 61.57 / medical structure cleanly and includes the working-CFI consideration of endorsements.

### Stage 4: Aircraft documents and equipment

> "Documents under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/203?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/9?at=2026) -- AROW. Airworthiness certificate (visible to passengers per 91.203(a)(1)). Registration certificate (in the airplane). Operating limitations -- the POH and the cockpit placards. Weight and balance current.
>
> Equipment under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026). For our IFR flight, the cumulative list:
>
> **(b) ATOMATOFLAMES** -- airspeed, tachometer, oil pressure, manifold pressure (constant-speed prop -- yes for the SR22), altimeter, oil temperature, fuel quantity for each tank, anti-collision per the date and the night-flight rule, magnetic direction indicator, ELT, seat belts.
>
> **(c) FLAPS additions if night** -- spare fuses (if the airplane uses fuses), position lights, anticollision lights, source of electrical power. Tomorrow morning's mission is IFR in IMC -- if we're departing before sunrise we're in 91.205(c) territory; if we're departing after sunrise but operating IFR in IMC we're not in (c) territory and we don't add the night equipment unless we're night.
>
> **(d) GRABCARD additions for IFR** -- generator/alternator, two-way radios and nav appropriate for the route, attitude indicator, slip-skid indicator, sensitive altimeter (replaces the basic altimeter from (b)), clock with hours/minutes/seconds, rate-of-turn indicator, directional gyro. The Cirrus G3 cockpit's PFD/MFD covers most of these as FAA-approved equivalents under 91.205(a).
>
> Plus airspace-based equipment requirements: 91.215 transponder for any flight transitioning Class B/C/Mode C veil. 91.225 ADS-B Out for the same airspace plus Class E above 10,000 MSL in the contiguous 48. The Cirrus has both, with 1090ES installed and tested."

This stage exercises the cumulative 91.205 structure and the airspace-based ADS-B / transponder rules.

### Stage 5: Operational rules for the flight

> "Operational rules from Week 4 -- subpart B:
>
> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/103?at=2026) -- preflight action, all available information for the flight.
>
> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/167?at=2026) -- IFR fuel requirements: enough fuel to fly to the first airport of intended landing, then to the alternate, plus 45 minutes at normal cruise. Alternate required if KSBA's forecast doesn't allow the 1-2-3 rule (1 hour before/after ETA, 2,000 ft ceiling, 3 sm visibility).
>
> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/169?at=2026) -- IFR alternate weather minima. Standard 600/2 for precision, 800/2 for non-precision (with the airport-specific minima from the IAP).
>
> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/175?at=2026) -- approach to the destination, the ILS rules.
>
> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/189?at=2026) -- IFR climb/descent procedures, the 1,000-ft/1,500-ft minimum-en-route altitude. (...)"

(The oral expects the candidate to know subpart B from Week 4. We name the operational rules to show the integration even though the focus is Week 5.)

### Stage 6: The inop fuel quantity gauge -- 91.213(d) walkthrough

> "Examiner asks: I find a fuel quantity gauge that's reading zero with the tank full. The other tank's gauge works. I run [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d) the four-step test:
>
> **Step 1**: is the gauge required by [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026)? 91.205(b)(8) requires 'a fuel quantity gauge for each tank.' For each tank means each tank needs one gauge. The right gauge being inop is technically a violation of 91.205(b)(8) regardless of how many other gauges work. **Step 1 fails the deferral.** I cannot defer this gauge under 91.213(d).
>
> If for some reason 91.205 didn't bind (it does here, but suppose it didn't), I'd continue:
>
> **Step 2**: KOEL. The Cirrus POH KOEL lists fuel quantity indicators as required for VFR day, VFR night, and IFR. Even if 91.205 didn't bind, the KOEL would. **Step 2 also fails.**
>
> **Step 3**: AD compliance. No AD on the fuel quantity gauges of an SR22 of this build that I'm aware of, but I'd verify against the AD list.
>
> **Step 4**: any other Part 91 rule for IFR. None specific.
>
> Even if Steps 3 and 4 didn't bind, Steps 1 and 2 would. The gauge cannot be deferred. The flight is grounded until the gauge is fixed.
>
> If the inop item were instead a cabin map light, Step 1 (no -- not in 91.205), Step 2 (no -- not in KOEL), Step 3 (no -- no AD), Step 4 (no -- no other rule). All four NO. Defer: deactivate or remove, placard the cockpit control INOPERATIVE per 91.213(d)(3), record per 14 CFR 43.9, pilot determines no hazard. Then fly."

This stage is the high-yield content for an examiner -- they'll ask the inop-item question for sure. The four-step walk and the contrast between fuel gauge and map light shows the candidate understands the structure.

### Stage 7: Records and post-maintenance

> "Records: under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/417?at=2026), the airframe / engine / prop logs are kept by the owner -- not in the airplane. The airworthiness and registration certificates are aboard per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/203?at=2026). The 411 and 413 records, AD compliance records, and Form 337s for major alterations are kept in the maintenance logs and travel with the airplane on sale per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/419?at=2026).
>
> Post-maintenance under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/407?at=2026) -- if the airplane has had recent maintenance, I check whether (a) approval-for-return-to-service was signed and (b) whether a (b) test flight is required. A top overhaul or autopilot replacement triggers (b); a tire change does not. The line is whether the work could appreciably change flight characteristics."

### Stage 8: Pilot continuing duty under 91.7

> "Finally, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/7?at=2026) is the pilot's continuing airworthiness duty. Even after I've checked all the inspection currencies, all the equipment, all the documents, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/7?at=2026)(b) makes me responsible for determining the airplane is in condition for safe flight, and discontinuing the flight if a mechanical condition develops. The mechanic's signature is a presumption; the pilot's call is contemporaneous."

## Compressed answer (under 90 seconds)

> "Airplane currencies: annual ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(a), 12 cal mo), 411 ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026), 24 cal mo, IFR only), 413 ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026), 24 cal mo, all ops), ELT inspection ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/207?at=2026)(d), 12 cal mo). Plus AD compliance ongoing per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/403?at=2026). For-hire ops add 100-hour ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(b), 100 TIS).
>
> Pilot currencies: flight review ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026), 24 cal mo), passenger recency ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(a), 90d), IFR recency ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(c), 6 cal mo), medical or BasicMed.
>
> Documents: AROW from [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/203?at=2026) (A, R) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/9?at=2026) (O, W).
>
> Equipment: [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026) cumulative -- (b) for VFR day, plus (c) for night, plus (d) for IFR. Plus 91.215 transponder for B/C/veil airspace, 91.225 ADS-B Out for same plus E ≥ 10,000 MSL.
>
> Inop item: run [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d) four-step. 91.205 / KOEL / AD / other Part 91 rule. If all four NO, deactivate / remove, placard, record, hazard-determine. Then fly. The placard is step 3, not step 1.
>
> Continuing pilot duty: [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/7?at=2026)(b)."

## What goes wrong (failure modes)

| Failure mode                                               | Diagnosis                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| ---------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Names only the annual                                      | Misses 411/413/ELT -- the four 24-month / 12-month checks that often lapse                                                                                                                            |
| Confuses 411 and 413                                       | 411 is altimeter/static/encoder for IFR; 413 is transponder for all ops. Independent timers.                                                                                                          |
| Says 91.413 is IFR-only                                    | Wrong. 91.413 applies to any operation with an installed transponder.                                                                                                                                 |
| Says the annual covers the 100-hour for personal use       | The 100-hour is not required for personal-use (no for-hire / no instruction-for-hire-providing-the-airplane). The candidate should state that 100-hour does NOT apply, not that the annual covers it. |
| Tries to defer a fuel quantity gauge through placard alone | Misses 91.213(d) Step 1 (91.205 lookup). The gauge is required by 91.205(b)(8) and cannot be deferred.                                                                                                |
| Puts placard at Step 1                                     | Placard is step 3 of the deferral process, after the four authorities clear.                                                                                                                          |
| Says ATOMATOFLAMES requires a landing light                | Wrong. 91.205(c)(2) requires a landing light only for-hire ops. Private night doesn't need one (recommended, not required).                                                                           |
| Says position lights are required from civil twilight      | Wrong. 91.209(a) is sunset-to-sunrise plain. Civil twilight is the 61.51 logging concept.                                                                                                             |
| Says ADS-B Out is required for any IFR flight              | Wrong. 91.225 is airspace-based, not flight-rules-based. IFR in pure Class E below 10,000 MSL with no Class B/C transitions: ADS-B Out not required.                                                  |
| Doesn't mention the 91.225(g) deviation                    | The deviation pathway is operationally important. A candidate who says "no ADS-B = grounded" misses it.                                                                                               |
| Says the maintenance logbooks have to be in the airplane   | Wrong. 91.417 records are kept by the owner; only the AROW documents (91.203 + 91.9) must be aboard.                                                                                                  |
| Doesn't cite 91.7 as the pilot's continuing duty           | The 91.7(b) responsibility is the bridge between the maintenance signature and the actual flight. A complete answer names it.                                                                         |
| Conflates 91.405 with 91.7                                 | 91.405 is the owner/operator's responsibility for *maintaining* the airplane. 91.7 is the pilot's responsibility for *contemporaneous* airworthiness. Different actors.                               |
| Says all maintenance records have to be kept forever       | Wrong. 91.417(b)(1) is 1-year for routine entries. (b)(2) is perpetual for total time, ADs, life-limited parts, Form 337s.                                                                            |
| Cites only AROW and not 91.9                               | 91.203 covers A and R; 91.9 covers O and W. Cite both.                                                                                                                                                |

## Variant prompts

| Variant                                                                                                                   | What changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "Same question, but VFR night instead of IFR"                                                                             | Drop the 91.205(d) GRABCARD additions and 91.411 currency. Keep 91.413, ELT, annual, AD. The night lights ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/209?at=2026)) become operational rules.                                          |
| "Same question, but the airplane is a flight school airplane used for instruction with the school providing the airplane" | Add 91.409(b) 100-hour. Add the 100-hour vs. annual interaction (an IA-signed annual resets both clocks).                                                                                                                      |
| "Same question, but the airplane has no ADS-B Out installed"                                                              | Add 91.225(g) deviation pathway. Discuss the 1-hour pre-flight notice and the route planning to minimize rule airspace.                                                                                                        |
| "Same question, but the airplane just had a major maintenance event last week"                                            | Add the 91.407 walkthrough -- (a) approval-for-return-to-service signed; (b) test flight required if work appreciably changed flight characteristics; (c) ground tests as alternative.                                         |
| "Same question, but the airplane is being purchased today and the buyer is the renter"                                    | Add 91.419 records transfer. The seller transfers (a)(2) perpetual records and the (a)(1) non-superseded records. The buyer's records review at closing is essential.                                                          |
| "Same question, but the inop item is the right alternator-out warning light, not a fuel gauge"                            | Walk 91.213(d) and reach a different result -- the warning light may not be in 91.205, but the KOEL likely lists it. Step 2 binds. Compare with the cabin map light (deferrable) and the fuel quantity gauge (not deferrable). |
