# Week 5 drills

Week 5 lessons walked subpart C (equipment) and subpart E (maintenance). The drills exercise the operational question that defines this week:

> **Given this aircraft and this op, what's required, what's currency-current, and what isn't?**

The drills come in three formats:

1. **Locate** -- given a question, name the section in seconds
2. **Scenario** -- 20 specific aircraft / operation pairs; identify what's required and what's missing
3. **Trap detector** -- common misreadings to correct on sight

Format 1 -> navigation flash cards in apps/study. Format 2 -> scenario cards. Format 3 -> misconception-correction cards.

## Format 2 -- 20 scenarios

For each: state what is required, what is currency-current, and what (if anything) blocks the flight.

### Scenario 1

> A 1976 C172 owned by a private individual. VFR day flight at 11 a.m. Annual signed 6 months ago. 411 expired 3 months ago. 413 expired 3 months ago. ELT 12-month inspection valid. Pilot rated and current.

Required: AROW documents (91.203 + 91.9), 91.205(b) ATOMATOFLAMES, 91.207 ELT, 91.213 review of any inop items, 91.215 transponder operating in any 91.215(b) airspace transited.

Currency: Annual current (91.409(a)). 411 expired -> *no IFR* but VFR is OK. 413 expired -> transponder cannot be operated per 91.413(a); flight in 91.215(b) airspace requires 91.215(d) deviation.

Blocker: If route transits 91.215(b) airspace (Class B/C/Mode C veil), needs deviation. If route stays in pure Class E away from veils, legal.

### Scenario 2

> The same airplane, but VFR night flight at 8 p.m. Same currencies. Same equipment status.

Required: 91.205(b) + (c) -- VFR day items plus position lights, anticollision (if installed), spare fuses (if airplane uses fuses), adequate electrical source. Plus 91.209 lights in sunset-to-sunrise window.

Currency: Annual OK. 411 not relevant for VFR. 413 expired -> same transponder issue.

Blocker: Position and anticollision lights must be operating (91.209(a) and (d)). Plus the transponder issue.

### Scenario 3

> The same airplane, but IFR cross-country in IMC. Same currencies. Same equipment status.

Required: 91.205(b) + (c) (if night) + (d) -- full IFR equipment list. Plus 91.411 altimeter / static / encoder current. Plus 91.413 transponder current. Plus 91.225 ADS-B Out if route transits ADS-B rule airspace.

Currency: 411 expired -> **flight is illegal under 91.411(a)**. 413 expired -> transponder unusable.

Blocker: 91.411 expiration grounds the IFR flight. Legal IFR is not available until both 411 and 413 are restored.

### Scenario 4

> A flight school's C152 used for primary training. Annual signed 8 months ago. 100-hour signed at 1240 hours of time-in-service. Airplane is now at 1339.4 hours. Trainee booked for a 1.0 hour flight.

Required: 91.409(a) annual + 91.409(b) 100-hour. The school provides the airplane and instruction is for hire -> 91.409(b) applies.

Currency: Annual valid (8 months in). 100-hour math: 1339.4 - 1240 = 99.4. Next 100-hour at 1340.0 hours.

Blocker: A 1.0-hour flight will end at 1340.4 -> 0.4 hours over the 100-hour interval. Cannot dispatch this flight. Either dispatch a shorter flight (≤ 0.6 hr) or do the 100-hour first.

### Scenario 5

> Same flight school. Same C152. The school just arranged a CFI to come teach the school's owner in the school owner's *personal* C172 (a different airplane the owner owns separately). Does 91.409(b) apply to the C172?

Required for the C172: 91.409(a) annual.

Currency: 91.409(b) does NOT apply to the C172 in this case -- the instructor is teaching for hire, but in the *student's* airplane. The instructor is not "providing the aircraft." Annual still applies.

Blocker: None on this front (91.409(b) doesn't apply).

### Scenario 6

> A 1992 Cirrus SR22 with no MEL. The cabin map light bulb is burned out. VFR day flight planned. KOEL doesn't list cabin map light. AD search clean. No other Part 91 rule binds.

Required: Run 91.213(d) four-step check.

Process: 91.205 -- not listed (NO). KOEL -- not listed (NO). AD -- none (NO). Other Part 91 rule for VFR day -- none (NO). Defer.

Action: Deactivate or remove the bulb. Placard the switch INOPERATIVE (visible to pilot). Make the maintenance entry per 14 CFR 43.9. Pilot determines no hazard.

Blocker: None after the deferral process. Legal.

### Scenario 7

> Same Cirrus, same flight, same situation -- but the inop item is the right fuel quantity gauge. KOEL lists the right fuel quantity gauge as required for VFR day, VFR night, and IFR.

Process: 91.213(d) Step 2 -- KOEL lists the gauge as required (YES). Cannot defer.

Blocker: Fix the gauge before flight. The KOEL traps a 91.205-level deferral attempt.

### Scenario 8

> Same Cirrus, same flight planned VFR day. The slip-skid indicator on the PFD shows "INVALID."

Process: 91.205(b) does NOT list a slip-skid indicator (it's only in (d)(4) for IFR). Step 1 -> NO. Step 2 -> KOEL check: typical Cirrus KOEL lists the slip-skid as required for IFR but not VFR day.

Blocker: VFR day legal with the deferral process. IFR is not.

### Scenario 9

> A 1946 Champ (no engine-driven electrical system, no transponder). Flight is pattern work at a private grass strip 22 nm inside the SFO Mode C veil. Pattern stays at 1,200 ft AGL. The Class B floor over the strip is 4,000 ft.

Required: AROW documents, 91.205(b) ATOMATOFLAMES (no electrical system means no fuel quantity gauges that need power, but mechanical gauges are still required if installed by type design). ELT may not apply -- 91.207(e)(2) training-flight 50-nm exemption may govern if training, plus the no-electrical-system airplane provisions.

Mode C veil exemption: 91.215(b)(3) carves out aircraft not originally certificated with electrical system, operated outside Class A/B/C and below the relevant ceiling. Champ at 1,200 ft AGL under a 4,000-ft Class B floor satisfies the conditions.

ADS-B Out: equivalent carve-out applies under 91.225 (the airplane has no electrical system).

Blocker: None. Legal.

### Scenario 10

> A pilot just had their alternator replaced. Mechanic signed return to service. The pilot wants to fly with their family today.

Process: 91.407(a) -- approved for return to service: yes. 91.407(b) -- did the maintenance "appreciably change" flight characteristics? An alternator swap is electrical and does not change flight characteristics significantly. (c) ground tests confirm normal alternator output.

Action: Pilot may carry passengers. Test flight not strictly required. Best practice: a short solo test flight to confirm electrical systems are normal in flight conditions.

Blocker: Probably none. The judgment call is the pilot's.

### Scenario 11

> Same pilot, but the work was an engine replacement (new engine, not just a top overhaul -- the original engine was removed, a different one installed).

Process: 91.407(a) signed -- yes. 91.407(b) -- a new engine appreciably changes flight characteristics. Test flight required before passenger carriage.

Action: Solo (or crew-only) test flight. Log as post-maintenance test flight. Then the family flight.

Blocker: Test flight first.

### Scenario 12

> A Cirrus SR22 IFR cross-country planned. Annual signed 4 months ago. 411 signed 14 months ago. 413 signed 14 months ago. ELT 12-month signed 14 months ago. ADS-B Out installed and tested 18 months ago.

Required: All currencies for IFR.

Currency: Annual OK. 411 -- 14 months in, valid for 24 calendar months. OK. 413 -- 14 months in. OK. ELT -- 14 months in -- **expired** (12 calendar months under 91.207(d)). ADS-B Out -- no specific 24-month check on ADS-B Out itself (the 411 covers data correspondence).

Blocker: ELT inspection expired. The pilot cannot operate the airplane (period -- it's not just IFR) without addressing the ELT. Either complete the 12-month inspection or use the 91.207(e)/(f) exemptions if applicable. For a non-training cross-country, no exemption applies.

### Scenario 13

> A 1985 Piper Archer at a flight school. Annual signed 90 days ago. 100-hour signed at 1500 hours. Airplane now at 1605 hours. Owner is 5 hours past the 100-hour interval. Owner argues the 91.409(b) 10-hour overrun lets them dispatch student flights.

Process: The 10-hour overrun is *only for ferry to a maintenance base*. It is not for revenue or instructional use. The airplane is in 91.409(b) violation for any student flight at this point.

Blocker: 100-hour overdue. Ground until inspection completed. The 5 hours over count against the next interval.

### Scenario 14

> A pilot in a Mode-C-equipped Cessna 172 (no ADS-B Out) wants to fly from KCCR (in SFO Mode C veil) to a small uncontrolled airport in the central valley. Route at 4,500 ft, never enters Class B/C, but route does cross the SFO Mode C veil for the first 25 nm.

Required: 91.215 Mode C in the veil -- airplane has it. 91.225 ADS-B Out in the veil -- airplane does NOT have it.

Blocker: Without ADS-B Out, the flight needs a 91.225(g) deviation request submitted at least 1 hour before departure to the controlling ATC facility. Once granted, the flight is legal.

### Scenario 15

> A pilot's ADS-B Out fails 30 minutes into a flight. Currently at 7,500 ft MSL in Class E, en route to a Class C airport.

Process: 91.225(g)(1) allows an inflight deviation request "at any time" for an inop ADS-B Out. ATC notified, deviation granted for the rest of the flight.

Blocker: None (with ATC accommodation). The transponder remains operating; ATC retains radar contact. If the destination Class C is reached, the deviation covers entry.

### Scenario 16

> A C172 with the right fuel quantity gauge inoperative. KOEL lists the right gauge as required. Pilot suggests just using the dipstick to verify fuel before takeoff and runs 91.213(d) anyway.

Process: 91.213(d) Step 2 (KOEL) blocks deferral. The dipstick-substitution argument is not a valid deferral pathway -- the regulation is binary on the gauge.

Blocker: Fix the gauge. No 91.213(d) pathway available.

### Scenario 17

> A pilot is teaching a paid student in the student's airplane (the student owns the airplane; the pilot is hired as a CFI). Annual is 11 months in. The airplane has 145 hours since the last 100-hour was performed (the student opted to do them voluntarily for resale value). Today is the dual flight.

Required: 91.409(a) annual.

Currency: Annual OK. 91.409(b) does NOT apply (instructor is not providing the airplane).

Blocker: None on the inspection regime. The airplane's voluntary 100-hour status is not regulatory; the pilot can fly past 100 hours as long as the annual is current.

### Scenario 18

> A renter is given keys to a C172. They open the airframe logbook to verify the annual. The most recent entry is dated 11 months ago. They check the airworthiness certificate -- it's posted on the back of the rear-seat headrest, legible.

Required: AROW documents (91.203 + 91.9). Annual currency (91.409(a)).

Currency: Annual valid. Airworthiness certificate properly displayed per 91.203(a)(1).

Blocker: None on the renter's check. Other items (411/413/ELT) need separate verification.

### Scenario 19

> A pilot launches a flight at 4:30 a.m. (sunset was 9 p.m. previous day, sunrise is 6:48 a.m.). Position lights work, anticollision lights work, landing light burned out. Flight is private VFR.

Required: 91.209(a) position lights from sunset to sunrise -- airplane has them. 91.209(d) anticollision lights -- airplane has them. Landing light not required by 91.205(c) for private operations.

Currency: All lights compliant.

Blocker: None. Legal.

### Scenario 20

> A pilot purchased a C182 from a dealer 2 weeks ago. They are now reviewing the records and notice that AD 2018-23-04 (a fuel cap modification) shows compliance dated 4 years ago, but no Form 337 is in the records, and the buyer cannot find evidence the fuel cap was actually replaced.

Required: 91.403(a) places AD compliance on the owner. The buyer is now the owner. 91.417(a)(2)(v) requires the records reflect AD compliance.

Process: The buyer should contact the seller for documentation, contact the A&P/IA who signed the original entry, and verify physical compliance. If compliance cannot be verified, the buyer must comply with the AD before further flight (the AD's compliance method governs; it may require the cap replacement, or it may be a one-time inspection).

Blocker: The airplane is not airworthy until AD compliance is confirmed and documented. This is the single most common pre-purchase records issue and the reason the records review before closing is essential under 91.419.

## Format 3 -- Trap detector

For each statement, identify the misreading and correct it.

| Statement                                                                       | Trap and correction                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "AROW is in 91.203."                                                            | Half-true. 91.203 covers A and R. O and W are in [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/9?at=2026). Cite both.                                                                                                     |
| "Anticollision lights only required at night."                                  | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/209?at=2026)(d) requires them whenever operating, day or night, if installed.                                                                                        |
| "The Mode C veil and the ADS-B rule airspace are the same thing."               | Almost. Differences exist (Class E above 10,000 MSL, Gulf of Mexico carve-out). Operationally similar but not identical.                                                                                       |
| "If I placard an inop item INOPERATIVE, I can fly with it."                     | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d) is a four-step process. Placard is step 3, after clearing the four no-defer authorities and before the hazard determination.                         |
| "The 100-hour applies to every airplane used for training."                     | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(b) applies when *the instructor provides the aircraft*. CFI in student's personal airplane: no 100-hour.                                                |
| "After maintenance, the mechanic's signature is enough to fly with passengers." | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/407?at=2026)(a) covers signoff; (b) requires a test flight without passengers when work may have appreciably changed flight characteristics.                         |
| "91.411 covers the transponder."                                                | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026) covers altimeter / static / encoder. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026) covers the transponder unit. They overlap on data correspondence.  |
| "91.413 only applies to IFR."                                                   | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026) applies to any operation with an installed transponder. VFR pilots are bound.                                                                           |
| "All maintenance records have to be kept forever."                              | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/417?at=2026)(b)(1) is 1-year for routine entries. (b)(2) is perpetual for total time, AD status, life-limited parts, Form 337s.                                      |
| "The seller can keep the maintenance logs as a souvenir of their old airplane." | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/419?at=2026) requires transfer at sale. The seller hands them to the buyer.                                                                                          |
| "ATOMATOFLAMES requires a landing light for VFR night."                         | Wrong. The L in FLAPS is recommended but [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026)(c)(2) requires it only "if the aircraft is operated for hire." Private night flying does not require a landing light. |
| "AD compliance is the mechanic's job."                                          | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/403?at=2026)(a) places AD compliance on the owner/operator. The mechanic does the work; the owner is responsible.                                                    |
| "The 100-hour 10-hour overrun lets me operate to 110 hours."                    | Wrong. The 10 hours is for *ferry* to a maintenance base. Not for student flights or revenue use.                                                                                                              |
| "If I'm under 50 nm from the airport, I don't need an ELT."                     | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/207?at=2026)(e)(2) is for *training flights* within 50 nm. A non-training A-to-B flight at 40 nm is not exempt.                                                      |
| "Position lights are required from civil twilight."                             | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/209?at=2026)(a) is sunset-to-sunrise. Civil twilight is the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/51?at=2026) logging concept.                                          |
| "ADS-B Out is required for any IFR flight."                                     | Wrong. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/225?at=2026) is airspace-based, not flight-rules-based. IFR in pure Class E below 10,000 MSL with no Class B/C transitions: ADS-B Out not required.                  |

## Hardcover drill -- 90 seconds, no notes

Integration. Read the prompt, give the answer in 90 seconds.

1. **List the inspection currencies for a typical Part 91 GA airplane used for personal flying with no for-hire ops.** -> Annual ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(a), 12 calendar months). 411 ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026), 24 calendar months, IFR only). 413 ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026), 24 calendar months, all ops if transponder installed). ELT 12-month inspection ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/207?at=2026)(d)). AD compliance per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/403?at=2026)(a). No 100-hour (not for hire, not instruction-for-hire by the owner-instructor).

2. **Walk the no-MEL pathway in [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/213?at=2026)(d).** -> Step 1: required by 91.205? Step 2: required by KOEL or aircraft equipment list? Step 3: required by AD? Step 4: required by other Part 91 rule for this op? If all four NO, deferral available. Process: deactivate or remove the item; placard the cockpit control INOPERATIVE; record per 14 CFR 43.9; pilot or mechanic determines no hazard.

3. **Distinguish [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/413?at=2026).** -> 411 is altimeter / static system / encoder, 24 calendar months, *IFR in controlled airspace* only. 413 is transponder only, 24 calendar months, all operations with an installed transponder. The 411 data correspondence test in (a)(3) overlaps with the 413(b) re-test after maintenance.

4. **What does the seller transfer to the buyer at sale?** -> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/419?at=2026) requires transfer of the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/417?at=2026)(a)(2) records (perpetual: total time, life-limited parts, AD status, inspection status, Form 337s) plus the (a)(1) records not yet superseded. Practically: airframe / engine / propeller logbooks, Form 337s, AD compliance records, recent 411/413/100-hour/annual entries.

5. **What's the Mode C / ADS-B status for an airplane with a working Mode S transponder, current 413, but no ADS-B Out installed, planning a route through SFO's Mode C veil at 4,500 ft (Class B floor at 4,000 ft above the route)?** -> 91.215 satisfied (Mode C transponder operating in the veil). 91.225 *not* satisfied (ADS-B Out required in Mode C veil). Pilot needs 91.225(g) deviation requested at least 1 hour before flight. Without deviation, the flight is illegal.
