# Week 10 -- Capstone, integrated oral

The integration week. Nine weeks of front-to-back walks, deep dives, drills, and weekly orals all converge here. By Friday the learner can answer any reasonable CFI-level FAR question without notes.

This is also a reflective week. The lessons run lighter than the deep weeks because the work is no longer about acquiring new content -- it is about exercising the structural map that has been built up over Weeks 1-9 and noticing the shape of what is missing.

## What you'll be able to do by end of week

- Deliver the four canonical capstone orals in [orals/](../orals/) without notes, in order, at oral-exam pace
- Switch between sibling variants without losing the regulatory chain
- Self-assess which subparts in your Title 14 map are sparse, and act on the gap
- Run a partner exam against another learner using the rubric, and have the partner do the same back to you

## The four canonical capstone orals

The spine of this week. Each is a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61?at=2026)-and-[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91?at=2026)-spanning integration that pulls from multiple weeks. Read each before the partner exam; deliver each without notes during the partner exam.

| Gear-up landing on a night IFR XC   | [orals/gear-up-night-ifr.md](../orals/gear-up-night-ifr.md)       | 91.3, 91.7, 91.13, 91.405, 91.407, 91.417, 49 CFR 830, PBR2, 61.51, 61.193                                          |
| CFI giving a friend's flight review | [orals/friend-flight-review.md](../orals/friend-flight-review.md) | 61.56, 61.57, 61.31, 61.59, 61.193, 61.195, 61.197, AC 61-65, AC 61-98                                              |
| 250-hour PPL applies for the IR     | [orals/ppl-applies-for-ir.md](../orals/ppl-applies-for-ir.md)     | 61.65, 61.51, 61.57, 61.31, 61.49, 61.23, 61.189, 91.169, AC 61-65, AC 61-98                                        |

The four are *siblings*, not duplicates. Each exercises a different mode of integration:

- **Night IFR with a passenger** -- pre-flight regulatory check across the full Title 14 stack
- **Gear-up landing** -- post-incident, multi-agency, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/3?at=2026) emergency authority into 49 CFR 830 reporting
- **Friend flight review** -- CFI-side authority, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) social-pressure refusal, AC 61-65 endorsement discipline
- **PPL applies for IR** -- qualifications counting, [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/65?at=2026) sub-requirement nesting, simulator substitution, endorsement timing

A learner who can deliver all four cleanly has the structural map.

## Reading

No new reading. Re-read [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91?at=2026) subpart A (general). Re-cite from memory: [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/3?at=2026) (PIC authority), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/13?at=2026) (careless or reckless), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) (CFI privileges), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) (CFI limitations). The aim is durability of the bedrock -- a working pilot recites these in their sleep, not by reaching for a phone.

## Drills

[drills.md](drills.md) -- 50 prompts, 60 seconds each, mixed across Weeks 1-9. The drill is timed because oral exams are timed. The drill measures *navigation-under-pressure*, not deep knowledge -- a learner who can name the section in 60 seconds can recall the substance from there.

## Oral

[oral.md](oral.md) is the exam pointer. The actual exam is the four sibling capstones in [orals/](../orals/), delivered to a partner who grades against the rubric in [01-how-the-capstone-works.md](01-how-the-capstone-works.md).
