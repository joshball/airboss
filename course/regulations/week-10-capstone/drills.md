# Week 10 drills

A single timed mixed drill, 50 prompts, 60 seconds each. The prompts pull from any of Weeks 1-9 without warning. The drill measures *navigation under pressure* -- whether the structural map of Title 14 holds up when the question is unpredictable.

Run the drill in one continuous 50-minute session. Do not pause between prompts. Do not look up answers in the middle. Mark each prompt as a hit (answered correctly within 60 seconds), miss (could not answer in time), or wrong (answered incorrectly).

After the session, score: hits / 50 = drill rate. Target: 40/50 (80%) at oral-test-ready level. 35-39/50 is on the edge. Below 35 means the structural map has gaps that need targeted work before the partner exam.

## Format

Cover the right column. Time yourself per row. The drill is *not* "answer the question fully" -- it is "name the section / source / answer in 60 seconds."

## Scoring

Count hits. Target: 40+ correct in the 50-minute window.

| Score    | Diagnosis                                                                                                                                                 |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 45-50    | Oral-test-ready. The structural map is solid. Spend remaining time on variant-prompt depth, not navigation drills.                                        |
| 40-44    | Strong. The map has 5-10 thin spots. Re-read the relevant week deep-dive for each missed prompt; re-drill in 24-48 hours.                                 |
| 35-39    | On the edge. The map has gaps that surface under pressure. Schedule another full pass before the partner exam; do not skip the re-drill.                  |
| 30-34    | Below ready. Several subparts are still memorized as lists rather than integrated into a structural map. Return to the deep weeks for the missed regions. |
| Under 30 | Not ready. The capstone scenarios will collapse under examiner thread-pulling. Spend a week in deep-dive mode before attempting partner exams.            |

## Why 60 seconds

The 60-second window is the working CFI's calibration. In a real oral, the examiner expects an answer to a navigation prompt within about a minute. A candidate who needs three minutes to recall a section number is going to run out of time on the harder integration questions.

The drill is *not* a knowledge test. It is a *speed* test on knowledge that has already been built. A learner who consistently misses prompts in the drill has not built the knowledge yet; the drill is feedback, not instruction.

## Variant: scenario drill

For deeper rehearsal, swap the navigation drill for a scenario drill. Format: the partner reads a short fact pattern, the candidate names every regulation that applies, in order, with one sentence per regulation. Time: 5 minutes per scenario. Sample fact patterns:

| Fact pattern                                                                                                         | Approximate regulation chain                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 200-hour PPL, 18-month-old flight review, friend on board, 172, day VFR, 80 nm.                                      | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(a), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/103?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/151?at=2026) (VFR weather, abbreviated), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026)(b), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/409?at=2026)(a) |
| Commercial pilot, no IR, wants to take a paying passenger 60 nm at night.                                            | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/133?at=2026) (50nm day VFR limit), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/205?at=2026)(c)                                                                                                            |
| 250 PIC XC, 41 instrument hours total, 10 hours from a CFII, no recent CFII work, wants the IR practical next month. | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/65?at=2026)(d): missing 5 hours from CFII (need 15), missing 3 hours within 2 cal months in airplane with CFII, possibly missing the long XC. Audit.                                                                                                                                                |
| Aircraft owner discovers altimeter inspection is 25 cal months old. Wants to fly today, day VFR.                     | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/411?at=2026) is IFR-only -> can fly day VFR. Confirm via 91.411 reading. | You are CFI. Student lands hard at night, no damage, but tail tiedown ring is bent.                                  | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/7?at=2026) (airworthiness call), 49 CFR 830.2 (probably not substantial damage; not an accident), [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/91/417?at=2026) (logbook entry on the discrepancy), no NTSB notification needed.                                                                                  |

The scenario drill is the bridge between the timed navigation drill and the four full capstone orals. Use it when the navigation drill is consistently 45+/50 and the four-scenario rehearsal is still rough.
