# Week 10 oral -- the four canonical capstones

## What this is

This file is a pointer. The Week 10 oral exam is *not* a single oral. It is the four sibling capstone orals authored under [orals/](../orals/), delivered in sequence to a partner who grades against the rubric in [01-how-the-capstone-works.md](01-how-the-capstone-works.md).

The four scenarios are different *modes of integration*, not different *subsets of regulations*. A learner who can deliver all four cleanly has demonstrated the structural map of Title 14 from four angles -- pre-flight, post-incident, CFI-side authority, and qualifications counting.

Total: roughly 100 minutes of spoken oral content, plus examiner-thread-pull and partner-feedback time per scenario. A full delivery session is about 3 hours including breaks.

## How to use this file

The Week 10 oral is run in three modes. Pick whichever matches your situation:

**Mode 1 -- Solo rehearsal.** Read each capstone file. Deliver each scenario to camera. Re-watch and self-score against the rubric in [01-how-the-capstone-works.md](01-how-the-capstone-works.md). Write a reflection note per [03-self-assessment-and-reflection.md](03-self-assessment-and-reflection.md). Cycle.

**Mode 2 -- Partner exam.** Pair with another learner. One plays examiner, one plays candidate. Deliver, score, swap roles. See [01-how-the-capstone-works.md](01-how-the-capstone-works.md) for the partner-exam mechanics.

**Mode 3 -- DPE-style mock.** Find a CFII or experienced CFI willing to play examiner. Run the four scenarios in a single session, with breaks. Treat the session as the rehearsal for the actual practical test. The DPE-style mock is the most expensive in time and external dependency, and the most calibrated.

A learner who has not yet read all four scenario files in full should do that before any mode. The rubric depends on knowing what the model answer looks like; without that reference, the partner exam is unscored conversation.

## What this oral does not include

- A single integrated prompt -- the four scenarios are separate, by design
- A model answer -- each scenario file contains its own model answer
- A score sheet -- the rubric in [01-how-the-capstone-works.md](01-how-the-capstone-works.md) is the score sheet

## Authoring posture

The Week 10 oral exists as a pointer rather than a self-contained file because the four scenarios are *the* exam. Rewriting them inline here would either duplicate the canonical files or compress them in ways that lose the model-answer fidelity. Pointing is the more durable design.

If a future revision adds a fifth or sixth canonical scenario, this file is the place to update the order and the table. The scenarios themselves stay self-contained under [orals/](../orals/).
