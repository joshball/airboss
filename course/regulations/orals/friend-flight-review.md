# A friend asks you to give them their flight review

## The question

> "A friend of yours -- current PPL, current Class 3 medical, current renter at your school -- texts you Tuesday and asks if you'd give them their flight review next Saturday. Walk me through every regulatory check, every conversation, every signature, and every thing you absolutely do not do. They're a friend. The CFI cert is yours."

This is a CFI-side capstone. The pilot side of the relationship is easy: §61.56 and the calendar. The CFI side is the regulated side -- §61.193, §61.195, §61.197, AC 61-65, AC 61-98, the §61.59 falsification trap, the §61.51 logging trap, and the social pressure that makes friends ask for things they wouldn't ask a stranger. The skill being tested is whether you can be a friend and a CFI simultaneously without compounding either role.

## What this is testing

- Whether you treat §61.56 as the regulation it is, not as a courtesy ritual
- Whether you handle CFI-side authority and limitations under §61.193 / §61.195 cleanly
- Whether you check your own §61.197 expiration before signing
- Whether you write the §61.51 / AC 61-65 endorsement in a CFI-defensible way
- Whether you refuse to backdate, retro-sign, or otherwise touch §61.59 territory
- Whether you understand the standard is "satisfactorily completed" -- not a checkride pass, not a recommendation
- Whether you sense the social-pressure failure mode and have a script for it

## Model answer (full walkthrough)

A defensible answer has eight stages. Order matters: the pre-agreement checks are the gating ones. Skip them and the rest of the conversation is built on a flawed foundation.

### Stage 1: Before agreeing -- check your own CFI cert

Per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026), a flight instructor certificate is valid for 24 calendar months from the month of issue (or last renewal). Before saying yes, you check:

- Your CFI cert expiration date
- Whether you hold the airplane category and class for the airplane you'll fly
- Whether you currently meet [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) limitations -- recent flight time, no certificate suspension, etc.

> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) **(b)** A flight instructor certificate that has not been renewed expires.

> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) **(h)** A flight instructor may not conduct flight training in any aircraft for which the flight instructor does not hold ... (1) A pilot certificate with the applicable category and class rating; and (2) A flight instructor certificate with the applicable category and class rating.

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "Before I say yes, I check my own logbook. My CFI expires August 31, 2027 -- I'm good. I'm CFI-A, the airplane is a 172, fine. I've flown the 172 in the last 90 days, so I'm not walking in cold. If any of those answers were no, I'd say no to the request, not just 'we'll figure it out.' A CFI who signs an endorsement on a lapsed cert hasn't 'almost helped'; they've made the pilot's review invalid and exposed themselves to a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) violation."

**Common pitfall:** CFIs whose cert renewed last week but the new card hasn't arrived in the mail. Per [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026), the renewal practical (or FIRC graduation, or commercial-pilot proficiency check) creates the new cert; the card is paperwork. But the CFI must be able to *prove* the renewal happened. Email confirmations from a FIRC, the IACRA temporary, the SOE practical results -- keep them.

### Stage 2: What §61.56 actually requires

Quote the regulation, do not paraphrase:

> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) **(a)** Except as provided in paragraphs (b) and (f) of this section, a flight review consists of a minimum of 1 hour of flight training and 1 hour of ground training. The review must include ---
>
> (1) A review of the current general operating and flight rules of part 91; and
>
> (2) A review of those maneuvers and procedures that, at the discretion of the person giving the review, are necessary for the pilot to demonstrate the safe exercise of the privileges of the pilot certificate.
>
> ...
>
> **(c)** Except as provided in paragraphs (d), (e), (f), and (g) of this section, no person may act as pilot in command of an aircraft unless, since the beginning of the 24th calendar month before the month in which that pilot acts as pilot in command of an aircraft, that person has ---
>
> (1) Accomplished a flight review given in an aircraft for which that pilot is rated by an authorized instructor; and
>
> (2) A logbook endorsement from an authorized instructor who gave the review certifying that the person has satisfactorily completed the review.

**Plain reading:** 1 hour of ground, 1 hour of flight, in an aircraft the pilot is rated for, with an authorized instructor. The review covers Part 91 plus whatever maneuvers the CFI judges necessary. Ends with a logbook endorsement saying "satisfactorily completed."

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) is short. One hour ground, one hour flight, minimum. Part 91 review and maneuvers at my discretion. The endorsement says 'satisfactorily completed.' That's the floor; AC 61-98 is the recommended content and the FSDO de-facto standard. The standard isn't 'pass' the way a checkride is pass; it's 'satisfactorily completed in my judgment.' I'm the gate."

**Common pitfall:** Treating the flight review like a sport-pilot endorsement -- "you flew with me, you're good." [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) requires both ground *and* flight portions. Both have to actually happen. A coffee-shop chat and a Saturday flight is not a flight review unless the coffee-shop chat actually covered Part 91.

### Stage 3: Pre-flight: their records

Before agreeing on a date, ask the friend to send you photos of:

- Their pilot certificate (front and back)
- Their medical certificate / BasicMed records
- The relevant pages of their logbook -- last review date, last 90 days, last 12 months
- Their renter checkout / club currency status if applicable

Why each one matters:

| Document           | What you're checking                                                                                                     | Where it lives in the regs                                            |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Pilot certificate  | Cert level, ratings, endorsements                                                                                        | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/3?at=2026)                         |
| Medical / BasicMed | Currency for the cert level                                                                                              | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/23?at=2026), Part 67, 49 USC 44703 |
| Last review date   | When the current 24-cal-month window closes                                                                              | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(c)                     |
| Last 90 days       | Whether they're [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(a) recent for the takeoffs and landings during the review | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)                        |
| Last 12 months     | How rusty the pilot is in practice (proficiency signal)                                                                  | (not regulatory)                                                      |

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "I ask for photos of the cert, the medical, and the relevant logbook pages before I agree to a date. Two reasons. One, I want to know what I'm signing for -- the endorsement names the cert and rating, and I have to be giving the review for an airplane the pilot is rated in. Two, the logbook tells me how long it's been since they actually flew. The eight-month gap matters -- not for [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026), but for what AC 61-98 is going to ask me to cover. A pilot 8 months out is going to need more than 1+1; AC 61-98 recommends 2-3 hours total when the AC elements are honored."

**Common pitfall:** Skipping the document review because it feels formal between friends. The document review is exactly what the FSDO would do if your endorsement is later questioned. If you can't reproduce the records you saw, you're left arguing from memory.

### Stage 4: Plot twist 1 -- "I haven't flown in 8 months"

The friend mentions in passing that their last flight was 8 months ago. What changes?

Nothing in the regulation. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) does not have a recency-of-flight floor. A pilot who hasn't flown in 5 years can still receive a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)-compliant flight review.

What does change: AC 61-98 explicitly recommends scaling the review's depth to the pilot's recency. AC 61-98D notes that a pilot returning after a long lapse should expect a more thorough review -- multiple sessions, more flight content, additional ground topics. The "satisfactorily completed" gate is the CFI's professional judgment. A 1+1 review for a pilot 8 months out is *technically* compliant and probably not responsible.

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "8 months out doesn't change the rule. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) is still 1+1 minimum, satisfactorily completed. What it changes is my expectation. AC 61-98 recommends 2-3 hours when the AC elements are honored, and I'd tell my friend up front: 'this is going to be more like 4-5 hours of flying spread over two days, plus a longer ground portion. We're going to do basic maneuvers, slow flight, stalls, an emergency, a diversion, and probably a partial-panel scan since you're instrument-rated. If you're satisfied at the end, I sign. If I'm not satisfied, I don't sign and we keep going.' Setting that expectation Tuesday saves the awkward conversation Saturday afternoon."

**Common pitfall:** Letting the friend frame the review as "just a quick refresh." The CFI sets the scope. That's [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(a)(2): "at the discretion of the person giving the review."

### Stage 5: Plot twist 2 -- the medical lapses 3 weeks after

You're looking at the medical photo. It's a Class 3, third-class issued April 1, 2024. Today is April 29, 2026 -- the friend is over 40, so the third-class is valid for 24 calendar months for private privileges, which means it expires April 30, 2026 (next day). The proposed flight review date is Saturday, May 9.

The medical is invalid on May 9. The pilot cannot legally act as PIC or sole occupant of the airplane on May 9 without a current medical or BasicMed.

But: does the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review require the pilot to have a current medical? The answer is more textured than people expect.

[@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/23?at=2026) requires a medical to *act as PIC or required crewmember*. During a flight review, the CFI is acting as PIC (per the Hicks letter, 2010, the CFI manipulating controls is acting as PIC; even when not manipulating, the CFI giving instruction is in a position to intervene). The pilot receiving the review is *not required to be PIC* -- they're receiving instruction. So a pilot whose medical has lapsed *can* receive a flight review with a CFI on board acting as PIC.

But the pilot still cannot act as PIC the moment the review ends. The endorsement [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) gives them does not refresh their medical. The pilot needs both a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) endorsement *and* a current [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/23?at=2026) medical to act as PIC.

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "The medical lapses May 1. Our flight is May 9. So I'm going to be PIC for the review per Hicks -- the friend can receive instruction without a current medical. But the moment we land, the friend cannot legally act as PIC. The flight review endorsement doesn't fix that. So I tell them: 'Get the medical renewed before May 9, or get a BasicMed exam done before May 9, or know that the endorsement I'm going to write doesn't make you legal to fly without me. Most clean-and-simple option: book the AME for next week, before our flight. Then the medical is current, your flight review endorsement is valid for 24 calendar months, and you can fly Saturday afternoon as PIC.' That's the conversation Tuesday, not Saturday morning."

**Common pitfall:** Assuming a medical lapse blocks the review. It does not, *if the CFI is PIC*. But the pilot post-review can't fly solo until the medical is current. Many CFIs miss this and tell the friend the review is impossible; that's a regulatory misread that costs the friend a week.

### Stage 6: Plot twist 3 -- the complex aircraft

Friend asks if you can do the review in a Bonanza they want to start renting. They haven't flown the Bonanza before. They don't have a high-performance endorsement (Bonanza A36 is 285 hp -> needs the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026)(f) high-performance endorsement). They also don't have a complex endorsement (Bonanza has retract gear, controllable prop, flaps -> needs [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026)(e)).

Two separate problems:

1. **The pilot is not legal to act as PIC of the Bonanza.** [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026)(e)(1) and (f)(1) require the relevant endorsement before acting as PIC. So the flight review *cannot* be the pilot's first time at the controls in this airplane, because the pilot needs the endorsements first.

2. **The flight review does happen "in an aircraft for which that pilot is rated"** ([@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(c)(1)). Category and class -- ASEL. The Bonanza is ASEL, so the airplane is rated. But the *endorsements* aren't there.

Solution: do the high-performance + complex training first, write those endorsements *separately* under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026), and then the flight review can occur in the Bonanza. Or do the flight review in a 172 the friend is already current in.

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "The Bonanza is two endorsements -- complex under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026)(e), high-performance under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026)(f). Those are training records, not just signatures. AC 61-65 has the recommended language for both. We can do all three in the same training event -- complex training, HP training, then the flight review -- but the endorsements are separate items in the logbook with separate dates. I'm not going to wrap them into the flight review endorsement; that's sloppy and it's how AC 61-65 deviation gets noticed in audits. Cleaner: do the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) training and sign those first. Then the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review at the end. Three endorsements, three signatures, one logbook entry per."

**Common pitfall:** Bundling endorsements. The flight review endorsement (AC 61-65 Endorsement A.1) covers the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) review. It does not cover [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsements. Each [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsement is its own AC 61-65 entry (A.6 high-performance, A.5 complex, etc.). Combining them into one entry is a paperwork violation that becomes a real violation if the airplane goes off the runway and the FAA opens the logbooks.

### Stage 7: Plot twist 4 -- "Just backdate it to last month"

Friend texts the night before: "Hey, awkward question, my last review was actually March 2024 not May 2024. I lied a little. Can you just backdate the new endorsement to be effective two weeks ago? My checkout flight last month -- I told the dispatcher my review was current. I'd hate for them to find out."

Stop. This is [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) territory.

> [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) **(a)** No person may make or cause to be made any of the following:
>
> (1) Any fraudulent or intentionally false statement on any application for a certificate, rating, authorization, or duplicate thereof, issued under this part.
>
> (2) Any fraudulent or intentionally false entry in any logbook, record, or report that is required to be kept, made, or used to show compliance with any requirement for the issuance or exercise of the privileges of any certificate, rating, or authorization under this part.
>
> ...
>
> **(b)** The commission of an act prohibited under paragraph (a) of this section is a basis for ---
>
> (1) Suspending or revoking any airman certificate, flight instructor certificate, ground instructor certificate, pilot school certification, instructor certification, or examiner designation held by that person ...

Backdating an endorsement is a [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026)(a)(2) violation. Knowing it's a violation and signing anyway is intentional. Intentional [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) is grounds for revocation -- of the friend's pilot certificate *and* of your CFI certificate. There is no judgment call here. The answer is no.

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "I say no, immediately, and I don't soften it. 'I can't backdate the endorsement. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) makes it a falsification, and the FAA reads that as grounds for revocation -- yours and mine. We do the review Saturday with today's date on the endorsement. If your dispatcher asks why your records show a one-month gap from when you said the review was current, you tell them you misremembered and the new review reflects the actual date. That's the answer. I'm not going to be your friend on this; I'm going to be your CFI.' Then -- this is important -- I follow up afterward and check in. Did the friend handle it? Did they make a fresh false statement to the dispatcher to cover the original one? At some point a CFI's continued professional relationship is itself a question. But the immediate answer is the same: no, never, not for a friend, not for a job, not for any reason."

**Common pitfall:** Soft-deflecting. "I can't backdate but maybe you could ..." opens a door. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) is a hard wall. The CFI says no, plainly, and does not negotiate the terms of the backdate.

### Stage 8: The endorsement itself

The flight review is satisfactorily completed. You're signing the logbook. Use AC 61-65 Endorsement A.1 verbatim:

> **A.1 Flight review: § 61.56(a) and (c).**
>
> I certify that [First name, MI, Last name], [grade of pilot certificate], [certificate number], has satisfactorily completed a flight review of § 61.56(a) on [date].
>
> /s/ [J. J. Jones] [date] [J. J. Jones] [CFI Number] [Expiration date]

Required elements:

- Pilot's full name
- Pilot's certificate level and number
- "Satisfactorily completed a flight review of § 61.56(a)"
- Date
- CFI's signature, printed name, certificate number, expiration date

What you do *not* write:

- Maneuvers performed -- those go in your CFI training-given log, not in the endorsement
- Areas of weakness -- those go in your private notes you give the pilot, not in the endorsement
- Hours flown -- already in the flight time entry
- Ground hours covered -- already in the ground entry
- The pilot's home airport, your school's name, etc. -- not required, adds nothing

**What I'd say in the oral:**

> "AC 61-65 Endorsement A.1 verbatim. I keep a print-out in my flight bag and I copy from it. Pilot name, cert level and number, 'satisfactorily completed a flight review of § 61.56(a),' date, my signature, my CFI number, my expiration. Both the pilot's logbook and -- [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) -- my flight instructor records get an entry too: name of the pilot, date, what was given, signed. Five-year retention on the CFI side. AC 61-65 Endorsement A.1 is *recommended* language, not required, but the FSDO's de-facto standard is the AC language, so I use it."

**Common pitfall:** Inventing endorsement text. AC 61-65 has the canonical wording for a reason: it's been audited. CFIs who freelance the language regularly miss elements ("the cert number," "the expiration date") that come up in audits years later when the endorsement is needed as evidence.

## Compressed answer (under 90 seconds)

> "Eight checks. **One**: my CFI cert -- [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) -- expiration and category/class. No to the flight if either is wrong. **Two**: the regulation -- [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) -- 1+1 minimum, in-aircraft-they're-rated-for, satisfactorily completed; AC 61-98 recommends 2-3 hours of substance. **Three**: their records -- cert, medical, last 90 days, last review date. Reviewed before agreeing. **Four**: 8-month lapse doesn't change the regulation but expands AC 61-98 expectations; I set scope on Tuesday. **Five**: medical lapses May 1 -- I'm PIC under Hicks, friend can receive instruction without a current medical, but cannot act as PIC post-review without renewal. Tell them. **Six**: complex aircraft -- separate [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsements for high-performance and complex *before* the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) review can be in that airplane. Don't bundle. **Seven**: backdate request -- [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026), no, never, not negotiable. **Eight**: the endorsement -- AC 61-65 Endorsement A.1 verbatim plus my [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) instructor record. Five-year retention. The standard is satisfactorily completed in my judgment, not a checkride pass; I'm the gate."

## What goes wrong (failure modes)

| Failure mode                                                                         | Diagnosis                                                                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Skips own CFI expiration check                                                       | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) violation if expired; review is invalid even if signed                      |
| Treats the review as a courtesy, signs without 1+1 substance                         | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(a) violation; the floor is a real floor, not aspirational                    |
| Bundles complex / high-performance endorsements into the review                      | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/31?at=2026) endorsements are separate; AC 61-65 has different entry IDs (A.5, A.6, A.1)  |
| Refuses the review because the friend's medical lapses                               | Misreads the rule. The pilot can receive instruction without a current medical; they just can't act as PIC after            |
| Says "yes, I'll backdate" or "I'll just date it last week"                           | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/59?at=2026) territory; grounds for CFI revocation                                        |
| Soft-negotiates the backdate ("I can't, but ...")                                    | Same regulation, slower violation. The wall is hard.                                                                        |
| Signs without writing the [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/189?at=2026) CFI record | Instructor record requirement; 5-year retention; missing it is a separate violation                                         |
| Freelances the endorsement wording                                                   | AC 61-65 is the canonical language; deviations get noticed                                                                  |
| Treats "satisfactorily completed" as "pass"                                          | The standard is the CFI's professional judgment, not a checkride score; the CFI can decline to sign and continue the review |
| Misses the Hicks PIC-while-instructing point                                         | Affects who logs PIC, who needs a medical, who's on the hook if something goes wrong                                        |

## Variant prompts

| Variant                                                                                                                                            | What changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Same friend, but they're a CFI candidate asking you to give them their CFI flight review."                                                        | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026) flight review still applies. CFI privileges under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/193?at=2026) and [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026) are independent of the flight review; renewal under [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/197?at=2026) is a separate timer. Don't confuse "CFI giving a flight review to a CFI" with "CFI renewing their own CFI." |
| "Same friend, instrument-rated, asks for a combined IPC + flight review."                                                                          | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(d) IPC has its own AC 61-65 endorsement (A.4); both endorsements can be issued from the same training event if both standards are met, but they are separate signatures. AC 61-98 explicitly contemplates the combination.                                                                                                                                            |
| "Same friend, but the friend's last 'review' was a WINGS phase 26 months ago."                                                                     | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(f) lets a WINGS phase substitute for the review. 26 months out -> the WINGS phase has expired the 24-cal-month window. The friend needs a real review or a fresh WINGS phase. Walk the WINGS-vs-review choice.                                                                                                                                                        |
| "Same friend, but you don't currently meet [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b) night currency, and the proposed flight is at night." | I cannot conduct the night portion of the review at night without [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b) currency *if I'm acting as PIC and carrying passengers* -- the friend isn't a passenger if they're receiving instruction. Hicks complicates this. Practical answer: schedule the review in daylight, or restore my own night currency first.                                                     |
| "Same friend, but the airplane is a glider."                                                                                                       | I'm CFI-A, not CFI-G. [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/195?at=2026)(h) blocks me from giving instruction in a glider. The friend needs a CFI-G. I refer; I do not "stretch" my privileges.                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| "Same friend, but they want to use a flight simulator for the ground portion."                                                                     | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/56?at=2026)(a) requires 1 hour of *ground* training. Sim time is not ground time. Flight time in an FFS or FTD can count toward the *flight* hour under specific conditions; ground is ground. Read AC 61-98 for the simulator-substitution rules.                                                                                                                                |
| "Same friend, post-review, asks you to also sign their [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b) night currency."                          | [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b) is not a CFI signoff -- it's a logbook entry the pilot makes themselves of three full-stop landings to a night landing in 90 days. There's no "CFI endorsement" for [@cite](airboss-ref:regs/cfr-14/61/57?at=2026)(b). Do not invent one.                                                                                                                          |
