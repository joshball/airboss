# Syllabus

10 weeks. Roughly 4-6 hours per week of focused work, plus time for the front-to-back reading. Maps to a quarter-length college course; can be compressed to 5 weeks at double cadence or stretched to a semester.

Each week ends with an oral exam segment. The orals build cumulatively -- Week 5's oral can pull from anything in Weeks 1-5.

## Week-by-week

### Week 1: The architecture of Title 14

**Goal:** Given any regulatory question, the learner points at the part / subpart where the answer lives in <30 seconds.

**Topics:**

- Title 14 -> Chapter -> Subchapter -> Part -> Subpart -> Section -> Paragraph
- The parts that matter to pilots: 1, 21, 23, 39, 43, 61, 67, 71, 91, 97, 119, 121, 135, 141, 145
- The pilot / the flight / the operation framing
- How to read a citation: 91.103(b)(1)
- The companion documents and what each is for: AIM (expected knowledge), AC (guidance), Chief Counsel (interpretation), NTSB Part 830 (post-incident)
- Why 91.3 and 91.13 are different things, and one is far more important than the other

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 1, definitions. Brisk -- ~30 minutes. Not memorize; orient.

**Drills:** "Where does this rule live?" speed drills. 20 prompts, 60 seconds each.

**Oral:** "Walk me through Title 14 like you're showing it to a student." See [week-01-architecture/oral.md](week-01-architecture/oral.md).

### Week 2: Part 61 -- the pilot (certificates and currency)

**Goal:** The learner can map any pilot, situation, or question into the right Part 61 subpart and find the answer.

**Topics:**

- The shape of 61: subparts A-K, organized by certificate level
- Why 61 has parallel subparts (E for private, F for commercial, G for ATP) -- the structural pattern
- 61.51 logging -- what counts and what doesn't
- 61.56 flight review and 61.57 recent experience -- the most-tested sections
- 61.58 PIC proficiency check (when does this apply?)
- Medical certificates: 67 not 61, but live here in the curriculum
- BasicMed (49 USC 44703) -- not a CFR section but a parallel pathway

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 61 subparts A-G (general + each cert level). Heavy commentary on parallels.

**Drills:** "Given this pilot, what currency requirements apply?" -- 15 scenarios.

**Oral:** Currency for a private pilot flying IFR at night with passengers tomorrow. (Builds toward the full capstone.)

### Week 3: Part 61 -- the CFI (subpart H, endorsements, FOI)

**Goal:** The learner knows what they are about to become, what they are authorized to do, and what their endorsements actually accomplish.

**Topics:**

- Subpart H -- the entirety of CFI authority
- 61.183 eligibility, 61.187 aeronautical experience, 61.189 logbook records
- 61.193 privileges, 61.195 limitations -- the daily-life sections
- 61.197 renewal (FIRC, FAA-approved practical test, recent activity record)
- Endorsements: 61.39, 61.87, 61.93, 61.107, 61.129; AC 61-65 (the endorsement bible)
- Spin endorsement (61.183(i)) -- famous and tested
- Subpart I (light-sport instructors) -- name and locate, skim

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 61 subpart H, complete. Then AC 61-65 sections 1-5.

**Drills:** "Given this student / situation, what endorsement applies and where does it come from?"

**Oral:** "A friend asks you to sign them off for their flight review. Walk me through what you check, what you require, and what you sign."

### Week 4: Part 91 -- general + flight rules (subparts A-B)

**Goal:** The learner internalizes the spine of every flight: PIC authority, careless/reckless, preflight, fuel, alcohol, seatbelts.

**Topics:**

- 91.3 PIC authority (the deviation clause)
- 91.13 careless or reckless (the catch-all enforcement section)
- 91.7 airworthiness (PIC determination, continuing airworthiness)
- 91.103 preflight action (NWKRAFT mnemonic; what "all available information" really means)
- 91.105 / 91.107 seatbelts (the difference matters)
- 91.111 / 91.113 / 91.115 right-of-way
- 91.119 minimum safe altitudes (the "congested area" trap)
- 91.151 / 91.155 / 91.157 / 91.159 VFR weather and altitudes (cloud clearances)
- 91.167 / 91.169 / 91.171 IFR fuel, alternate, equipment
- 91.175 / 91.177 / 91.179 / 91.181 / 91.183 / 91.185 -- the IFR ops section (DH/MDA, contact/visual, lost comm)
- 91.211 oxygen requirements
- 91.215 transponder

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 91 subparts A-B, complete, with running commentary.

**Drills:** "Find the rule in 60 seconds" -- 25 prompts pulling from 91 subpart A and B.

**Oral:** "VFR cross-country, marginal weather, what regulatory checks before launching?"

### Week 5: Part 91 -- equipment + maintenance (subparts C-E)

**Goal:** The learner can answer "is this airplane legal to fly today, for this operation?" without notes.

**Topics:**

- 91.203 documents required (AROW)
- 91.205 equipment by op type (ATOMATOFLAMES / FLAPS / GRABCARD); the *structure* of why VFR day vs. VFR night vs. IFR add equipment
- 91.207 ELTs (testing, batteries, exemptions)
- 91.209 lights
- 91.213 inoperative equipment (MEL / no-MEL pathway, the placard rule)
- 91.215 transponder (Mode A/C/S, ADS-B Out 91.225/91.227)
- 91.225 / 91.227 ADS-B Out -- the rule of the decade
- 91.405 maintenance responsibilities
- 91.407 ops after maintenance / preventive maintenance / rebuilding / alteration
- 91.409 inspections (annual, 100-hour -- the trap)
- 91.411 altimeter / static system (24 calendar months, IFR only)
- 91.413 transponder (24 calendar months, all ops)
- 91.417 maintenance records (what stays in the airplane, what stays in the logs)
- 91.419 transfer of records on aircraft sale

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 91 subparts C, D, E, complete.

**Drills:** "Given this aircraft and this op, what's required?" -- 20 scenarios.

**Oral:** "Walk me through every inspection currency check on this airplane for an IFR flight tomorrow."

### Week 6: Part 91 -- special ops + integration

**Goal:** Round out 91. Pull the whole part together.

**Topics:**

- Subpart D special ops: aerobatic (91.303), parachute ops (91.307), towing (91.309), formation (91.111(b))
- 91.311 / 91.313 / 91.317 -- restricted, limited, experimental
- Subpart F (large/turbine multi-engine) -- name + locate, do not memorize
- Subpart G (RVSM, foreign) -- name + locate
- Subpart H+ (foreign aircraft, fractional, etc.) -- name + locate
- Integration: walking the whole of 91 in 90 minutes -- "what's where, why, when does it apply"

**Front-to-back reading:** Skim subparts F-N. Then re-read subpart A in light of everything.

**Drills:** Mixed drills pulling from any of 91.

**Oral:** Multi-part integration scenario.

### Week 7: Parts 141 and 135 -- the operation

**Goal:** Literacy. The learner knows what these parts are, when they apply, where to look.

**Topics:**

- Part 141: structured curriculum, TCO-approved, fewer hours, stage checks. Why a 141 school can graduate a private at 35 hours but a Part 61 school usually takes 60+. The CFI's role differs (you're teaching to a syllabus, not a freeform plan).
- Part 141 appendices A-N (the syllabi for each cert level) -- name + locate
- Part 135: on-demand commercial. Why your future commercial students keep asking about it. Higher minimums, more equipment, ops specs, chief pilot, drug testing.
- Part 119 -- the certification-of-operators part that 135 hangs off of
- Part 121 (airline) -- name + locate, you don't operate here yet
- Part 145 (repair stations) -- name + locate

**Front-to-back reading:** Part 141 subpart A. Skim the rest of 141 + 135.

**Drills:** "Is this a 91, 135, 141, or 121 operation?" -- 20 scenarios.

**Oral:** "Your student is leaving you for a 135 operator. What changes for them?"

### Week 8: Companion documents -- AIM, AC, Chief Counsel

**Goal:** The learner knows the regulatory ecosystem outside Title 14.

**Topics:**

- AIM as expected knowledge (not regulatory, but enforceable via 91.13)
- How the AIM is structured: chapters on airspace, navigation, ATC procedures, weather, emergencies
- Advisory Circulars -- numbering scheme, how to find current versions
  - AC 00-6 weather
  - AC 61-65 endorsements
  - AC 90-66 non-towered ops
  - AC 91-79 runway overrun prevention
  - AC 120-71 SOPs
- Chief Counsel interpretations -- the Mangiamele letter, the Hicks letter, the Murphy letter
- 49 CFR 1552 (TSA flight training rule)
- NTSB Part 830 -- not 14 CFR, lives in 49 CFR
- Where the FAA hides the real answer (often in an AC, not the FAR itself)

**Front-to-back reading:** AIM Chapter 1 (Air Navigation) and Chapter 4 (Air Traffic Procedures). Sample 3 ACs in detail.

**Drills:** "Where does this question's answer actually live -- FAR, AIM, AC, or Chief Counsel letter?"

**Oral:** "A regulation is unclear to you. Walk me through how you find the authoritative interpretation."

### Week 9: The enforcement system + NTSB Part 830

**Goal:** The learner understands what happens when things go wrong, and what their rights are.

**Topics:**

- The compliance program (formerly "compliance philosophy") vs. legal enforcement
- FAA enforcement action: warning notice -> letter of investigation -> certificate action
- Pilot's Bill of Rights, NASA ASRS
- 61.16, 91.17 (drug/alcohol), 67.403 (medical fraud) -- the career-enders
- NTSB Part 830 -- accidents and incidents, what to report and when
- The 91.123 -- when ATC gives an instruction you can't comply with
- Remedial training in lieu of certificate action

**Front-to-back reading:** NTSB Part 830, complete. FAA Order 2150.3 (the enforcement handbook) -- skim Chapter 1 only.

**Drills:** "Given this incident, what reports are required, by whom, by when?"

**Oral:** Career-ending mistake walk-through (defensible answers, not panic).

### Week 10: Capstone -- integrated oral

**Goal:** The learner can answer any reasonable CFI-level FAR question without notes.

**Topics:**

- Full-day oral exam, scenario-based
- Partner exams: students grade each other against a rubric
- Final scenario: "You're the CFI. Your student lands gear-up on a night IFR cross-country. Walk me through your regulatory obligations from second of impact to FAA ramp check."
- Reflection: which subparts do you find slowest? What does your map of Title 14 look like in your head?

**Reading:** No new reading. Re-read subpart A of 91. Re-read 61.193 / 61.195 / 91.3 / 91.13 from memory check.

**Drills:** Full mixed timed drill -- 50 prompts, 60 seconds each.

**Oral:** [orals/night-ifr-passenger.md](orals/night-ifr-passenger.md) -- the canonical capstone. Plus three sibling capstones from [orals/](orals/).
