# A.9.1 -- The IPC

Questions covering IPC administration per SS 61.57(d), ACS/PTS Rating Task Table requirements, required maneuvers, instructor discretion, and Special Emphasis areas.

---

### Q-A9-17 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-09)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-2, AV-3

An instrument-rated pilot asks you to administer an IPC. They last flew IFR 14 months ago and their instrument currency has lapsed under 14 CFR 61.57(c). Which regulatory section specifically governs the IPC you will conduct?

A) 14 CFR 61.56 -- Flight Review
B) 14 CFR 61.57(d) -- Instrument Proficiency Check
C) 14 CFR 61.58 -- Pilot-in-Command Proficiency Check
D) 14 CFR 61.57(c) -- Instrument Experience

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 states that "the instrument flight instructor who administers the IPC described in SS 61.57(d) must know how to conduct an IPC effectively." The IPC is the mechanism for regaining instrument currency after the grace period under 61.57(c) expires. Option A governs flight reviews, not IPCs. Option C applies to type-rated aircraft. Option D describes the currency requirements that, when not met, trigger the need for an IPC.

### Q-A9-18 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-09)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RC-2, ES-1

When conducting an IPC, what is the minimum set of maneuvers and procedures the flight portion must include?

A) Whatever the CFI considers appropriate based on the pilot's experience
B) Those listed in the Rating Task Table of the current ACS or PTS for the instrument rating, plus any additional maneuvers the instructor deems necessary
C) At least six instrument approaches, holding, and intercepting/tracking courses
D) The same maneuvers required for the instrument rating practical test in their entirety

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 states that "the flight portion must include certain aeronautical tasks" and that "maneuvers and procedures selected for the IPC must include those listed in the Rating Task Table in the current edition of the ACS or PTS guide." The instructor also "has the discretion to require any other maneuver(s) necessary." Option A omits the required minimum. Option C specifies a fixed list that may not match the current Rating Task Table. Option D overstates the requirement -- the IPC uses the Rating Task Table, not the full practical test.

### Q-A9-19 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-10)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** AV-3, RC-2

According to AC 61-83K, what must a flight instructor know about the ACS/PTS when conducting an IPC? (Select all that apply)

A) The ACS or PTS for the instrument rating contains the required aeronautical tasks for the IPC flight portion
B) Maneuvers selected must include those in the Rating Task Table
C) The instructor may not add any maneuvers beyond those listed in the Rating Task Table
D) New ACS replacement guides do not label tasks as "Special Emphasis" since all required tasks are emphasized
E) The instructor should pay particular attention to areas within the PTS identified as "Special Emphasis"

**Correct:** A, B, D, E
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 explicitly states all four correct points. The ACS/PTS contains required tasks (A), the Rating Task Table sets the minimum (B), new ACS guides treat all tasks as emphasized rather than labeling some "Special Emphasis" (D), and for the PTS the instructor should pay particular attention to Special Emphasis areas (E). Option C is incorrect -- the instructor explicitly "has the discretion to require any other maneuver(s) necessary."

### Q-A9-20 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-10)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, ES-2

A pilot who holds an instrument rating for single-engine land asks you to conduct an IPC in a multi-engine airplane. They want to use the IPC to also demonstrate multi-engine instrument proficiency. Which ACS or PTS guide should you reference for the Rating Task Table?

A) The ACS/PTS for the instrument rating applicable to the category and class of aircraft being used
B) Any instrument rating ACS/PTS since they are all the same
C) The multi-engine commercial ACS since it covers multi-engine operations
D) No specific guide is required -- the CFI has full discretion over IPC content

**Correct:** A
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 states maneuvers must include those in the Rating Task Table of "the ACS or PTS guide, as applicable." The applicable guide depends on the rating and aircraft used. Option B incorrectly assumes all instrument ACS/PTS are identical. Option C references the wrong standard -- commercial ACS is not the instrument proficiency standard. Option D ignores the AC's requirement to use the Rating Task Table as a minimum.

### Q-A9-21 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-11)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-1, ES-2

During an IPC, you notice a pilot completes all Rating Task Table maneuvers to standard but demonstrates poor situational awareness when given an amended clearance by ATC in a busy terminal environment. The Rating Task Table maneuvers are complete. What should you do?

A) Sign off the IPC since all required maneuvers were completed to standard
B) Exercise your discretion to require additional scenarios that evaluate the pilot's ability to manage workload and maintain situational awareness under realistic IFR conditions before completing the IPC
C) Note the concern in the logbook but sign off the IPC since the Rating Task Table is the only requirement
D) Recommend the pilot take an instrument refresher course but sign off the IPC now

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 states the instructor "has the discretion to require any other maneuver(s) necessary to determine that the pilot can safely operate under instrument flight rules (IFR) in a broad range of conditions appropriate to the aircraft flown and the ATC environment selected." Situational awareness under workload is directly relevant to safe IFR operations. Option A ignores a demonstrated weakness. Option C defers action inappropriately. Option D separates the training from the evaluation.

### Q-A9-22 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-11)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-2, RM-1

A pilot requests an IPC and tells you they only fly IFR in VMC conditions using flight following. They want to "just get the paperwork done" so they can fly IFR legally. How should you approach this IPC?

A) Conduct a minimal IPC since the pilot will only fly IFR in VMC
B) Conduct the IPC to the full standard, including evaluation in simulated IMC conditions, because the IPC certifies the pilot to operate in all IFR conditions -- not just the ones they prefer
C) Refuse to conduct the IPC since the pilot's attitude suggests they are not serious about instrument proficiency
D) Conduct the IPC in VMC only, matching the pilot's expected operating environment

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 requires the IPC to determine the pilot "can safely operate under instrument flight rules (IFR) in a broad range of conditions." An IPC is not tailored to a pilot's self-limited operating intentions -- it certifies instrument proficiency across the full scope of IFR operations. A pilot who is legal to fly IFR may encounter IMC unexpectedly. Option A creates a false standard. Option C is premature -- the pilot's attitude should be addressed through the training. Option D reduces the IPC below the required standard.

### Q-A9-23 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-12)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, AV-2

You are conducting an IPC for a pilot who flies a Cirrus SR22 with a Garmin Perspective+ avionics suite. The pilot is proficient with coupled approaches using the autopilot but has difficulty hand-flying a partial-panel ILS. Should you require the hand-flying demonstration as part of the IPC?

A) No -- modern glass cockpit aircraft are designed to be flown with the autopilot, and hand-flying is no longer relevant
B) Yes -- the instructor has discretion to require maneuvers beyond the Rating Task Table, and hand-flying proficiency is essential for safe IFR operations in the event of automation failure
C) Only if the PTS specifically lists partial-panel as a required maneuver
D) No -- the IPC should match how the pilot normally operates the aircraft

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 gives the instructor "discretion to require any other maneuver(s) necessary to determine that the pilot can safely operate under IFR." Automation failure in IMC is a realistic emergency scenario. A pilot who cannot maintain aircraft control without the autopilot has a critical proficiency gap. Option A ignores automation failure scenarios. Option C limits the instructor's authority to only the Rating Task Table. Option D prioritizes comfort over safety.

### Q-A9-24 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-12)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-1, RC-2

A CFI colleague tells you they conduct IPCs by having the pilot fly three approaches -- one precision, one non-precision, and one circling -- and if those go well, they sign off. They don't reference the Rating Task Table. What is the problem?

A) Three approaches is sufficient for an IPC as long as they are varied
B) The IPC must include the maneuvers listed in the Rating Task Table of the applicable ACS or PTS -- the instructor cannot substitute their own abbreviated list for the published standard
C) The problem is only that they should include four approaches instead of three
D) There is no problem as long as the pilot demonstrates proficiency

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 is explicit: "The maneuvers and procedures selected for the IPC must include those listed in the Rating Task Table in the current edition of the ACS or PTS guide." An instructor who creates their own abbreviated list may omit required tasks such as holding, unusual attitudes, or other Rating Task Table items. Option A and D accept a non-compliant IPC. Option C focuses on approach count rather than the actual standard.

### Q-A9-25 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-13)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** CJ-1, ES-2

A FIRC attendee asks about "Special Emphasis" areas in the context of IPCs. Based on AC 61-83K, which of the following statements are correct? (Select all that apply)

A) The PTS identifies certain areas as "Special Emphasis" that deserve particular attention during an IPC
B) New ACS replacement guides do not label tasks as "Special Emphasis" because all required tasks are emphasized
C) Special Emphasis areas can be safely ignored if the pilot performs well on other maneuvers
D) The instructor should pay particular attention to Special Emphasis areas when using the PTS

**Correct:** A, B, D
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K A.9.1 states the instructor "should pay particular attention to those areas within the PTS identified as 'Special Emphasis'" and notes that "new ACS replacement guides do not label 'Special Emphasis' areas, since all required tasks are emphasized." Option C contradicts the AC's directive to pay particular attention to these areas.

### Q-A9-26 (Module 5, Pool A9-M5-13)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-2, CJ-2

You are conducting an IPC and the pilot asks you to conduct the entire check in a flight training device (FTD) rather than an aircraft. They cite convenience and cost savings. What factors should guide your decision?

A) FTDs can never be used for IPCs -- the check must be conducted in an aircraft
B) Consider whether the FTD is approved for the tasks required by the Rating Task Table and whether it adequately represents the conditions needed to evaluate the pilot's proficiency in a broad range of IFR conditions appropriate to their aircraft
C) Any FTD is acceptable since IPCs don't require an actual aircraft
D) Use the FTD for approaches but require the aircraft for other maneuvers regardless of FTD approval

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** 14 CFR 61.57(d) permits the use of approved flight simulators and FTDs for IPCs, but the device must be appropriate for the tasks being evaluated. AC 61-83K A.9.1 emphasizes evaluating proficiency "in a broad range of conditions appropriate to the aircraft flown." The instructor must verify the device is approved and adequate for a thorough evaluation. Option A is factually incorrect. Option C ignores approval requirements. Option D creates an arbitrary split not based on device capabilities.
