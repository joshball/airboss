# A.7.3 -- WINGS Program Structure

Questions covering WINGS as a structured/documented process, three levels (Basic/Advanced/Master), and evaluation and proficiency training.

---

### Q-A7-37 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-19)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** PS-2, AV-1

A certificated pilot asks you to explain how WINGS differs from simply "flying more hours." According to AC 61-83K, what is the fundamental nature of the WINGS program?

A) WINGS is an informal recommendation to fly more often
B) WINGS is a structured, documented process for pilot proficiency
C) WINGS is an aerobatic training program for advanced pilots
D) WINGS is a flight hour logging system that replaces a pilot logbook

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3, "WINGS is a structured, documented process." This distinguishes it from unstructured flying (A) by providing specific knowledge and flight activities organized into levels. It is not limited to aerobatics (C) and does not replace a logbook (D). The structured nature ensures pilots develop proficiency in targeted areas rather than simply accumulating hours.

C) Let the pilot self-certify their flight activities without instructor involvement

C) WINGS proficiency gains depend entirely on the pilot's self-study; instructors play no role

### Q-A7-42 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-21)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** PS-2, ES-3

Which of the following accurately describe the role of a WINGS instructor according to AC 61-83K A.7.3? (Select all that apply)

A) WINGS instructors evaluate pilot proficiency during flight activities
B) WINGS instructors train pilots to improve proficiency, not just verify minimum standards
C) WINGS instructors determine which WINGS level a pilot is assigned to based on total flight time
D) WINGS instructors work within a structured, documented process
E) WINGS instructors issue pilot certificates upon completion of Master level

**Correct:** A, B, D
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3, WINGS instructors "evaluate and train for proficiency" (A, B) within "a structured, documented process" (D). WINGS levels are not assigned based on flight time (C) -- they are achieved through completion of specific knowledge and flight activities. WINGS instructors do not issue pilot certificates (E) -- certificate issuance is governed by 14 CFR Part 61 and involves designated pilot examiners.

### Q-A7-43 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-22)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, PS-1

You are deciding how to structure a WINGS flight activity for a private pilot working on Advanced level. The pilot wants to "just do some pattern work." How should you approach this based on AC 61-83K's description of WINGS?

A) Pattern work is fine -- let the pilot choose whatever they want to practice
B) Design the activity around the specific proficiency objectives for the Advanced level, evaluating and training the pilot's skills against those standards
C) Decline to provide the flight activity since Advanced level requires a different instructor
D) Simply endorse the pilot's logbook without flying, since they already demonstrated Basic proficiency

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3, WINGS is a "structured, documented process" where instructors "evaluate and train for proficiency." This means activities should be designed around specific proficiency objectives, not unstructured practice (A). The CFI should evaluate against the level's standards and train toward improvement. No special instructor qualification is needed beyond CFI status (C), and signing without flying (D) violates the evaluation and training requirement.

### Q-A7-44 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-22)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** RM-1, PS-2

A pilot who completed WINGS Basic two years ago says they don't need to do it again because "once you earn a level, you keep it forever." Is this understanding correct?

A) Yes -- WINGS levels, once earned, never expire and require no further action
B) No -- WINGS is an ongoing proficiency program; pilots attain and maintain phases on a rolling basis, and continued participation is encouraged for ongoing skill development
C) WINGS levels expire every 12 calendar months and must be retested
D) Only the Master level is permanent; Basic and Advanced expire annually

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3 and A.7.4, WINGS is designed as an ongoing proficiency program. Pilots "attain and maintain phases on a rolling basis." While a completed phase satisfies the flight review requirement, the program encourages continued participation for ongoing proficiency. The pilot's assumption that one-time completion eliminates future need contradicts the program's continuous improvement philosophy. Options C and D describe specific expiration mechanisms not defined in the AC.

A CFI is preparing to become a WINGS instructor. Based on AC 61-83K A.7.3, which of the following are core characteristics of the WINGS program they should understand? (Select all that apply)

A) WINGS is a structured process with defined levels and activities
B) The program is documented -- activities and completions are recorded
C) Instructors evaluate pilot proficiency, not just observe
D) Instructors train pilots toward proficiency improvement
E) The program has two levels: Pass and Fail

**Correct:** A, B, C, D
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3, WINGS is "a structured, documented process" (A, B) where instructors "evaluate and train for proficiency" (C, D). The program has three levels -- Basic, Advanced, and Master -- not a binary pass/fail system (E). Understanding these characteristics helps the CFI approach WINGS instruction with the appropriate mindset of active evaluation and training rather than passive oversight.

### Q-A7-46 (Module 5, Pool A7-M5-23)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** ES-1, CJ-1

During a flight review, a pilot mentions they've been meaning to try WINGS but finds it confusing. They ask whether WINGS is "just another bureaucratic program." How does AC 61-83K characterize the program?

A) As a regulatory compliance requirement that all pilots must complete annually
B) As a structured, documented proficiency process with three progressive levels, where instructors evaluate and train for genuine skill improvement
C) As an optional social club for pilots who enjoy attending safety events
D) As a flight hour tracking system managed by the FSDO

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** Per AC 61-83K A.7.3, WINGS is described as "a structured, documented process" with "three levels: Basic, Advanced, Master" where "WINGS instructors evaluate and train for proficiency." This characterization emphasizes purposeful structure (not bureaucracy), genuine proficiency development (not checkbox compliance), and progressive achievement (not a one-time event). It is not a regulatory requirement (A), a social club (C), or a tracking system (D).
