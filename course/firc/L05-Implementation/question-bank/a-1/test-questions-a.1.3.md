# A.1.3 -- NextGen

Questions covering NAS modernization, communications/navigation/surveillance upgrades, airport infrastructure improvements, and new procedures.

---

### Q-A1-37 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-19)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-3, PS-2

A CFI attending your FIRC asks, "What exactly is NextGen?" Which answer best reflects the AC 61-83K description?

A) NextGen is a new type of aircraft avionics suite replacing traditional instruments
B) NextGen is a large-scale FAA initiative to modernize the U.S. National Airspace System, encompassing upgrades to communications, navigation, surveillance, automation, and information management
C) NextGen is an airline-only program that does not affect general aviation
D) NextGen is the FAA's name for the GPS satellite constellation

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K defines NextGen as "a large-scale FAA initiative to modernize the U.S. National Airspace System (NAS) in the 21st Century" covering "air traffic infrastructure in communications, navigation, surveillance, automation, and information management." Option A confuses a national system modernization with cockpit equipment. Option C is incorrect -- NextGen affects all NAS users including GA. Option D confuses NextGen with the GPS constellation, which is one of many technologies NextGen leverages.

### Q-A1-38 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-19)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-2, OD-2

During a ground school session, a student asks how NextGen benefits them as a VFR-only private pilot. What is the most accurate response?

A) NextGen has no impact on VFR operations -- it only affects IFR and airline traffic
B) NextGen improvements to surveillance (ADS-B), weather information, and airspace efficiency benefit all NAS users, including VFR GA pilots, through better traffic awareness, improved weather data access, and more efficient airspace design
C) NextGen requires all VFR pilots to carry GPS and ADS-B, which is the only benefit
D) NextGen only benefits pilots who fly into Class B airspace

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K states that NextGen aims to increase "the safety, efficiency, capacity, predictability, flexibility, and resiliency of American aviation." These benefits extend to all NAS users. VFR GA pilots benefit from ADS-B traffic awareness, FIS-B weather services, improved procedures, and better airspace management. Option A incorrectly excludes VFR operations. Option C reduces NextGen to a single mandate. Option D fabricates an airspace limitation.

### Q-A1-39 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-20)

**Type:** multiple-select
**Competencies:** AV-3, RC-2

According to AC 61-83K, what are the stated goals of the NextGen initiative? (Select all that apply)

A) Increasing the safety of American aviation
B) Improving efficiency, capacity, and predictability of the NAS
C) Replacing all human air traffic controllers with automated systems
D) Enhancing flexibility and resiliency of the aviation system
E) Eliminating the need for pilot training in traditional navigation methods

**Correct:** A, B, D
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K states that NextGen aims at "increasing the safety, efficiency, capacity, predictability, flexibility, and resiliency of American aviation." Options A, B, and D directly quote or paraphrase these goals. Option C is fabricated -- NextGen modernizes the tools controllers use but does not eliminate human ATC. Option E contradicts AC 61-83K's emphasis on maintaining proficiency in traditional navigation methods alongside new technology.

### Q-A1-40 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-20)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-2, PS-2

Which of the following best describes the scope of NextGen as defined in AC 61-83K?

A) NextGen is limited to replacing radar with ADS-B surveillance
B) NextGen's scope includes airport infrastructure improvements, new air traffic technologies and procedures, and safety and security enhancements
C) NextGen only addresses en route airspace above FL240
D) NextGen is focused exclusively on communications upgrades

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K explicitly states that "NextGen's scope includes airport infrastructure improvements, new air traffic technologies and procedures, and safety and security enhancements." This is a comprehensive modernization program, not a single-technology initiative. Option A reduces NextGen to just one component (ADS-B). Option C fabricates an altitude limitation. Option D limits NextGen to communications when it actually spans communications, navigation, surveillance, automation, and information management.

### Q-A1-41 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-21)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, AV-2

A student asks whether they need to learn about NextGen since they fly a 1968 Cessna 150 with no GPS. How should you respond as a CFI?

A) NextGen does not apply to legacy aircraft, so they can skip it
B) NextGen changes to the NAS affect all pilots regardless of aircraft equipment -- new procedures, airspace changes, ADS-B traffic patterns, and communication methods apply to everyone operating in the system
C) They should upgrade their aircraft before learning about NextGen
D) NextGen only matters for pilots who fly above 10,000 feet MSL

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** NextGen modernizes the entire NAS -- not just the equipment in individual cockpits. Procedure changes, airspace redesigns, new communication technologies, and the shift to ADS-B-based surveillance affect every pilot operating in the system. AC 61-83K includes NextGen as required FIRC content because all CFIs must understand these systemic changes. Option A incorrectly ties NextGen relevance to aircraft equipment. Option C makes understanding conditional on spending money. Option D fabricates an altitude limitation.

### Q-A1-42 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-21)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** OD-2, RM-2

As part of NextGen, the FAA has introduced Performance Based Navigation (PBN) procedures at many airports. Your student is planning a flight to an airport where a new RNAV (GPS) departure procedure has replaced the previous conventional departure. The student's aircraft is not GPS-equipped. What should you teach?

A) The student cannot depart that airport without GPS equipment
B) The student should understand that NextGen procedure changes may affect available departure and arrival options, and should check NOTAMs and current procedures to determine what alternatives exist for non-GPS-equipped aircraft
C) Conventional departures are always available as backups to RNAV procedures
D) The student should install GPS before their next flight to that airport

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** NextGen has introduced PBN procedures that may replace conventional procedures at some airports. CFIs must teach students to understand how these changes affect their operations and to verify current procedure availability during flight planning. This connects to AC 61-83K's guidance that NextGen includes "new air traffic technologies and procedures." Option A may be overly restrictive -- alternatives like radar vectors or VFR departures may exist. Option C makes a dangerous assumption. Option D is a purchasing decision, not an instructional response.

### Q-A1-43 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-22)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-3, PS-2

Why does AC 61-83K specifically require FIRC providers to include NextGen training for flight instructors?

A) Because CFIs are required to pass a NextGen knowledge test for certificate renewal
B) Because flight instructors need to understand how NAS modernization changes affect flight operations so they can teach students to operate effectively in the evolving system
C) Because NextGen equipment installation requires CFI supervision
D) Because the FAA mandates NextGen training for all pilots, not just CFIs

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K states that "flight instructors also need to fully understand" NextGen and directs FIRC providers to include "an overview of NextGen" in Topic A.1. CFIs are the front line of aviation education -- if they don't understand how the NAS is changing, they cannot prepare students for the system they'll actually fly in. Option A fabricates a knowledge test requirement. Option C fabricates a supervision requirement. Option D overstates the mandate -- AC 61-83K addresses FIRC content requirements, not a universal pilot training mandate.

A) Communications infrastructure
B) Navigation systems and procedures

**Correct:** A, B, C, E

### Q-A1-45 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-23)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** CJ-2, RC-2

During a FIRC session, an instructor argues that NextGen is "just an ADS-B mandate" and doesn't warrant significant training time. How should you respond as the FIRC provider?

A) Agree -- ADS-B is the only operationally relevant part of NextGen for GA
B) Explain that NextGen encompasses far more than ADS-B -- including airport infrastructure improvements, new procedures, communications modernization, and information management -- and that reducing it to one mandate misrepresents the scope of changes affecting flight operations
C) Acknowledge that NextGen is mostly irrelevant to GA instructors
D) Suggest the instructor research NextGen on their own time since it's not important enough for the FIRC

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K describes NextGen as a "large-scale FAA initiative" whose scope includes "airport infrastructure improvements, new air traffic technologies and procedures, and safety and security enhancements" -- far broader than ADS-B alone. Reducing NextGen to a single mandate would leave CFIs uninformed about procedure changes, communication upgrades, and airspace redesigns that directly affect their students' operations. Option A validates the misconception. Options C and D contradict the AC's explicit requirement to include NextGen training.

### Q-A1-46 (Module 1, Pool A1-M1-23)

**Type:** multiple-choice
**Competencies:** AV-2, OD-2

How does NextGen's modernization of NAS communications affect the way a CFI should train students?

A) There is no change -- voice communication with ATC remains identical to 50 years ago
B) CFIs should ensure students understand that NextGen is introducing data-link communications, digital information sharing, and other communication technologies that supplement and may eventually change how pilots interact with ATC
C) NextGen has eliminated the need for radio communications entirely
D) Only airline pilots need to understand NextGen communications changes

**Correct:** B
**Explanation:** AC 61-83K identifies communications as one of the five areas NextGen has modernized. Data communications (Data Comm), digital ATIS, and other technologies are changing how information flows between pilots and ATC. CFIs must understand these changes to prepare students for the evolving system. Option A ignores documented modernization. Option C is factually wrong -- voice communication remains critical. Option D incorrectly excludes GA from NAS-wide changes.
