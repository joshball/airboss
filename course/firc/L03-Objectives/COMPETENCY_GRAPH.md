# Competency Graph

The layer between FAA-required content, course design, the scenario engine, and assessment/reporting. Without this, everything else becomes disconnected content.

## Graph Structure (5 Layers)

| Layer                   | Description             | Example                                                     |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1. FAA Topic            | Required content bucket | LOC, TSA, ACS                                               |
| 2. Competency Domain    | Skill category          | Instructional judgment, Risk management                     |
| 3. Competency           | A real, teachable skill | "Detect early stall/spin precursors"                        |
| 4. Observable Behaviors | What the learner does   | "Identifies low airspeed before critical trend"             |
| 5. Evidence             | How the system knows    | "Paused before the break", "Chose coaching before takeover" |

## 8 Competency Domains

### Domain 1 -- Instructional Judgment (CJ)

_Can the CFI teach in real time?_

#### CJ-1: Diagnose Student State in Real Time

The instructor identifies what kind of problem is happening.

**Student States to Diagnose:**

- Lack of knowledge
- Lack of attention
- Incorrect scan
- Startle/freeze
- Poor control coordination
- Misunderstanding despite correct words

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Asks diagnostic questions
- Distinguishes confusion from defiance
- Adjusts intervention based on student response

**Evidence:**

- Chooses information-gathering question before over-controlling
- Changes strategy when student parrots but performs incorrectly

**Scenario Examples:**

- Base-to-final stall precursor
- Student says "airspeed is fine" when it is not
- Student freezes after overshoot

**Prerequisites:** Basic cue recognition, intervention ladder understanding

---

#### CJ-2: Use the Least Invasive Effective Intervention

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Asks before commanding when safe
- Prompts before takeover when time allows
- Takes controls immediately when safety margin is gone

**Evidence:**

- Intervention timing score
- Intervention type appropriateness
- Avoided unnecessary takeover
- Did not wait too long

**Scenario Examples:**

- Unstable approach
- Takeoff pitch excursion
- Taxi confusion before runway entry

---

#### CJ-3: Debrief Causes, Not Just Outcomes

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Explains chain, not just error
- Links behavior to risk
- Gives corrective strategy
- Confirms student understanding

**Evidence:**

- Debrief structure score
- Root-cause tags used
- Replay improvement after debrief

**Scenario Examples:**

- Post-stall recovery
- Runway incursion near-miss
- Automation mode confusion

---

### Domain 2 -- Aircraft Control and Safety Intervention (AC)

_Can the CFI recognize and interrupt unsafe flight paths?_

#### AC-1: Detect Unstable Approach Trends Early

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Notices low/decaying airspeed
- Notices excessive bank in final turn
- Notices skid/slip cues
- Notices fixation on centerline or altitude

**Evidence:**

- Pause or intervention before threshold tick
- Correct cue identification
- Verbalized safer option

**Scenario Examples:**

- Base-to-final night pattern scenario
- Gusty crosswind final
- Long day / fatigued student / late turn to final

---

#### AC-2: Prevent Stall/Spin Chain Development

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Coaches pitch/power appropriately
- Prioritizes wings/airspeed/safe path
- Commands go-around when appropriate
- Takes controls before unrecoverable margin loss

**Evidence:**

- Safe branch outcome
- Time-to-intervention
- Safety margin preserved

**Scenario Examples:**

- Overshoot + tightening turn
- Departure stall
- Botched go-around

---

#### AC-3: Execute Positive Exchange of Controls Correctly

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Uses standard verbal transfer (three-step)
- Confirms transfer
- Avoids ambiguous handoff

**Evidence:**

- Correct sequence used under pressure
- No delayed takeover due to poor wording

**Scenario Examples:**

- Takeoff intervention
- Final approach takeover
- Inadvertent IMC handoff

---

### Domain 3 -- Risk Management and ADM (RM)

_Can the CFI see risk stacking before it becomes an event?_

#### RM-1: Identify Risk Stacking Before Flight

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Notices fatigue, illness, hunger, pressure, weather, unfamiliar airport, night, W&B

**Evidence:**

- Flagged risks in pre-brief
- Modified plan or lesson
- Chose safer mission scope

**Scenario Examples:**

- First night landing after long XC
- Student with cold and poor recent sleep
- High-density-altitude checkout

---

#### RM-2: Reassess Risk Dynamically

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Changes plan when conditions change
- Recommends discontinue or go-around
- Stops lesson when learning has degraded

**Evidence:**

- Dynamic risk detection
- Changed recommendation as cues evolved

**Scenario Examples:**

- Weather trending down
- Student saturation mid-flight
- Unstable approach after earlier errors

---

#### RM-3: Teach Ethical Passenger/Student Responsibility

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Resists external pressure
- Explains why the safer call matters
- Models conservative judgment

**Evidence:**

- Selected safer option under pressure
- Post-event rationale quality

**Scenario Examples:**

- Owner wants flight completed
- Student pushes to continue
- Schedule pressure before dark

---

### Domain 5 -- Airspace, Deviations, and Operational Discipline (OD)

_Can the CFI prevent avoidable operational errors?_

#### OD-1: Prevent Taxi and Runway Deviations

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Teaches heads-up taxi scan
- Confirms hold short / crossing instructions
- Uses airport diagram effectively
- Uses appropriate lighting and sterile cockpit

**Evidence:**

- Correct clearance action
- Correct runway crossing behavior
- Intervention before incursion

**Scenario Examples:**

- Complex airport at night
- Student reads back incorrectly
- Distraction during taxi

---

#### OD-2: Prevent Airborne Deviations

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Monitors altitude, route, and airspace margins
- Catches disorientation early
- Uses ATC/resources appropriately

**Evidence:**

- Airspace violation avoided
- Earlier correction path selected

**Scenario Examples:**

- Student lost on XC
- Class B edge drift
- Wrong descent expectation

---

#### OD-3: Teach Intercept/Special-Use-Airspace Response

**Observable Behaviors:**

- Identifies restriction risk
- Teaches route planning around it
- Knows immediate response logic

**Evidence:**

- Selected correct planning response
- Selected correct in-flight response

**Scenario Examples:**

- Pop-up TFR
- Route near special-use airspace
- Intercept recognition sequence

---

**Evidence:**

## Assessment Dimensions (Per Competency)

Each competency is scored across four dimensions:

1. **Recognition** -- Did they notice the problem?
2. **Judgment** -- Did they choose the right level of response?
3. **Execution** -- Did they actually do the right thing?
4. **Debrief** -- Can they explain and teach it afterward?

A person can:

- Notice the problem but intervene badly
- Intervene safely but debrief poorly
- Answer knowledge probes but miss it live

That richness is the whole point.

## Failure Taxonomy

What kinds of mistakes exist:

- **Early detection failure** -- Didn't notice the cue
- **Late intervention** -- Noticed but waited too long
- **Wrong intervention** -- Applied incorrect response
- **Over-intervention** -- Took controls unnecessarily
- **Shallow debrief** -- Addressed symptom not cause
- **Knowledge gap** -- Didn't know the relevant rule/procedure
