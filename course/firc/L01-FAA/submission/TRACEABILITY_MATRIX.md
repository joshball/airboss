## Detailed Topic Coverage

### Core Topic A.1: Navigating in the 21st Century

**FAA Requirement:** GPS, automation, TAA (including regulatory definition), ADS-B, NextGen. Emphasizes hazards like heads-down time, automation fixation, dependency, and staying proficient in manual control.

**Covered In:** Module 1

**Competencies:**

- AV-1: Teach automation without dependency
- AV-2: Recognize mode confusion and automation surprises
- AV-3: Teach GPS/TAA limitations

**Scenario Examples:**

- GPS database out of date
- Autopilot mode confusion
- RAIM / integrity issue
- Glass cockpit fixation in busy pattern
- ADS-B/weather delay misunderstanding
- TAA transition briefing

**Assessment/Evidence:**

- Decision logs showing appropriate automation coaching
- Scenario scores on mode confusion resolution
- Correct response to GPS failure/degradation branches
- Replay improvement delta

### Core Topic A.3: TSA Responsibilities

**FAA Requirement:** Citizenship documentation, recordkeeping, foreign student processing, security awareness training, current TSA requirements (49 CFR Part 1552).

**Key Operational Duties (from regulation):**

- Cannot provide flight training until citizenship/eligibility established
- Must examine government-issued documentation for U.S. citizens/nationals
- Must use FTSP Portal for non-U.S. candidates
- Must terminate training if TSA issues Determination of Ineligibility
- Must designate Security Coordinator and keep current
- Must retain records for at least 5 years

**Covered In:** Module 4, with spaced reinforcement in Modules 5 and 6

**Delivery Model:**

- 15-20 min core instruction
- 10 min first scenario (walk-in prospect)
- 10 min second scenario (transfer student variation)
- 5 min spaced recall
- 5 min recap/evidence check
- Total: ~45 identifiable minutes

**Competencies:**

- RC-1: Determine whether training may legally begin
- RC-2: Maintain required records and process steps

**Scenario Examples:**

- Beat 1: Non-U.S. prospective student walks in (Can you start today? What do you verify? Where do you confirm? What records?)
- Beat 2: Vary one fact (U.S. citizen without documents, foreign military, "other school already checked me")
- Beat 3: Escalation (eligibility changes, TSA notification, incomplete records, Security Coordinator changed)

**Assessment/Evidence:**

- Eligibility decisions (correct "do not start" branch selection)
- Verification path identification
- Record-keeping decisions
- Improvement on later variants (spaced repetition proof)

**Design Rule:** Reward knowing when you don't know, knowing where to verify, refusing to start without eligibility, documenting properly. Do NOT reward remembering weird exceptions.

## Time Aggregation Note

Time is accumulated across multiple instructional interactions within and across modules. A single scenario may contribute to multiple FAA core topic areas simultaneously.

Example: The base-to-final tick engine scenario contributes to:

- LOC (A.11)
- Teaching effectiveness (A.4)
- Safety culture (A.4)
- Pilot deviations (A.6)
- ACS (A.12)
- Ethics (A.10, if pressure element added)

This overlap model means the course achieves >= 45 minutes per core topic while maintaining a cohesive, scenario-driven learning experience rather than isolated topic blocks.

## Coverage Assurance Statement

The system ensures that all learners receive sufficient instructional exposure in each FAA-required core topic area through structured scenario sequencing and reinforcement. Completion logic verifies that every core topic has accumulated the minimum required instructional time before a graduation certificate is issued.
