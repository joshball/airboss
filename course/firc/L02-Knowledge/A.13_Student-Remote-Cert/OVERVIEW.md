## FAA Description (verbatim from AC 61-83K Appendix A)

### A.13 Main Section

Flight instructors need to understand that significant changes in the process of applying for and issuing student pilot certificates took effect on April 1, 2016, in conjunction with a new rule adopted by the FAA. Since April 1, 2016, Aviation Medical Examiners (AME) are no longer able to issue student pilot certificates. The FAA has released a final rule that requires student pilots to apply for a plastic student pilot certificate from a FSDO, Designated Pilot Examiner (DPE), Airman Certification Representative (ACR), or a flight instructor. Under the authority of the FAA, the 14 CFR part 107 rules were enacted on August 29, 2016. Now, an aviation safety inspector (ASI), DPE, ACR, or a flight instructor accepts remote pilot applications. AC 61-65, Certification: Pilots and Flight and Ground Instructors, reflects these changes. This lesson provides flight instructors training for their review and acceptance of an application for a Student Pilot Certificate or an application for a Remote Pilot Certificate. This lesson should provide training for the submission of both the Integrated Airman Certification and Rating Application (IACRA) and the paper applications. This lesson should also provide training to flight instructors regarding their responsibility to evaluate the applicant's eligibility for meeting FAA AELS. FIRC providers should develop a comprehensive lesson to facilitate the flight instructor's full understanding and implementation of their new responsibilities regarding this topic.

- You can find more information concerning part 107 pilot certification at https://www.faa.gov/uas/commercial_operators/become_a_drone_pilot.
- You can find more information concerning student pilot certification at https://www.faa.gov/pilots/become/student_cert/.

## What the FAA Wants Taught

The AC treats this as a practical compliance-and-process topic. Instructors need to understand their responsibilities when accepting student pilot or remote pilot certificate applications.

The non-negotiable teaching points are:

- student pilot certificate issuance changed in 2016, and AMEs no longer issue those certificates;
- instructors need training on reviewing and accepting student pilot and remote pilot certificate applications;
- training should cover both `IACRA` and paper-application workflows;
- instructors must verify applicant identity and eligibility where the rules assign that responsibility;
- instructors must evaluate the applicant's FAA AELS eligibility;
- providers should build a comprehensive lesson so instructors fully understand and can carry out these responsibilities.

This is an area where process details can change, so the durable lesson is correct responsibility, verification discipline, and current-source use.

## What This Topic Is Really About

This topic is about treating application acceptance as an instructor responsibility with compliance and safety consequences, not as a clerical formality.

The FAA wants CFIs to understand:

- when they are authorized to accept an application;
- what they must verify before doing so;
- and when a problem with eligibility, identity, English-language ability, or paperwork means the process must stop or be referred.

## Teaching Emphasis for CFIs

CFIs should be able to explain and perform:

- the current student pilot application path and the instructor's role in it;
- the instructor responsibilities tied to accepting eligible remote pilot certificate applications;
- identity and eligibility verification;
- accurate use of `IACRA` and awareness of paper-application handling when applicable;
- FAA AELS screening as part of the acceptance and training process;
- when to pause, correct, or refer an application instead of forcing it through.

This topic is strongest when it feels operational:

- not "here are some forms";
- but "here is the exact responsibility the instructor is assuming by accepting the application."
