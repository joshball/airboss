# A.3 -- TSA Requirements

FAA Core Topic: Transportation Security Administration (TSA): What Flight Instructors Need to Know to Stay Out of Trouble

**Internal name:** TSA Requirements
**AC 61-83K reference:** Appendix A, Section A.3 (no sub-sections)
**Minimum time:** 45 minutes
**Our module:** Module 4 -- Airspace, Security, and Compliance

---

## FAA Description (verbatim from AC 61-83K Appendix A)

### A.3 Main Section

The TSA now has a role in flight training. Consequently, flight instructors should know which airman certificates the TSA is interested in. Instructors should also be aware of the requirements for citizenship documentation, recordkeeping, foreign student processing, flight instructor and flight school security awareness training, and more. FIRC providers should deliver up-to-date training designed to assist and ensure that the flight instructor attendee is fully aware of the latest TSA requirements. The FIRC represents an ideal venue to update the instructor on the TSA's role and requirements in flight training. Not following the TSA guidelines properly can have serious consequences both for the student and for the flight instructor.

---

## What the FAA Wants Taught

The AC frames this as a direct instructor-compliance topic. TSA is part of the flight-training environment, and CFIs need enough current knowledge to avoid putting themselves or their students in trouble.

The non-negotiable teaching points are:

- TSA has a role in flight training;
- instructors should know which certificates and training situations draw TSA attention;
- citizenship documentation and recordkeeping matter;
- foreign student processing matters;
- flight instructor and flight school security awareness training matters;
- this topic must be kept current because TSA requirements can materially affect the legality of training activity.

## What This Topic Is Really About

This is not mainly a paperwork topic. It is a responsibility topic.

The FAA wants CFIs to understand that security-related compliance errors can begin before the first lesson:

- training the wrong applicant;
- accepting incomplete or improper documentation;
- mishandling citizenship verification;
- misunderstanding foreign-student processing;
- or failing to maintain required TSA-related records and training.

## Teaching Emphasis for CFIs

CFIs should be able to explain and apply:

- when TSA-related checks are required;
- what records the instructor or school must verify and retain;
- how foreign-student processing changes the workflow;
- why "another school already checked me" is not an adequate substitute for current instructor responsibility;
- why this topic requires current-source verification rather than memory.

The lesson should also reinforce the consequence side:

- TSA mistakes are not minor administrative trivia;
- they can create serious consequences for both the student and the instructor.
