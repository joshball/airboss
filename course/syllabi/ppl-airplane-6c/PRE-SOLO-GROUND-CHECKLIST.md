# Pre-Solo Ground School Checklist

This checklist defines the ground knowledge a student pilot should demonstrate before solo in the local training aircraft and local operating environment. It supports, but does not replace, the instructor's required pre-solo knowledge test, flight proficiency judgment, and endorsements.

## Student Readiness

The student can explain:

- Student pilot certificate requirements.
- Medical or BasicMed status as applicable.
- Required documents to carry.
- Solo limitations and endorsements.
- Prohibition on carrying passengers.
- Limitations on compensation/hire.
- Requirement to comply with instructor limitations.
- Personal minimums and school solo minimums.

Evidence:

- Student documents checked.
- Solo limitations oral complete.
- Endorsement path understood.

## Local Airport Operations

The student can:

- Draw or brief the airport layout.
- Identify runways, taxiways, hotspots, hold-short lines, movement/non-movement areas.
- Explain runway numbers, traffic pattern altitude, pattern direction, and noise abatement.
- Interpret local signs, markings, lighting, wind indicators, segmented circle, and visual glide indicators.
- Describe normal taxi route from parking to runway and back.
- Explain runway incursion avoidance.

Evidence:

- Airport diagram brief complete.
- Taxi/hotspot oral complete.

## Communications

The student can make and understand calls for:

- ATIS/AWOS/ASOS.
- Ground/tower if towered.
- CTAF if non-towered.
- Taxi.
- Runup complete/ready for departure.
- Pattern legs.
- Go-around.
- Full-stop landing.
- Abnormal or emergency situation.

The student can explain:

- Readback requirements.
- Hold-short instructions.
- Light-gun signals.
- Lost communications plan.
- Sterile cockpit in the pattern.

Evidence:

- Radio role-play complete.
- Light-gun signals reviewed.

## Local Airspace

The student can:

- Identify the local airport's airspace class.
- State local weather minimums and school/instructor solo minimums.
- Identify nearby controlled airspace, shelves, special-use airspace, TFR risk areas, terrain, obstacles, and neighboring airports.
- Explain where solo practice is authorized.
- Explain routes to/from the practice area.
- Explain altitude limits and geographic boundaries.

Evidence:

- Sectional/TAC local-area briefing complete.
- Practice-area boundaries memorized and charted.

## Weather

The student can:

- Obtain and interpret METAR, TAF, winds aloft, radar/satellite summary, NOTAM/TFR status, and relevant advisories.
- Identify no-go conditions for solo.
- Explain wind, gust, crosswind component, ceiling, visibility, precipitation, convective activity, turbulence, wind shear, density altitude, and temperature limits.
- Compare conditions against legal minimums, school minimums, instructor minimums, and personal minimums.

Evidence:

- Solo weather brief exercise complete.
- Crosswind component calculation complete.

## Aircraft Systems and Limitations

The student can explain the training aircraft:

- Engine and propeller.
- Fuel system and fuel quantity verification.
- Oil system and limits.
- Electrical system.
- Ignition/start system.
- Pitot-static system.
- Vacuum/pressure or attitude-source system if applicable.
- Flight instruments.
- Flaps/trim.
- Brakes/steering.
- Doors/windows/canopy.
- Avionics needed for solo.

The student can state:

- V-speeds needed for solo operations.
- Fuel type/capacity/usable fuel.
- Oil limits.
- Weight limits.
- Normal, caution, and redline instrument ranges.
- Crosswind limitation or demonstrated crosswind component.

Evidence:

- Aircraft systems oral complete.
- POH/AFM tabbed or accessible.

## Preflight and Airworthiness

The student can:

- Perform a complete preflight inspection.
- Explain required aircraft documents.
- Explain annual, 100-hour if applicable, ELT, transponder, AD, and squawk status at a basic level.
- Identify whether obvious defects ground the airplane.
- Explain required day VFR equipment.
- Explain what to do with an inoperative item.
- Check fuel quantity, quality, caps, vents, oil, tires, brakes, flight controls, pitot/static openings, lights as applicable.

Evidence:

- Aircraft document/maintenance status walkthrough complete.
- Inoperative equipment scenario complete.

## Performance and W&B

The student can:

- Calculate or verify weight and balance for solo.
- Determine takeoff and landing distance for current conditions.
- Explain density altitude.
- Explain runway length, wind, slope, surface, obstacle, and temperature effects.
- Apply instructor/school performance margins.

Evidence:

- Solo W&B complete.
- Solo takeoff/landing performance calculation complete.

## Normal Procedures

The student can brief:

- Preflight.
- Engine start.
- Taxi.
- Runup.
- Before takeoff.
- Normal takeoff.
- Climbout.
- Pattern entry/departure.
- Normal landing.
- Go-around.
- After landing.
- Shutdown/securing.

Evidence:

- Checklist flow oral complete.
- Go-around decision triggers reviewed.

## Emergency and Abnormal Procedures

The student can talk through:

- Engine failure on takeoff roll.
- Engine failure after liftoff with runway remaining.
- Engine failure after takeoff without runway remaining.
- Engine failure in the pattern.
- Go-around from unstable approach.
- Radio failure.
- Door/window opening.
- Electrical failure.
- High/low engine indications.
- Brake failure or steering issue.
- Fire during start, taxi, or flight.
- Loss of situational awareness.
- Traffic conflict.

Evidence:

- Emergency chair-flying complete.
- Memory items vs checklist items understood.

## Human Factors and ADM

The student can explain:

- IMSAFE.
- External pressures.
- Hazardous attitudes.
- Task saturation.
- Fatigue, hydration, food, medication, alcohol.
- Personal minimums.
- Decision to discontinue solo.
- How to call the instructor or ATC for help.

Evidence:

- Personal solo minimums written.
- "Cancel/delay/return" scenario complete.

## Required Pre-Solo Knowledge Test Topics

The instructor-administered pre-solo knowledge test should include:

- Applicable sections of Parts 61 and 91.
- Airspace rules/procedures for the solo airport.
- Flight characteristics and operational limitations of the make/model aircraft.
- Local airport procedures.
- Weather minimums and solo limitations.
- Emergency procedures.

Review:

- All incorrect answers reviewed.
- Instructor documented satisfactory correction.

## Final Pre-Solo Ground Gate

Before solo, confirm:

- Student documents complete.
- Pre-solo knowledge test complete and reviewed.
- Required endorsements complete.
- Weather within all limits.
- Aircraft legal and suitable.
- Student can explain the plan.
- Instructor has no unresolved ground-knowledge concerns.
