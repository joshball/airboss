# PPL Written Test Prep Plan

This plan prepares a private pilot airplane applicant for the FAA Private Pilot Airplane knowledge test. It assumes the learner has already started a ground school course and needs a structured final push.

The goal is not just to pass. The goal is to convert written-test knowledge into practical-test readiness.

## Target Standard

Before the instructor endorses or recommends the learner, the learner should show:

- Consistent practice test scores of 85% or higher.
- No repeated misses in weather, airspace, performance, W&B, regulations, or navigation.
- Ability to explain why wrong answers are wrong.
- Ability to find the supporting source in the ACS, FAA handbooks, regulations, AIM, POH/AFM, charts, or testing supplement.
- A remediation plan for weak ACS codes.

The FAA passing score is lower than this, but private pilot training should not aim at the floor.

## Required Materials

Use:

- Primary ground school course.
- FAA-S-ACS-6C.
- PHAK.
- AFH.
- Aviation Weather Handbook.
- AIM/PCG.
- FAA-CT-8080-2H Airman Knowledge Testing Supplement.
- Current VFR chart and Chart Supplement.
- Training aircraft POH/AFM.
- Practice test system such as Sporty's, King, Gleim, ASA Prepware, Pilot Institute, Gold Seal, or equivalent.

## Two-Week Intensive Plan

This assumes the learner has already completed most ground-school lessons.

### Day 1 -- Diagnostic

Tasks:

- Take one full practice test under test-like conditions.
- Do not pause, search, or study mid-test.
- Record every missed question by topic and ACS code if available.

Output:

- Weak-area list.
- Initial score.
- Top five weak topics.

### Day 2 -- Regulations and Privileges

Study:

- Certificates, documents, medical, BasicMed awareness, student limitations, private pilot privileges, currency, endorsements, required aircraft documents.
- Part 61 and Part 91 basics.

Practice:

- 40-60 regulation questions.
- Oral drill: "May this pilot make this flight?"

Output:

- One-page privileges/limitations summary.

### Day 3 -- Aircraft Systems and Instruments

Study:

- Engine, propeller, fuel, oil, electrical, pitot-static, vacuum/pressure, flight instruments, compass errors, inoperative equipment.
- POH/AFM systems and limitations.

Practice:

- 40-60 systems/instruments questions.
- Aircraft-specific systems oral.

Output:

- Systems failure cue/action table.

### Day 4 -- Aerodynamics, Stalls, and Maneuvers

Study:

- Four forces, angle of attack, stalls, load factor, stability, torque effects, coordination, maneuvering speed, spins awareness.

Practice:

- 40-60 aerodynamics questions.
- Explain accelerated stall and spin awareness without rote language.

Output:

- Load-factor/stall-speed explanation.

### Day 5 -- Weight, Balance, and Performance

Study:

- W&B calculations, CG envelope, performance charts, density altitude, fuel planning, runway/obstacle margins.

Practice:

- 60-80 calculation questions using the testing supplement.
- Do at least one real POH/AFM performance scenario.

Output:

- Three W&B/performance scenarios with decisions.

### Day 6 -- Airports, Airspace, and Communications

Study:

- Signs, markings, lighting, runway operations, traffic patterns, CTAF, ATC, airspace classes, VFR weather minimums, special use airspace, TFR awareness.

Practice:

- 60-80 chart/airspace/communications questions.
- Trace one local cross-country route through every airspace segment.

Output:

- Route airspace table.

### Day 7 -- Weather Theory

Study:

- Air masses, fronts, pressure systems, clouds, fog, thunderstorms, turbulence, wind shear, icing awareness, stability, lapse rates.

Practice:

- 50-70 weather theory questions.

Output:

- Weather hazards and no-go triggers list.

### Day 8 -- Weather Products

Study:

- METAR, TAF, winds aloft, PIREPs, AIRMETs, SIGMETs, Convective SIGMETs, prog charts, radar, satellite, graphical forecasts, briefings.

Practice:

- 60-80 weather product questions.
- Conduct one real weather briefing.

Output:

- Weather brief with go/no-go decision.

### Day 9 -- Navigation and Flight Planning

Study:

- Pilotage, dead reckoning, variation, deviation, wind correction, ground speed, fuel, checkpoints, diversions, lost procedures, VOR/GPS basics.

Practice:

- 60-80 navigation questions using the testing supplement.
- Build a short nav log.

Output:

- Nav log and diversion calculation.

### Day 10 -- ADM, Human Factors, Emergencies, Night

Study:

- IMSAFE, PAVE, 5P, hazardous attitudes, hypoxia, hyperventilation, spatial disorientation, fatigue, alcohol/medications, CO poisoning, emergency operations, night illusions.

Practice:

- 40-60 ADM/aeromedical/emergency questions.

Output:

- Personal minimums draft.

### Day 11 -- Practice Test 2

Tasks:

- Full timed practice test.
- Review every missed question.
- Tag misses as knowledge, reading, calculation, or rushing.

Output:

- Updated weak-area list.
- Remediation plan for any topic below 80%.

### Day 12 -- Targeted Remediation

Tasks:

- Re-study only weak areas.
- Use FAA sources, not just question explanations.
- Teach each weak topic aloud for one minute.

Output:

- Mistake log with source references.

### Day 13 -- Practice Test 3 and Endorsement Review

Tasks:

- Full timed practice test.
- If score is 85%+ and weak areas are resolved, meet instructor for endorsement review.
- If not, repeat targeted remediation.

Output:

- Instructor readiness decision.

### Day 14 -- Light Review and Test Logistics

Tasks:

- Review formulas, chart symbols, weather codes, airspace minimums, and common traps.
- Confirm FTN, ID, testing appointment, allowed materials, calculator/E6B rules, and endorsement.
- Sleep.

Output:

- Test-day checklist.

## Four-Week Plan

Use this if the learner is still learning, not just reviewing.

| Week | Focus | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Regulations, certificates, aircraft systems, aerodynamics | Privileges oral, systems oral, aerodynamics quiz |
| 2 | W&B, performance, airports, airspace, communications | Performance packet, route airspace analysis |
| 3 | Weather theory/products, navigation, cross-country planning | Weather brief, nav log, XC mini-packet |
| 4 | ADM, emergencies, practice tests, weak-code remediation | 2-3 practice tests, endorsement review |

Weekly rule:

- One full review session with the instructor or ground mentor.
- One source lookup assignment.
- One oral explanation assignment.
- One practice test or topic test.

## Mistake Log Template

Use this table for every missed practice question.

| Date | Topic | Miss type | Why I missed it | Correct concept | Source | Oral explanation ready? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| | | Knowledge / reading / calculation / rushing | | | | Yes / No |

Miss types:

- Knowledge: I did not know it.
- Reading: I knew it but misread the question/figure.
- Calculation: I used the wrong formula, chart, unit, or interpolation.
- Rushing: I moved too fast or failed to check reasonableness.

## High-Yield Weak Areas

Watch these closely:

- VFR weather minimums in Class G at different altitudes/times.
- Airspace boundaries on sectional charts.
- Magnetic course vs heading vs wind correction vs variation/deviation.
- Density altitude and runway performance.
- Weight and balance with moment/index units.
- METAR/TAF time groups and weather codes.
- Required equipment and inoperative equipment logic.
- Private pilot compensation/pro-rata share rules.
- Currency vs proficiency.
- Fuel reserve requirements.
- Hypoxia, hyperventilation, spatial disorientation, alcohol/medication rules.

## Instructor Endorsement Review

Before endorsement, the instructor should ask:

- Which topics are still below 85%?
- Can the learner explain missed questions without memorizing answer letters?
- Has the learner used the actual testing supplement figures?
- Has the learner connected written-test topics to the training aircraft?
- Does the learner know test logistics?
- Does the learner understand that knowledge-test deficiencies must be reviewed before the practical test?

## After the Test

Do not throw away the report.

After the test:

- Save the Airman Knowledge Test Report.
- List deficient ACS codes.
- Review each code with a CFI.
- Add deficiencies to the checkride oral prep plan.
- Bring proof of deficiency review/endorsement as required for practical-test preparation.

The score matters less than the remediation. A high score with ignored weak areas is still poor preparation.
