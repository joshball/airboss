# Ground School Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to evaluate an online course, local class, CFI-run ground program, school syllabus, or self-study plan for private pilot airplane training.

Score each category from 0 to 4.

| Score | Meaning                   |
| ----- | ------------------------- |
| 0     | Missing or unusable       |
| 1     | Present but weak          |
| 2     | Adequate                  |
| 3     | Strong                    |
| 4     | Excellent / model quality |

- 50-60: Excellent.

## 1. FAA and Regulatory Alignment

Score 0:

- Does not clearly cover 14 CFR 61.105 or private pilot privileges/limitations.

Score 2:

- Covers required topics but does not show a clear source map.

Score 4:

- Explicitly maps lessons to 61.105, Part 91, endorsements, and practical operating rules.
- Teaches source lookup and current-reference habits.

Look for:

- Part 61.
- Part 91.
- NTSB 830.
- AC 61-65 awareness.
- Current FAA handbooks.

## 2. ACS Integration

Score 0:

- Treats training as a knowledge-test-only exercise.

Score 2:

- Mentions ACS but does not use it as a teaching framework.

Score 4:

- Uses ACS knowledge, risk management, and skill elements throughout.
- Helps remediate weak ACS codes.
- Connects written, oral, and flight tasks.

Look for:

- ACS-code tracking.
- Knowledge/risk/skill debriefs.
- Practical-test scenario alignment.

## 3. Conceptual Teaching Depth

Score 0:

- Mostly memorization.

Score 2:

- Explains concepts but not consistently.

Score 4:

- Builds durable understanding with examples, diagrams, scenarios, and source references.
- Explains why wrong answers are tempting.

Look for:

- Aerodynamics beyond slogans.
- Weather beyond decoding.
- Performance beyond chart arithmetic.
- Systems tied to failures.

## 4. Written-Test Preparation

Score 0:

- No realistic practice testing.

Score 2:

- Practice quizzes exist but weak review/remediation.

Score 4:

- Timed practice tests, topic drills, explanations, missed-area tracking, endorsement path, and testing supplement familiarity.

Look for:

- PAR-specific practice.
- FAA-style figures.
- Mistake log or analytics.
- Endorsement process.

## 5. Oral and Checkride Preparation

Score 0:

- No oral prep.

Score 2:

- Checkride tips but little structured practice.

Score 4:

- Scenario-based oral prep across ACS areas.
- Aircraft documents, maintenance records, weather, XC plan, systems, privileges, emergencies.
- Mock oral guidance.

Look for:

- "Show me the source" questions.
- Checkride binder/packet.
- Knowledge-test deficiency review.

## 6. Flight Lesson Integration

Score 0:

- Ground school exists completely apart from flight training.

Score 2:

- Some maneuver videos or flight tie-ins.

Score 4:

- Ground lessons are sequenced before related flight lessons.
- Maneuver, risk, and briefing/debrief content is built in.
- CFI can assign lessons and track readiness.

Look for:

- Pre-solo module.
- Cross-country readiness.
- Maneuver briefings.
- Postflight debrief prompts.

## 7. Aircraft-Specific Work

Score 0:

- Generic airplane facts only.

Score 2:

- Mentions POH/AFM but does not require aircraft-specific assignments.

Score 4:

- Requires POH/AFM systems, limitations, W&B, performance, emergency procedures, aircraft documents, and maintenance status work.

Look for:

- POH exercises.
- Aircraft records review.
- Real performance and W&B scenarios.

## 8. Weather Decision-Making

Score 0:

- Only decodes METAR/TAF.

Score 2:

- Covers weather theory/products but few decisions.

Score 4:

- Teaches decode, understand, and triage.
- Uses real or realistic briefing packs.
- Requires go/no-go decisions and personal minimums.

Look for:

- Convective, wind, visibility, turbulence, icing awareness.
- PIREPs, AIRMETs/SIGMETs, radar/satellite, prog charts.
- Weather trend reasoning.

## 9. Navigation and Cross-Country Planning

Score 0:

- Little beyond basic chart symbols.

Score 2:

- Teaches nav calculations but weak operational planning.

Score 4:

- Requires complete XC package: route, airspace, weather, NOTAMs, fuel, W&B, performance, alternates, communications, diversions.

Look for:

- Pilotage/dead reckoning.
- EFB discipline.
- Lost procedures.
- Flight following.

## 10. ADM and Risk Management

Score 0:

- ADM appears as memorized acronyms only.

Score 2:

- Covers ADM concepts but little application.

Score 4:

- Risk management is embedded in every lesson.
- Uses PAVE/5P/IMSAFE/DECIDE as tools, not slogans.
- Requires personal minimums and external-pressure scenarios.

Look for:

- Cancel/delay/reroute decisions.
- Passenger pressure.
- Marginal weather.
- Fatigue/medication scenarios.

## 11. Instructor Tools and Progress Tracking

Score 0:

- No instructor visibility.

Score 2:

- Completion certificate only.

Score 4:

- CFI dashboard, assignable lessons, analytics, weak-topic reports, ACS-code tracking, and completion documentation.

Look for:

- Exportable progress.
- Quiz analytics.
- Endorsement documentation.
- Stage-gate support.

## 12. Practice and Feedback Quality

Score 0:

- Passive content only.

Score 2:

- Quizzes exist but feedback is shallow.

Score 4:

- Frequent checks, explanations, spaced review, scenario feedback, and remediation paths.

Look for:

- Why wrong answers are wrong.
- Mixed review.
- Adaptive study.
- Oral prompts.

## 13. Accessibility and Learner Fit

Score 0:

- Hard to use or incompatible with learner needs.

Score 2:

- Usable but limited.

Score 4:

- Works across devices, supports offline/mobile use where needed, has readable materials, captions/transcripts/audio as useful, and matches the learner's style.

Look for:

- Mobile/tablet/TV support.
- Download/offline option.
- Audio lessons.
- Captions/transcripts.
- Clear navigation.

## 14. Currency and Update Policy

Score 0:

- Unknown or stale.

Score 2:

- Updated occasionally but policy unclear.

Score 4:

- Clearly updated for current ACS, FAA references, testing changes, and modern avionics/EFB/weather sources.

Look for:

- Lifetime updates or clear renewal.
- Edition/date notes.
- Current ACS language.

## 15. Cost and Value

Score 0:

- Expensive and incomplete.

Score 2:

- Fair price but needs predictable supplements.

Score 4:

- Price, access duration, support, endorsement, and content depth are well matched.

Look for:

- Lifetime vs annual vs monthly access.
- Refund/pass guarantee terms.
- Support included.
- Hidden material costs.

## Minimum Acceptable Course

A course is acceptable only if it:

- Covers all 61.105 knowledge areas.
- Prepares for the FAA knowledge test.
- Uses current FAA references.
- Sends the learner to the POH/AFM.
- Covers weather, airspace, W&B, performance, navigation, regulations, systems, ADM, and emergencies.
- Provides an endorsement path or clear instructor review path.

## Excellent Course

An excellent course:

- Teaches the written, oral, and flight as one integrated system.
- Uses ACS knowledge/risk/skill framing.
- Requires real planning artifacts.
- Gives instructors progress visibility.
- Builds source lookup habits.
- Includes realistic weather and cross-country decisions.
- Makes the learner more conservative and more capable, not merely test-ready.
