# PPL Ground School Reference Map

This map connects a typical private pilot airplane ground school to the controlling FAA/regulatory sources and supporting study materials. Use it as an instructor planning checklist, student reading guide, or audit tool after using a commercial ground school.

The controlling structure is:

- 14 CFR 61.105 for required aeronautical knowledge.
- 14 CFR 61.107 for flight proficiency areas that ground lessons must support.
- 14 CFR 61.109 for airplane aeronautical experience planning.
- FAA-S-ACS-6C for practical-test knowledge, risk management, and skill tasks.
- Part 91 for day-to-day operating rules.

## Reading Sequence

For most learners, assign readings just before the topic is used in flight:

1. ACS intro and ACS Guide early, so the learner understands the target.
2. PHAK aerodynamics/systems before basic maneuvers and pre-solo.
3. AFH traffic pattern, takeoff, landing, slow flight, stalls, steep turns, and ground reference chapters before those lessons.
4. POH/AFM systems, limitations, W&B, performance, and emergency procedures before solo preparation.
5. AIM airport operations, communications, airspace, and weather services before cross-country planning.
6. AvWx weather theory/products before solo cross-country.
7. RMH throughout, with a formal personal-minimums assignment before solo cross-country.

## Instructor Notes

- Do not let the commercial ground school become the syllabus of record unless it is audited against 61.105 and ACS.
- Require source lookup, not just correct answers.
- Keep a running list of weak ACS codes from quizzes, practice tests, lessons, and debriefs.
- Tie ground lessons to flight lessons whenever possible: systems before preflight, airspace before leaving the practice area, performance before short-field work, weather before cross-country.
- Use the POH/AFM and aircraft records early. Many students can pass written-test questions but cannot prove their actual airplane is legal and suitable for today's flight.
