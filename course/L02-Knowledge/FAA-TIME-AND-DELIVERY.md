# FAA Time and Delivery Requirements

What the FAA actually requires for FIRC time, delivery, and content -- sourced directly from AC 61-83K. No paraphrasing, no interpretation without labeling it.

---

## The 16-Hour Requirement

**Source:** AC 61-83K SS 10.4

> "Curricula presented for FAA approval consideration should consist of no fewer than 16 hours of ground and/or flight instruction."

The FAA term is **"ground and/or flight instruction."** Not "seat time," not "engagement," not "active interaction." The AC does not define what qualifies as instruction beyond this phrase.

Key flexibility (SS 10.4):

- Hours do NOT need to be contiguous
- Do NOT need to be completed in a 2-day span
- Can be distributed over time (e.g., monthly portions)
- Provider may offer MORE than 16 hours

## The 12-Hour Content Rule (Internet FIRCs)

**Source:** AC 61-83K SS 12.1.1.3

> "FIRC training standards require audio/video, aural, and text lesson presentations to contain and present a cumulative period of no less than 720 minutes (12 hours) of the FIRC's 16-hour minimum course content."

This means for an online FIRC:

- **12 hours minimum** must be actual lesson content (audio, video, text presentations)
- **4 hours** can be testing, breaks, and other non-presentation activities
- The LMS must ensure "the attendee views and completes each lesson in its entirety without skipping any portion"

**Our interpretation:** Scenarios, debriefs, micro lessons, and reference reading all count as "lesson presentations." Questions/testing count toward the remaining 4 hours. This gives us roughly:

- 12 hours of scenarios + debriefs + micro lessons + reading
- 4 hours of knowledge checks + breaks + module transitions

## Per-Topic Minimums

**Source:** AC 61-83K SS 10.5.2, SS 10.5.3, Appendix A footnote

- **Core topics (A.1-A.13):** 45 minutes minimum each (SS 10.5.2)
- **Elective topics:** 30 minutes minimum each (SS 10.5.3), FAA may approve shorter with justification

## Elective Topics

**Source:** AC 61-83K SS 10.2.2

- Electives are **encouraged, not required** (Appendix B footnote 6)
- Provider "may choose and add as many elective topics as they wish"
- FAA suggests building a "library" of electives
- Electives require FAA approval before presenting (SS 10.2.4)
- Once approved, swapping electives requires only email notification (SS 14.1)
- Elective time counts toward the 16-hour total

## What "SS 12.1.1.3" Means

The notation "SS" stands for "section." AC 61-83K is organized with numbered paragraphs. SS 12.1.1.3 means paragraph 12, sub-paragraph 1, sub-sub-paragraph 1, item 3 -- specifically "Lesson Presentation Period Integrity" within the "Internet-Based FIRCs" section.

## Internet FIRC Specific Requirements

**Source:** AC 61-83K SS 12

- Must include a **Minimum Hour Assurance Plan Matrix** in the TCO (SS 12.1.1.1)
- LMS must ensure attendee views each lesson completely without skipping (SS 12.1.1.3)
- Identity verification at logon and during navigation (SS 12.1.3)
- Testing materials must be inaccessible until prerequisite lessons are completed (SS 12.1.4)
- Video/aural content is expected to be higher proportion than in-person FIRCs (SS 9.8.5)

## How Scenario-Based Delivery Fits

The AC does not specifically address scenario-based interactive delivery as a content type. It lists:

- "audio/video, aural, and text lesson presentations" (SS 12.1.1.3)
- Video presentations limited to 50% for in-person, but higher for internet (SS 9.8.5)
- "Interactive action" referenced in the context of timer-gated courses (existing FIRC competitors)

**Our position:** Interactive scenarios are a form of "lesson presentation" -- the learner is receiving instruction through guided experience. The scenario IS the lesson, not a supplement to it. The debrief IS the reinforcement. This is consistent with the AC's emphasis on SBT (scenario-based training) throughout:

> "Emphasize the concept of scenarios. The FIRC provider should develop an innovative overall course that compels and inspires its attendees." (SS 9.3)

> "The provider should think of each topic as a chapter out of a larger book, with that 'book' being the entire 16-hour program." (SS 9.3)

## What We Still Need to Clarify

- Does the FAA consider interactive scenario time as "lesson presentation" time (counting toward the 12-hour minimum)?
- How does the Minimum Hour Assurance Plan Matrix work for a non-linear, adaptive course?
- What identity verification methodology is acceptable for an internet FIRC?
- Can scenario replay time count toward topic time, or only the first attempt?

These questions will need answers before FAA submission. They may require direct communication with the General Aviation and Commercial Division.
