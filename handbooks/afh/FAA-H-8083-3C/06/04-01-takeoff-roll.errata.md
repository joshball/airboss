## Replacement text

Managing weather risks is important for all pilots, but becomes more significant as the
weight of an airplane decreases. Smaller, lighter-weight airplanes are more affected by
strong winds (especially direct crosswinds), turbulence, terrain influences, and other
hazardous conditions. Pilots should carefully consider any hazardous weather and effectively
use an appropriate set of personal minimums to mitigate flight risk. Some aircraft have a
maximum recommended wind velocity regardless of wind direction. While this is not a
limitation, it would be prudent to heed any factory recommendations.
