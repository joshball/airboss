## Replacement text

Pilots holding a higher-level certificate with the appropriate category and class ratings may
exercise the privileges of a lower-grade certificate as long as the pilot holds either a valid
medical certificate, a valid U.S. driver’s license, or complies with BasicMed, as appropriate
for the privileges the pilot is intending to exercise. However, when exercising sport pilot
privileges, if the pilot’s most recent medical certificate was denied, revoked, suspended, or
withdrawn, a U.S. driver’s license is not sufficient. The pilot would then need to reapply for
and be issued a valid FAA medical certificate to operate an aircraft.
