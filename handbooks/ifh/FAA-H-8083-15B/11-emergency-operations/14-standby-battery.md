# Standby Battery

Standby Battery
One technique for conserving the main battery charge is to
fly the aircraft to the airport of intended landing while using
the standby battery. A two-position battery master/ alternator
rocker switch is installed on most aircraft with EFDs, which
can be utilized to isolate the main battery from the electrical
system. By switching the MASTER side off, the battery is
taken offline and the standby battery comes online to power the
essential bus. However, the standby battery switch must be in
the ARM position for this to occur. [Figure 11-6] Utilization
of the standby battery first reserves the main battery for use
when approaching to land. With this technique, electrical
power may be available for the use of flaps, gear, lights, etc. Do
not rely on any power to be available after the standby battery
has exhausted itself. Once the charging system has failed,
flight with a powered electrical system is not guaranteed.
