# Floating Waypoints

Floating Waypoints
Floating waypoints, or reporting points, represent
airspace fixes at a point in space not directly associated
with a conventional airway. In many cases, they may be
established for such purposes as ATC metering fixes,
holding points, RNAV-direct routing, gateway waypoints,
STAR origination points leaving the en route structure,
and SID terminating points joining the en route structure.
In the top example of Figure 2-54, a low altitude en route
chart depicts three floating waypoints that have been
highlighted:  SCORR, FILUP, and CHOOT. Notice that
waypoints are named with five-letter identifiers that are
unique and pronounceable. Pilots must be careful of
similar waypoint names. Notice on the high altitude en
route chart excerpt in the bottom example, the similar
sounding and spelled floating waypoint named SCOOR,
rather than SCORR. This emphasizes the importance
of correctly entering waypoints into database-driven
navigation systems. One waypoint character incorrectly
entered into your navigation system could adversely affect
