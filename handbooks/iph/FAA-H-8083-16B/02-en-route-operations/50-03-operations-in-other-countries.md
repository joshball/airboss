# Operations in Other Countries

Operations in Other Countries
When flight crews transition from the U.S. NAS to another
country’s airspace, they should be aware of differences not
only in procedures but also airspace.  For example, when
flying into Canada as depicted in Figure 2-67, notice the
change from transition level (QNE) to transition altitude
(QNH) when flying north-bound into the Moncton flight
information region (FIR).
