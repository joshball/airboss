# Reviewing the Approach

Reviewing the Approach
Once the aircrew has determined which approach to
expect, review the approach chart thoroughly before
entering the terminal area. Aircrews should check fuel level
and make sure a prolonged hold or increased headwinds
have not cut into the aircraft’s fuel reserves because there is
always a chance the pilot has to make a missed approach or
go to an alternate. By completing landing checklists early,
aircrews can concentrate on the approach.
In setting up for the expected approach procedure when
using an RNAV, GPS, or FMS system, it is important to
understand how multiple approaches to the same runway
are coded in the database. When more than one RNAV
procedure is issued for the same runway, there must be a
way to differentiate between them within the equipment’s
database, as well as to select which procedure is to be
used. (Multiple procedures may exist to accommodate GPS
receivers and FMS, both with and without VNAV capability.)
Each procedure name incorporates a letter of the alphabet,
starting with Z and working backward through Y, X, W, and
so on. (Naming conventions for approaches are covered in
more depth in the next chapter). [Figure 3-15]
