## CFI Population

- **138,127 CFI certificates held** as of end of 2024 ([JasonBlair.net](https://jasonblair.net/?p=4334))
- This is certificates _held_, not active instructors. Many are airline pilots who got their CFI as a stepping stone.
- New CFI issuances in 2024 were the highest since 1990.
- CFI population skewing younger due to heavy influx of new instructors.
- ~20% of current CFIs projected to reach average life expectancy within 11 years -- significant older cohort.

**Unknown:** How many of the 138k actively maintain recent experience. No public data on FIRC vs. other renewal method usage, but FIRC is widely cited as the most common path.

**Conservative estimate:** ~50,000 CFIs actively renew via FIRC per 2-year cycle (~25,000/year across all providers).

## 2024 Rule Change

Effective December 1, 2024, [CFI certificates no longer expire](https://www.aopa.org/news-and-media/all-news/2024/october/08/long-awaited-rule-removes-cfi-expiration). Key changes:

- Certificates now have a "recent experience end date" (REED) instead of an expiration date
- CFIs still must establish recent experience every 24 months per 14 CFR SS 61.197(b)
- **3-month reinstatement window now only allows FIRC** (not other methods) -- potentially increases FIRC importance for lapsed instructors
- Two-phase rollout: Phase 1 on Dec 1, 2024; Phase 2 on March 1, 2027

Sources: [AOPA](https://www.aopa.org/news-and-media/all-news/2024/october/08/long-awaited-rule-removes-cfi-expiration), [Gleim](https://aviation.gleim.com/2024/12/17/gleim-clarifies-cfi-rule-change-with-faa/), [FAA Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/10/01/2024-22018/removal-of-expiration-date-on-a-flight-instructor-certificate-additional-qualification-requirements)

## Chief Instructor Requirement

Per AC 61-83K SS 11.7.1 and 14 CFR SS 141.35(d), the TCO must name a chief instructor who meets:

- Commercial or ATP certificate + current CFI with appropriate ratings
- 2,000 hours PIC
- 1,500 hours giving instruction (or 3 years + 1,000 hours)
- Pass knowledge and proficiency tests per SS 141.35

**Responsibilities:**

- Named in the TCO with airman certificate number
- Signs every graduation certificate (SS 10.11.2)
- Responsible for course integrity, test security, attendee completion (SS 10.5.11, SS 13.6)
- FAA must be notified 45 days before a change (SS 16)
- Provider cannot conduct FIRCs without one (SS 16)

**Time estimate (at 750 students/year):**

| Activity                                                     | Hours/year |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------- |
| Upfront course review (year 1 only)                          | ~100       |
| Monthly maintenance (~10 hrs/mo)                             | ~120       |
| Per-student review (~1.5 min pass, ~5 min fail, 90/10 split) | ~18        |
| **Year 1 total**                                             | **~238**   |
| **Year 2+ total**                                            | **~138**   |

**Compensation models:**

- Per-cert only ($2-5/cert) -- undervalues the risk and ongoing responsibility
- Retainer + per-cert ($500/mo + $5/cert) -- ~$10,000/year at moderate volume
- Partnership/equity -- most aligned if you find a true believer
